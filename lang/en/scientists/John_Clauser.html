👨‍🔬 John Clauser

1942–наст. время · quantum optics, quantum mechanics
American experimental physicist who was the first to test Bell's inequalities using entangled photons. His experiments refuted local hidden variables and confirmed the nonlocality of quantum mechanics. Winner of the Nobel Prize in Physics in 2022.

Biography

Born in Pasadena. He earned his PhD at Columbia University. He worked at the University of California, Berkeley, and the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. In 1972, together with Stuart Freedman, he performed the famous experiment that demonstrated the violation of Bell's inequalities.

Key discoveries

  • Experimental test of Bell's inequality — the first convincing proof of quantum nonlocality.
  • Development of the Clauser–Horne–Shimony–Holt (CHSH) inequality — an experimentally convenient form of Bell's inequality.
💡 In his youth, he built homemade rockets and even won a science fair with a project on a cloud chamber.
Quote: "I hoped to prove that Einstein was right, but nature ruled otherwise."
