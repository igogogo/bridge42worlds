👨‍🔬 Anton Zeilinger

1945–наст. время · quantum information, quantum optics
Austrian physicist, pioneer of quantum information and quantum optics. He designed and carried out key experiments on quantum teleportation, multi-particle entanglement, and Bell inequality tests with all local loopholes closed. Winner of the Nobel Prize in 2022.

Biography

Born in Ried (Austria). He earned his doctorate at the University of Vienna. Professor at the University of Innsbruck and the University of Vienna. President of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. He led the group that achieved the first quantum teleportation of an independent qubit and entanglement distribution between the Canary Islands.

Key discoveries

  • Quantum teleportation — transfer of a quantum state without transferring matter.
  • Entanglement swapping — creation of entanglement between particles that have never interacted.
  • Experiments with loophole closure — simultaneous closing of detection and communication loopholes in the Bell test.
💡 In one experiment, his team teleported photon states between the islands of La Palma and Tenerife over a distance of 143 km.
Quote: "Quantum mechanics teaches us that reality does not exist until we measure it."
