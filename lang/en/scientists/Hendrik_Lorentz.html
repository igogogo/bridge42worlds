👨‍🔬 Hendrik Lorentz

1853–1928 · Electrodynamics, Theoretical physics
Hendrik Lorentz was a Dutch physicist who developed classical electron theory and the Lorentz transformations, which became the mathematical foundation of special relativity. He also contributed to the explanation of the Zeeman effect, for which he received the Nobel Prize in 1902. Lorentz predicted length contraction and time dilation, which were later confirmed.

Biography

Lorentz graduated from Leiden University, where he later became a professor of theoretical physics. He also worked in Haarlem and was director of the Teylers Physics Laboratory.

Key discoveries

  • Lorentz transformations: mathematical formulas relating coordinates and time in different inertial frames, necessary to reconcile electrodynamics with the principle of relativity.
  • Electron theory: explanation of many optical and electromagnetic phenomena through the motion of electrons.
💡 Lorentz was so respected that after his death, national mourning was declared in the Netherlands, and telegraph stations fell silent for three minutes.
