👨‍🔬 Werner Heisenberg

1901–1976 · Quantum physics, Quantum mechanics, Particle physics
Werner Heisenberg is one of the founders of quantum mechanics. He formulated the uncertainty principle, which states that it is impossible to simultaneously measure both the momentum and position of a particle with absolute precision. This principle became fundamental to understanding the microscopic world and led to the development of quantum field theory. Heisenberg also contributed to the theory of nuclear forces and elementary particle physics. His work laid the foundations for modern quantum technologies, including quantum computers.

Biography

He studied at the University of Munich and the University of Göttingen under Arnold Sommerfeld and Max Born. He worked as a professor at the University of Leipzig and later as director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics in Berlin.

Key discoveries

  • Uncertainty principle: a fundamental limit to the precision with which position and momentum can be simultaneously measured.
  • Matrix mechanics: one of the first formulations of quantum mechanics, developed jointly with Born and Jordan.
  • Theory of ferromagnetism: explanation of magnetic properties of materials based on quantum mechanics.
💡 Heisenberg was a passionate pianist and even considered a career as a musician.
Quote: ""What we observe is not nature itself, but nature exposed to our method of questioning.""
