👨‍🔬 Werner Heisenberg

1901–1976 · Quantum mechanics, Nuclear physics
German theoretical physicist, one of the creators of quantum mechanics. He formulated the matrix version of quantum theory and the famous uncertainty principle, which states that it is impossible to simultaneously measure the exact position and momentum of a particle. He contributed to nuclear theory, quantum electrodynamics, and particle physics.

Biography

Born in Würzburg. Studied at the University of Munich, worked with Niels Bohr, Max Born, Pascual Jordan. Professor in Leipzig, director of the Max Planck Institute for Physics. Winner of the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1932 for the creation of quantum mechanics.

Key discoveries

  • Matrix mechanics (1925) — the first consistent mathematical formalism of quantum mechanics, describing physical quantities as matrices.
  • Uncertainty principle (1927) — established a fundamental limit to the precision of simultaneous measurement of certain pairs of variables, such as position and momentum.
  • Isospin — introduced the concept of isotopic spin to explain the symmetry of the proton and neutron in nuclear interactions.
  • Model of ferromagnetism — explained the nature of ferromagnetism through the exchange interaction of electrons.
💡 Heisenberg was a talented pianist and seriously agonized between a career as a musician and a physicist.
