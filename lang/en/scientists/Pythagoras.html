👨‍🔬 Pythagoras

ок. 570 – ок. 495 до н.э. · Mathematics, Philosophy, Music, Astronomy
Pythagoras was an ancient Greek philosopher and mathematician, founder of the Pythagorean school. He is credited with the first proof of the theorem that bears his name, although the relation was known earlier in Babylon. His teaching combined mathematics, music and mysticism; the Pythagoreans believed that numbers are the basis of all things and discovered the connection between harmonic intervals and simple numerical ratios.

Biography

Born on the island of Samos, travelled extensively through Egypt and Babylon, studying secret knowledge. Around 530 BC he founded a community in Croton (southern Italy), where he and his disciples conducted scientific and philosophical research. The school was closed after a revolt, and Pythagoras himself likely died in Metapontum.

Key discoveries

  • Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c² for a right-angled triangle – one of the most famous mathematical statements.
  • Doctrine of numerical ratios in music: the intervals of the octave, perfect fifth and perfect fourth correspond to ratios 2:1, 3:2 and 4:3.
  • Discovery of incommensurability (irrational numbers) exemplified by the diagonal of a square.
  • The concept of 'harmony of the spheres' – the idea that celestial bodies move according to mathematical laws and produce an unheard music.
💡 Pythagoras imposed strict rules on his followers, including a ban on eating beans; according to one legend, he died because he refused to flee through a bean field.
Quote: "All is number."
