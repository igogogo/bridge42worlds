👨‍🔬 Vera Rubin

1928–2016 · Astronomy, Astrophysics, Cosmology
An American astronomer whose observations of galaxy rotation provided compelling evidence for the existence of dark matter. She measured the rotation curves of spiral galaxies and found that stars at the periphery move as fast as those in the center, indicating the presence of invisible mass. Her work changed the understanding of the composition of the universe.

Biography

She studied at Vassar College and Cornell University, earning her doctorate from Georgetown University. She worked at the Carnegie Institution for Science in Washington, D.C.

Key discoveries

  • Galaxy rotation curves: showed that the rotation speed of galaxies does not decrease with distance as predicted by visible mass.
  • Dark matter: her observations provided direct evidence for the existence of dark matter.
  • Andromeda anomaly: discovered that the Andromeda galaxy rotates differently than expected.
💡 Rubin faced gender discrimination early in her career but became a pioneer for women in astronomy.
Quote: "We live in an era when we can find out what the universe is made of."
