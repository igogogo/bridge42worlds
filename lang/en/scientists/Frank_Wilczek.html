👨‍🔬 Frank Wilczek

1951- · quantum field theory, elementary particle physics, quantum computing
American theoretical physicist, Nobel laureate for the discovery of asymptotic freedom in quantum chromodynamics. Contributed to axion physics, anyons, and other ideas important for quantum computing.

Biography

Born in New York. Studied at the University of Chicago, PhD at Princeton under David Gross. Worked at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, then professor at MIT. Nobel laureate (2004) together with Gross and Politzer.

Key discoveries

  • Asymptotic freedom in QCD (1973)
  • prediction of anyons (particles with intermediate statistics
  • used in topological quantum computing)
  • theory of axion as a candidate for dark matter
  • concept of 'time crystals'.
💡 Wilczek enjoys cooking and once compared the process of preparing an exotic dinner to creating a new theory.
Quote: "Physics is the art of asking questions that nature answers with beautiful mathematics."
