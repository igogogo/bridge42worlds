👨‍🔬 David DiVincenzo

1959 – настоящее время · quantum computing, condensed matter physics, quantum information
American physicist, known for formulating the DiVincenzo criteria — a list of necessary conditions for the physical realization of a quantum computer. These criteria have become a benchmark for quantum computing system developers worldwide.

Biography

Received his PhD from the University of Pennsylvania, worked at IBM Thomas J. Watson Research Center and as a visiting scientist at various institutes. Currently a professor at RWTH Aachen University and scientific head of quantum computing at Forschungszentrum Jülich.

Key discoveries

  • DiVincenzo criteria: a set of five (later seven) requirements that a physical chip must satisfy to implement quantum computing: scalable qubits, initialization, long coherence time, a universal gate set, readout measurement, and others.
  • Research on spin qubits in silicon and superconducting qubits.
  • Work on quantum error correction and topological quantum computing.
💡 Initially, DiVincenzo formulated the criteria for internal use at IBM, but later they were published and changed the industry's approach to designing quantum processors.
Quote: "The path to a quantum computer is not one big idea, but many small, yet correct steps."
