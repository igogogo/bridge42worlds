👨‍🔬 Jocelyn Bell Burnell

1943– · Astrophysics, Radio astronomy, Physics of compact objects
Jocelyn Bell Burnell is a British astrophysicist who discovered the first radio pulsars in 1967. Her discovery confirmed the existence of neutron stars and provided a powerful tool for studying gravity, cosmology, and extreme states of matter. Bell Burnell has also been active in promoting equality in science. Although the Nobel Prize for the discovery of pulsars was awarded to her supervisor, she has received many other awards and recognition.

Biography

Bell Burnell studied at the University of Glasgow and the University of Cambridge. She worked at Cambridge, the University of Southampton, and the Open University, and served as president of the Royal Astronomical Society.

Key discoveries

  • Discovery of pulsars: detected regular radio pulses from neutron stars, allowing study of their properties.
  • Confirmation of the existence of neutron stars: pulsars turned out to be rotating neutron stars, predicted earlier.
  • Use of pulsars to test general relativity: pulsars became 'cosmic lighthouses' for precise measurements.
💡 Bell Burnell did not receive the Nobel Prize for her discovery, but later was awarded the special Breakthrough Prize in 2018, which she donated to scholarships for women in physics.
Quote: "I believe that science should be accessible to everyone, regardless of gender, race, or background."
