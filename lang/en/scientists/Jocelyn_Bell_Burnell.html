👨‍🔬 Jocelyn Bell Burnell

1943– · astrophysics, radio astronomy
British astrophysicist who discovered the first radio pulsar — a rapidly rotating neutron star. Her observations became key evidence for the existence of neutron stars and revolutionized astrophysics. Although the Nobel Prize went to her supervisor, Bell Burnell received wide recognition and many awards.

Biography

Born in Northern Ireland. Studied at the University of Glasgow, earned her PhD at Cambridge under Anthony Hewish. During her dissertation she built a radio telescope and discovered pulsars. Worked at various scientific institutions, in X-ray and gamma-ray astronomy. President of the Royal Astronomical Society (2002–2004), winner of the Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics (2018) and many other awards.

Key discoveries

  • Discovery of the first radio pulsar CP 1919 (originally named LGM-1 due to the hypothesis of artificial origin). Discovery of three more pulsars
  • confirming the existence of neutron stars.
💡 While meticulously examining kilometers of paper charts, she noticed a regular pulsing signal and initially treated it with humor, suggesting it might be "little green men". The discovery was kept secret until alien interference was ruled out.
Quote: "Science is the quest for understanding, and to understand, one must observe."
