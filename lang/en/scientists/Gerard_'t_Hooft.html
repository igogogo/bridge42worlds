👨‍🔬 Gerard 't Hooft

1946- · quantum field theory, quantum gravity, elementary particle physics
Dutch theoretical physicist, one of the founders of modern quantum field theory. Made fundamental contributions to the proof of renormalizability of gauge theories, which laid the foundation for the Standard Model. Developed the holographic principle in quantum gravity, proposing a connection between quantum field theory and gravity.

Biography

Born in Den Helder. Studied at Utrecht University, where he obtained his doctorate under Martinus Veltman. Worked in Utrecht, then professor there. Winner of the Nobel Prize in Physics (1999) jointly with Veltman. Also known for work on instantons, magnetic monopoles, and quantum black holes.

Key discoveries

  • Proof of renormalizability of non-abelian gauge theories (with Veltman)
  • holographic principle (independently of Susskind)
  • contribution to the theory of quantum black holes
  • including S-matrix and holography.
💡 In his youth, he was interested not only in physics but also in music, playing the saxophone. He is also known for his hobby of collecting scientific errors and misconceptions.
Quote: "I am convinced that the final theory must be beautiful and natural."
