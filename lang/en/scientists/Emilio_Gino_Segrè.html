👨‍🔬 Emilio Gino Segrè

1905–1989 · Nuclear physics, Particle physics
Italian-American physicist who discovered the antiproton — the first antimatter particle in the baryon sector, confirming the symmetry of matter and antimatter.

Biography

Born in Tivoli, Italy. Studied at the University of Rome under the guidance of Enrico Fermi, participating in the legendary group 'the boys from Via Panisperna'. Due to fascist laws, he emigrated to the USA in 1938, worked at the University of California, Berkeley on nuclear physics and the discovery of plutonium. In 1955, together with Owen Chamberlain, he discovered the antiproton, for which he received the Nobel Prize in 1959.

Key discoveries

  • Antiproton: experimental confirmation of the existence of the antiparticle of the proton, predicted by the Dirac equation.
  • Plutonium-239: jointly with Fermi's group discovered a new element, which played a key role in nuclear weapons.
  • Technetium: participated in the synthesis of the first artificial element.
💡 Segrè and Chamberlain used the Bevatron accelerator to produce antiprotons, but to distinguish them from mesons, they built a complex detector system. They bet a case of wine with colleagues on the discovery — and won.
Quote: "“Nature loves symmetry — if there is an electron, there must be a positron, if there is a proton, then where is its anti-world?”"
