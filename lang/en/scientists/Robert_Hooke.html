👨‍🔬 Robert Hooke

1635–1703 · Physics, Biology
Robert Hooke was an English natural philosopher, one of the fathers of microscopy and cell theory. He discovered the law of elasticity (Hooke's law), formulated the idea of universal gravitation before Newton, and contributed to astronomy, physics, and biology. Hooke also improved the microscope and was the first to describe plant cells.

Biography

Hooke studied at Westminster School and the University of Oxford. He worked as Curator of Experiments at the Royal Society of London and as Professor of Geometry at Gresham College.

Key discoveries

  • Hooke's law: the force of elasticity is proportional to deformation (F = -kx), the basis of elasticity theory.
  • Cell theory: in the book 'Micrographia', he first described cells by observing a slice of cork under a microscope.
💡 Hooke was the first to suggest that gravity is inversely proportional to the square of distance, but could not prove it mathematically.
Quote: ""Nature does nothing in vain.""
