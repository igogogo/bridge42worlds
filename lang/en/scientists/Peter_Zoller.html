Peter Zoller

1952– · Quantum optics, Quantum computing, Quantum information
Austrian theoretical physicist, one of the fathers of quantum computing. Together with Ignacio Cirac, he proposed the first realistic scheme for a two-qubit gate on trapped ions (Cirac–Zoller gate). His works cover quantum optics, quantum simulation, and quantum networks, becoming a cornerstone of modern quantum technologies.

Biography

He studied physics at the University of Innsbruck, where he currently works. He had internships at Harvard and MIT. He is the founder of the Institute for Quantum Optics and Quantum Information in Innsbruck. He is a winner of the Wolf Prize in Physics (2013), the Planck Medal, and other awards. He is a member of many academies.

Key discoveries

  • Cirac–Zoller gate: proposed a mechanism for entangling ion qubits using laser pulses, key to quantum processors (1995).
  • Quantum simulation: developed concepts for simulating many-body quantum systems using ultracold atoms and ions.
  • Quantum networks: contributed to the theory of quantum repeaters and distributed quantum computing.
💡 Zoller is an avid mountaineer and often compares conquering mountain peaks to solving complex physics problems. His work on ion traps is used in commercial quantum computers.
Quote: ""A quantum computer is not just a faster calculator; it is a window into the world of quantum correlations.""
