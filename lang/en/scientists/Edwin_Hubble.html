👨‍🔬 Edwin Hubble

1889–1953 · Astronomy, Cosmology, Astrophysics
Edwin Hubble was an astronomer who proved that the universe is expanding. He discovered that galaxies are moving away from us at a speed proportional to distance (Hubble's law), which became direct evidence of cosmic expansion. Hubble also classified galaxies by morphology (spiral, elliptical, and irregular). His observations with the Hubble telescope refined the age of the universe and confirmed the Big Bang theory. He also studied supernovae and interstellar dust.

Biography

Hubble studied at the University of Chicago and Oxford, where he studied law, but then returned to astronomy. He worked at the Mount Wilson Observatory, using the 100-inch telescope for his discoveries.

Key discoveries

  • Hubble's law: linear relationship between the recession velocity of galaxies and their distance, proving the expansion of the universe.
  • Galaxy classification: Hubble sequence for galaxy types.
  • Discovery that nebulae are separate galaxies beyond the Milky Way.
💡 Hubble was an amateur boxer and even received an offer to become a professional boxer, but chose science.
Quote: "With the telescope we look into the past, and the farther we look, the deeper into time we penetrate."
