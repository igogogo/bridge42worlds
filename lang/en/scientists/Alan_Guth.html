👨‍🔬 Alan Guth

1947– · Cosmology, Particle physics, Theoretical physics
Alan Guth is an American theoretical physicist who proposed the theory of cosmic inflation. According to this theory, in the first moments after the Big Bang, the universe underwent a phase of exponential expansion, explaining its homogeneity and flatness. Inflation also predicts the spectrum of fluctuations that led to the formation of galaxies. Guth's work transformed cosmology and became the foundation of the modern model of the universe.

Biography

He earned his bachelor's and doctoral degrees from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). He worked at Princeton University, Columbia University, and Cornell University, then returned to MIT, where he is a professor.

Key discoveries

  • Theory of cosmic inflation: explanation of the early expansion of the universe and solution to the horizon and flatness problems.
  • Prediction of the fluctuation spectrum of the cosmic microwave background, confirmed by the COBE, WMAP, and Planck satellites.
  • Idea of 'false vacuum' as the driving force of inflation.
💡 Guth enjoys running and has participated in the Boston Marathon.
Quote: ""Inflation is not just a theory, but a paradigm that changed our view of the universe.""
