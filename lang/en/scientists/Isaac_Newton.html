Isaac Newton

1643–1727 · Physics, Mathematics, Astronomy, Optics
English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, and alchemist, one of the most influential scientists in history. He formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation, laying the foundations of classical mechanics. He made enormous contributions to optics, proving that white light consists of a spectrum of colors. He developed differential and integral calculus simultaneously with Leibniz.

Biography

Born in Woolsthorpe, England. Graduated from Cambridge University (Trinity College). Professor of mathematics at Cambridge, later president of the Royal Society. Author of “Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy” and “Optics”. Buried in Westminster Abbey.

Key discoveries

  • Law of universal gravitation — explained that the gravitational force between bodies is proportional to their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance; united celestial and terrestrial mechanics.
  • Three laws of motion — the basis of classical mechanics, describing the behavior of bodies under forces (inertia, acceleration, action-reaction).
  • Discovery of the spectrum of white light — using a prism, he decomposed white light into rainbow colors and proved that this is a property of light itself, not of the prism.
  • Invention of the reflecting telescope — built the first functional reflector telescope, free from chromatic aberration.
  • Mathematical analysis — independently of Leibniz, developed differential and integral calculus.
💡 Newton was a passionate alchemist and spent decades searching for the philosopher's stone, which was long concealed to avoid damaging his reputation.
Quote: "“If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants.”"
