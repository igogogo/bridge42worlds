👨‍🔬 Charles Bennett

1943–наст. время · quantum information, cryptography, physics of computation
American physicist and information theorist, one of the founders of quantum information theory. Together with G. Brassard, he developed the first quantum key distribution protocol (BB84) and made decisive contributions to the concept of quantum teleportation. He is also known for his work on reversible computing and the thermodynamics of information.

Biography

Born in New York City. PhD from Harvard. Since 1973 he has worked at IBM Research, where he has spent most of his career. In 1993, together with colleagues, he proposed the quantum teleportation protocol. Wolf Prize laureate in physics (2018) for contributions to quantum information.

Key discoveries

  • BB84 quantum key distribution — the first practically implementable quantum cryptography protocol, using photon polarization.
  • Quantum teleportation (jointly with Brassard, Crépeau, Jozsa, Peres, and Wootters) — transfer of an unknown quantum state between two parties using a classical channel and pre-shared entanglement.
  • Landauer–Bennett principle — the connection between thermodynamics and information, proving that computation can be reversible and does not necessarily dissipate heat.
💡 Bennett is interested in biology and film history; he can often be seen at conferences with a camera.
Quote: "Quantum mechanics gives us not only new physics but also a new way of thinking about the very concept of information."
