👨‍🔬 William Herschel

1738–1822 · Astronomy, Optics, Astrophysics
German-British astronomer and composer, discoverer of the planet Uranus, as well as infrared radiation. He conducted a systematic survey of the sky, compiled catalogs of thousands of nebulae and star clusters. He built the largest reflecting telescopes of his time, which advanced the study of the structure of the Milky Way.

Biography

Born in Hanover. He moved to England, first working as a musician (organist, conductor). He became interested in astronomy, grinding mirrors for telescopes himself, and became the King's Astronomer. His sister Caroline Herschel was a faithful assistant and herself became a renowned astronomer.

Key discoveries

  • Discovery of Uranus (1781) — for the first time since antiquity, discovered a new planet, doubling the boundaries of the solar system.
  • Infrared radiation — by measuring the temperature of different parts of the solar spectrum beyond the red limit, he discovered invisible "heat rays", later called infrared.
  • Surveys of double stars — proved that many of them are physically bound pairs, and began measurements of their orbits.
  • Structure of the Milky Way — proposed a model of our Galaxy as a disk, in which the Sun is not at the center.
  • Deep sky catalogs — together with his sister compiled the largest catalogs of nebulae and star clusters, many of which are known today as Herschel objects.
💡 Herschel was a composer and wrote 24 symphonies, and built his first telescope from scrap materials, including a manure fork as a holder.
Quote: ""We have seen what was invisible, and have looked into the far corners of the Universe.""
