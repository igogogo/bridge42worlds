Marie Curie

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1867–1934 · Physics, Chemistry, Radiology
French physicist and chemist of Polish origin, pioneer in the study of radioactivity. She discovered the elements polonium and radium, developed methods for isolating radioactive isotopes. Twice winner of the Nobel Prize: in Physics (1903) and in Chemistry (1911). Her work laid the foundations of nuclear physics and medical radiology.

Biography

Born in Warsaw (then Russian Empire) as Maria Skłodowska. Studied at the Sorbonne in Paris. Married Pierre Curie, with whom she worked. After his death, she headed the laboratory, became the first female professor at the Sorbonne. She founded the Radium Institute in Paris.

Key discoveries

  • Discovery of radioactivity — together with Pierre Curie, she investigated uranium radiation and coined the term 'radioactivity'.
  • Discovery of polonium and radium — isolated from uranium ore two new chemical elements, significantly more active than uranium.
  • Methods of isotope separation — developed laborious chemical procedures to extract pure radioactive substances from tons of ore.
  • Medical radiology — promoted the use of radioactive radiation for the diagnosis and treatment of cancer.
💡 Maria Curie used her Nobel medals as toys for her daughter Irène, who later also won a Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
