👨‍🔬 Philippe Grangier

1957– · Quantum optics, Quantum cryptography, Quantum measurements
French experimental physicist, pioneer of quantum cryptography and quantum optics. He was the first to experimentally demonstrate photon antibunching (1986), confirming the quantum nature of light. He made a decisive contribution to the creation of quantum key distribution systems based on weak laser pulses, which became a commercial technology.

Biography

He studied at the École Normale Supérieure and the University of Paris-Sud. He worked at the Institut d'Optique in Palaiseau, where he leads the quantum optics group. Winner of the Balzan Prize, the Albert Einstein Medal. Member of the French Academy of Sciences.

Key discoveries

  • Photon antibunching: first observed that a single atom emits photons one at a time, not in bunches, which became direct proof of the quantum nature of light (1986).
  • Plug-and-play quantum cryptography: developed a practical scheme for quantum key distribution with automatic compensation of polarization distortions, widely used in commercial systems.
  • Bell inequalities: conducted precision experiments closing loopholes in tests of local realism.
💡 Grangier is a passionate popularizer: he created the world's first website on quantum cryptography and regularly gives lectures to schoolchildren. His work led to the creation of secure communication lines used by banks.
Quote: ""The quantum world is not crazy; it is just much subtler than we are used to thinking.""
