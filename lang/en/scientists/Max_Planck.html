👨‍🔬 Max Planck

1858–1947 · Quantum physics, Thermodynamics, Theoretical physics
Max Planck was a German physicist, founder of quantum mechanics. In 1900, he introduced the concept of the quantum of action (Planck's constant), explaining the black-body radiation spectrum. This marked the beginning of quantum theory, which changed the understanding of the microcosm. Planck also contributed to thermodynamics and relativity theory.

Biography

Planck studied at the University of Munich and the University of Berlin, earning a doctorate in thermodynamics. He worked as a professor at the University of Kiel, then at the University of Berlin, where he headed the chair of theoretical physics. He was president of the Kaiser Wilhelm Society.

Key discoveries

  • Planck's constant (h) — fundamental constant linking energy and frequency of a quantum (E = hν).
  • Planck's law of radiation — accurate description of the black-body spectrum, resolving the 'ultraviolet catastrophe'.
  • Foundation of quantum theory, leading to the development of quantum mechanics and particle physics.
💡 Planck was a talented pianist and even hesitated between a career as a physicist and a musician. His son Erwin was executed for involvement in a plot against Hitler.
Quote: "Science cannot solve the ultimate mysteries of nature because in the end we ourselves are part of nature."
