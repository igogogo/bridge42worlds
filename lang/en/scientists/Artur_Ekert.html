👨‍🔬 Artur Ekert

1961 – настоящее время · quantum cryptography, quantum information, quantum computing
British-Polish physicist, one of the founders of quantum cryptography. Developed the E91 (Ekert) protocol, using entangled photon pairs for secure key distribution. His work combined Bell's concepts and quantum information, proving that quantum mechanics can guarantee security.

Biography

Studied physics at Jagiellonian University and Oxford, where he received his PhD. Worked at Oxford, then founded the Centre for Quantum Technologies in Singapore. Laureate of the Descartes Prize and the Hughes Medal. Professor at Oxford University and visiting professor in Singapore.

Key discoveries

  • E91 protocol: quantum key distribution based on violation of Bell inequalities, providing provable security independent of device trust.
  • Contributions to quantum computing theory: showed that any unitary operations are realizable in a quantum system.
  • Research in quantum machine learning and quantum networks.
💡 Ekert is an avid pilot and flies his own plane; he jokes that flying a plane is easier than operating a quantum computer.
Quote: "Quantum cryptography is the first technology that gives us absolute security based on fundamental laws of nature."
