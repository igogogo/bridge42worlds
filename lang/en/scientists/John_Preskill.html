👨‍🔬 John Preskill

1953 – настоящее время · quantum computing, quantum information, quantum gravity
American theoretical physicist, one of the pioneers of quantum information and quantum computing. He coined the term 'quantum supremacy' and described the stages of development of quantum technologies. Known for his work on topological quantum computing, quantum error correction, and the connection between black holes and quantum information.

Biography

Studied at Princeton (undergraduate) and Harvard (PhD). Professor at the California Institute of Technology, where he directs the Institute for Quantum Information and Matter. Actively popularizes science, gives a famous lecture course and runs the blog 'Quantum Frontiers'.

Key discoveries

  • Concept of quantum supremacy: a stage at which a quantum computer performs a task beyond the reach of classical supercomputers — this idea became a driving force for the industry.
  • Topological quantum information: research on anyons and toric codes for creating error-resilient qubits.
  • Firewall paradox: participated in formulating the black hole firewall paradox concerning information preservation.
💡 Preskill is known for losing a bet with a colleague (Stephen Jordan) about the achievement of quantum supremacy, but actively promotes the event, hailing it as a great achievement.
Quote: "We are at the dawn of the quantum era — a time when we are learning to control complex quantum systems, possibly the most important frontier of modern science."
