👨‍🔬 Yuri Manin

1937-2023 · mathematics, quantum computing, mathematical physics
Soviet and Russian mathematician, one of the first to propose the concept of quantum computing. In 1980, he published the work 'Computable and Noncomputable', where he introduced the idea of a quantum automaton. Made fundamental contributions to algebraic geometry, number theory, and mathematical physics.

Biography

Born in Simferopol. Studied at Moscow State University, where he later became a professor. Worked at the Steklov Mathematical Institute. From 1992 he worked at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn. Member of many academies, winner of the Lobachevsky Prize and other awards.

Key discoveries

  • First (along with independent works) mathematical justification of the possibility of quantum computers (1980)
  • contribution to algebraic geometry (theory of motives cohomology
  • Manin-Mumford conjecture)
  • theory of nonlinear differential equations
  • work on number theory.
💡 In addition to mathematics, he was interested in poetry and philosophy; he translated poetry from different languages and wrote essays on the relationship between mathematics and culture.
Quote: "Proof is not just formal verification, it is explanation."
