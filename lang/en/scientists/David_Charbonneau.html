David Charbonneau

1974–н.в. · astronomy, exoplanetology
American astronomer, one of the pioneers of the transit method for detecting exoplanets. He led the group that first recorded the transit of an exoplanet across the disk of a star (HD 209458 b), opening a new era in the study of planetary systems.

Biography

He studied at the University of Toronto and Harvard University. He is a professor at Harvard University, a leading researcher in the field of exoplanet transits and atmospheres.

Key discoveries

  • First detection of the transit of exoplanet HD 209458 b (1999)
  • Research on exoplanet atmospheres
💡 He earned the nickname 'planet hunter' because his work on transits led to the discovery of hundreds of new worlds.
Quote: "When we look at transits of exoplanets, we see the shadows of alien worlds."
