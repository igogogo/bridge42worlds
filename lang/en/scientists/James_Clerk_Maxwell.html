👨‍🔬 James Clerk Maxwell

1831–1879 · Electromagnetism, Thermodynamics, Optics
James Clerk Maxwell was a Scottish physicist who created the classical theory of electromagnetism. He formulated Maxwell's equations, which unified electricity, magnetism, and optics into a single theory. His work predicted the existence of electromagnetic waves, later discovered by Hertz. Maxwell also contributed to the kinetic theory of gases and thermodynamics. His ideas became the basis for the theory of relativity and quantum physics.

Biography

Maxwell was born in Edinburgh, Scotland. He studied at the University of Edinburgh and Cambridge. He worked as a professor at Marischal College in Aberdeen, then at King's College London, and finally as the first professor of experimental physics at Cambridge.

Key discoveries

  • Maxwell's equations (1861–1862) — four equations describing all electromagnetic phenomena and predicting electromagnetic waves.
  • Theory of color (1860) — showed that color vision is based on three types of receptors, and created the first color photograph (tartan ribbon).
💡 Maxwell was deeply religious and often wrote poetry; his favorite theme was the connection between science and faith.
Quote: "Pure mathematics is, in a sense, the poetry of logical ideas."
