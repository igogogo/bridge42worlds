👨‍🔬 Shuji Nakamura

1954– · Semiconductor physics, Optoelectronics, Lighting engineering
Shuji Nakamura is a Japanese-American physicist and engineer, inventor of the first blue light-emitting diode (LED). His discovery enabled the creation of white LEDs and energy-efficient light sources, revolutionizing lighting. Nakamura also contributed to the development of laser diodes and semiconductor technologies. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 2014 for his work.

Biography

He studied at the University of Tokushima, where he earned his bachelor's and master's degrees. He worked at Nichia Chemical Industries, where he developed the blue LED under conditions of secrecy. Later he became a professor at the University of California, Santa Barbara.

Key discoveries

  • First blue LED based on gallium nitride: creation of an efficient blue light source necessary for white LEDs.
  • Development of gallium nitride epitaxy technology: method for growing crystals for semiconductor devices.
  • Creation of the blue laser diode: foundation for Blu-ray and other optical devices.
💡 Nakamura worked alone for a long time, and his boss at Nichia did not believe in success, but after the invention, the company earned billions of dollars.
Quote: ""I wanted to do something that no one else could, and I did it.""
