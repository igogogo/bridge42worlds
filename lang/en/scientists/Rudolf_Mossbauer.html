👨‍🔬 Rudolf Mossbauer

1929-2011 · Nuclear Physics, Solid State Physics, Spectroscopy
German physicist who discovered the Mössbauer effect — the recoil-free emission and absorption of gamma rays in a crystal lattice. Nobel Prize laureate in Physics (1961). The effect enabled the creation of ultra-high resolution spectroscopy.

Biography

Born in Munich. Studied at the Technical University of Munich. In 1958, while a graduate student, made his discovery. Worked at Caltech and Munich. Awarded the Nobel Prize at the age of 32.

Key discoveries

  • Discovered recoilless resonant absorption of gamma radiation by nuclei (Mössbauer effect), which became the basis of Mössbauer spectroscopy.
  • Developed precision methods for measuring hyperfine interactions.
💡 The discovery was so unexpected that the first paper about it was rejected by a scientific journal.
