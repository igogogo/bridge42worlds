👨‍🔬 Michael Berry

1941 – настоящее время · quantum mechanics, optics, mathematical physics
British theoretical physicist, known for discovering the Berry phase (geometric phase) in quantum mechanics. This phase, acquired by the wave function during adiabatic transport along a closed loop in parameter space, has profound implications in condensed matter physics, molecular physics, and even robotics.

Biography

Graduated from the University of Exeter and St Andrews, where he received his PhD. Spent his entire career at the University of Bristol. Awarded the Lorentz Medal, Max Planck Medal, and Wolf Prize. Knight bachelor since 1996. Known for elegant explanations of complex physical phenomena (e.g., rainbows and rogue waves).

Key discoveries

  • Berry phase: an additional phase factor acquired by the wave function under cyclic parameter variation, depending on path geometry, not time.
  • Connection of the Berry phase with the Aharonov–Bohm effect and with light polarization in optical fibers.
  • Application of geometric phases to understand the nature of 'rogue waves' and caustics.
💡 Berry was a scientific consultant for the film "District 9" and helped the director devise physically plausible alien technologies.
Quote: "The most beautiful physics is often hidden behind simple, everyday phenomena — like rainbows or reflections on water."
