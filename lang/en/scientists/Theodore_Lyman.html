👨‍🔬 Theodore Lyman

1874–1954 · spectroscopy, atomic physics, astrophysics
American physicist who discovered the ultraviolet series of hydrogen (the Lyman series), widely used in astrophysics to study the interstellar and intergalactic medium, as well as in cosmology (Lyman-alpha forest).

Biography

He studied at Harvard University, where he later worked. He headed the physics laboratory at Harvard and worked on spectroscopy.

Key discoveries

  • Discovery of the Lyman series of hydrogen (1906–1914)
  • Research in ultraviolet spectroscopy
💡 Lyman was a wealthy man and paid for some of the equipment for his ultraviolet research out of his own pocket.
Quote: "The true value of a discovery is sometimes not immediately visible; it only becomes apparent over time when others find applications for it."
