👨‍🔬 Henri Poincaré

1854–1912 · Mathematics, Theoretical physics
Henri Poincaré was a French mathematician, physicist, and philosopher who laid the foundations of relativity theory and chaos theory. Independently of Einstein, he formulated the principle of relativity and Lorentz transformations, and introduced the concept of spacetime. Poincaré also contributed to the theory of gravity and celestial mechanics, including the three-body problem.

Biography

Poincaré graduated from the École Polytechnique and the École des Mines de Paris. He worked as a professor at the Sorbonne and the University of Paris. He was a member of the French Academy of Sciences.

Key discoveries

  • Lorentz transformations and the principle of relativity: Poincaré showed that the equations of electrodynamics are invariant under these transformations, anticipating special relativity.
  • Chaos theory: discovered that small changes in initial conditions can lead to huge differences in the behavior of dynamical systems (butterfly effect).
💡 Poincaré was so absent-minded that he once forgot he was invited to dinner and left without saying goodbye.
