👨‍🔬 Henri Poincaré

1854-1912 · mathematics, theoretical physics, astronomy, philosophy of science
French mathematician, theoretical physicist, and philosopher of science. He laid the foundations of special relativity, predicted the existence of gravitational waves, and made fundamental contributions to celestial mechanics, topology, and chaos theory. He is considered one of the greatest mathematicians of all time and the last universalist capable of covering all science.

Biography

Born in Nancy, France. Studied at the École Polytechnique and the École des Mines in Paris. He was a professor at the Sorbonne and a member of the French Academy of Sciences. In addition to physics and mathematics, he engaged in the philosophy of science and wrote several well-known books on methodology. His cousin was the President of France, Raymond Poincaré. He received many awards, including the Lorentz Medal.

Key discoveries

  • Principle of relativity and constancy of the speed of light: independently of Einstein, he formulated the postulates of special relativity, postulating that the speed of light is constant in all inertial frames.
  • Prediction of gravitational waves: in his 1905 work, he suggested that accelerated masses should generate gravitational waves propagating at the speed of light.
  • Chaos theory and the three-body problem: he proved the non-integrability of the three-body problem and discovered chaotic behavior in dynamical systems, initiating chaos theory.
  • Foundation of topology: he introduced the fundamental group and laid the foundations of algebraic topology, publishing the work 'Analysis situs'.
💡 Poincaré had a phenomenal memory and the ability to visualize multidimensional spaces; he solved complex problems mentally without using drafts. Despite severe nearsightedness, he never wore glasses, preferring his inner world.
Quote: "Science is a cemetery of hypotheses, where many beautiful ideas rest."
