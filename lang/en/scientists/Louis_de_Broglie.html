Louis de Broglie

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1892–1987 · Quantum physics, Wave mechanics, Theoretical physics
French theoretical physicist who proposed the concept of matter waves, laying the foundations of wave mechanics and quantum theory. His idea that particles can exhibit wave properties became one of the cornerstones of modern physics.

Biography

Louis de Broglie was born into an aristocratic family. He studied history at the Sorbonne but then switched to physics. During World War I, he served at a radio station, which sparked his interest in electromagnetic waves. In 1924, in his doctoral dissertation, he advanced the hypothesis of wave properties of matter, which was initially met with skepticism, but after experimental confirmation of electron diffraction, it earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics (1929). Subsequently, he worked on the interpretation of quantum mechanics, developing the pilot-wave theory, an alternative to the Copenhagen interpretation.

Key discoveries

  • Wave-particle duality of matter: he hypothesized that all particles possess wave properties, with wavelength λ = h/p, where h is Planck's constant, p is the particle's momentum.
  • De Broglie formula: mathematically linking wave and particle characteristics, which made it possible to explain electron diffraction and other quantum phenomena.
💡 He came from a noble French family, held the title of prince, and his younger brother Maurice also became a renowned physicist.
