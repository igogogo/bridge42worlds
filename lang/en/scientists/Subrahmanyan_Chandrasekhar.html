👨‍🔬 Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar

1910–1995 · Astrophysics, Stellar evolution, General relativity
Chandrasekhar was an Indian-American astrophysicist known for his discovery of the Chandrasekhar limit—the maximum mass of a white dwarf beyond which it collapses into a neutron star or black hole. He also made fundamental contributions to the theory of radiative transfer, stellar dynamics, and black hole theory. His work laid the foundation for understanding stellar evolution and compact objects.

Biography

He studied at Presidency College in Madras and the University of Cambridge. He worked at the University of Chicago and the Yerkes Observatory, where he spent most of his career.

Key discoveries

  • Chandrasekhar limit: the maximum mass of a white dwarf (about 1.4 solar masses), beyond which the star collapses.
  • Theory of radiative transfer: mathematical description of light propagation through stellar atmospheres.
  • Study of rotating black holes: solution of Einstein's equations for rotating objects.
💡 Chandrasekhar was the nephew of Nobel laureate Chandrasekhara Raman.
