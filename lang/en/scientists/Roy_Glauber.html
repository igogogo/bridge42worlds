👨‍🔬 Roy Glauber

1925–2018 · quantum optics, laser physics
American theoretical physicist, considered the founder of quantum optics. He developed the theory of higher-order optical coherence, introduced the concept of coherent states (Glauber states), and explained photon statistics in laser radiation. Winner of the Nobel Prize in 2005.

Biography

Born in New York. He received his PhD from Harvard. At age 18, he participated in the Manhattan Project. He worked at the Institute for Advanced Study, Caltech, and Harvard University. The Nobel Prize was awarded for his contribution to the quantum theory of optical coherence.

Key discoveries

  • Quantum theory of optical coherence — mathematical description of light coherence at the quantum level.
  • Glauber states — quantum states that are closest to classical electromagnetic waves, serving as the basis for describing laser radiation.
  • Explanation of the Hanbury Brown–Twiss effect — quantum interpretation of photon correlation.
💡 As a student, he was recruited for the Manhattan Project, where at age 18 he calculated nuclear reaction cross sections for the atomic bomb.
Quote: "Light is not only a wave or a particle: it is something more subtle that we are only beginning to understand with the help of quantum theory."
