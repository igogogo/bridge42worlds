👨‍🔬 Peter Higgs

1929–2024 · particle physics, theoretical physics
British theoretical physicist who predicted the existence of the elementary particle — the Higgs boson, responsible for the mechanism of spontaneous electroweak symmetry breaking. The experimental discovery of the boson at the Large Hadron Collider in 2012 confirmed his theory.

Biography

Studied at King's College London. Worked at the University of Edinburgh. Nobel Prize in Physics (2013) jointly with François Englert.

Key discoveries

  • Prediction of the Higgs mechanism and the Higgs boson (1964)
💡 Peter Higgs disliked the boson being called the 'God particle'; he preferred the technical name.
Quote: "The Higgs boson is part of the mechanism that gives other particles mass, and its existence proves that our understanding of the nature of symmetries is correct."
