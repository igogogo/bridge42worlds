👨‍🔬 Peter Higgs

1929–2024 · Particle physics, Quantum field theory
Higgs was a British theoretical physicist who predicted the mechanism of spontaneous symmetry breaking, which explains the origin of mass of elementary particles. His work led to the discovery of the Higgs boson at the Large Hadron Collider in 2012. The Higgs field permeates the universe, interacting with particles and giving them mass. This discovery completed the Standard Model of particle physics.

Biography

Born in Newcastle, England. He studied at King's College London and worked at the University of Edinburgh.

Key discoveries

  • Higgs mechanism: explanation of how particles acquire mass through interaction with the Higgs field.
  • Higgs boson: prediction of the particle carrier of the Higgs field, discovered in 2012.
💡 Higgs was so modest that he did not have a mobile phone and learned about the confirmation of the discovery from a passerby.
Quote: "I never thought I would live to see the discovery of the Higgs boson."
