👨‍🔬 Richard Feynman

1918–1988 · Quantum physics, Electrodynamics, Particle physics
Feynman made fundamental contributions to quantum electrodynamics (QED), for which he received the Nobel Prize. He developed Feynman diagrams, which became a standard tool for calculations in quantum field theory. He also participated in the Manhattan Project and the investigation of the Challenger shuttle disaster. His lectures on physics became classics and inspired generations of scientists.

Biography

Feynman studied at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (bachelor's) and Princeton University (doctorate). He worked at Los Alamos, Cornell University, and the California Institute of Technology (Caltech).

Key discoveries

  • Feynman diagrams — graphical representation of particle interactions, simplifying calculations in quantum field theory.
  • Quantum electrodynamics — theory describing the interaction of light and matter, confirmed with high precision by experiments.
  • Feynman path integrals — alternative formulation of quantum mechanics where a particle moves along all possible paths.
💡 Feynman was an avid bongo player and played in a band. He also cracked safes at Los Alamos for fun.
Quote: "I don't have to answer stupid questions."
