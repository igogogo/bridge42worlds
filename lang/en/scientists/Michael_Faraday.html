👨‍🔬 Michael Faraday

1791–1867 · Electromagnetism, Electrochemistry, Physics
English physicist and chemist, one of the greatest experimentalists in the history of science. He made fundamental contributions to the theory of electromagnetism: discovered electromagnetic induction, the laws of electrolysis, the rotation of the plane of polarization of light in a magnetic field. Invented the first electric motor and dynamo. His concept of the field became dominant in physics.

Biography

Born in London to a poor family, had almost no formal education. Worked as a bookbinder, then as a laboratory assistant to Humphry Davy. Became a professor at the Royal Institution, where he delivered the famous Christmas lectures. Declined many honors, including the presidency of the Royal Society.

Key discoveries

  • Electromagnetic induction — showed that a changing magnetic field generates an electric current in a conductor, which became the basis of electric power generation.
  • Laws of electrolysis — established a quantitative relationship between the amount of electricity passed and the mass of substance deposited.
  • Faraday effect — discovered the rotation of the plane of polarization of light under the influence of a magnetic field, proving the connection between light and magnetism.
  • Invention of the electric motor — created the first primitive motor converting electrical energy into mechanical energy.
  • Concept of the field — proposed the idea of lines of force and fields to describe electrical and magnetic interactions.
💡 Faraday was a deeply religious Christian and attended church every Sunday. Due to poor health, he declined offers to be buried in Westminster Abbey next to Newton.
Quote: "Nothing is too wonderful to be true, if it be consistent with the laws of nature."
