Albert Einstein

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1879–1955 · Theoretical physics, Quantum mechanics, Cosmology
Theoretical physicist who forever changed our understanding of space, time and gravity. Creator of special and general relativity, explained the photoelectric effect, introduced the concept of the photon. His equation E=mc² became a symbol of a new era in physics. His contributions to quantum theory and statistical mechanics are also fundamental.

Biography

Born in Ulm, Germany. Studied at the Swiss Polytechnic School in Zurich. Worked at the patent office in Bern, then professor in Zurich, Prague, Berlin. In the summer of 1933 he emigrated to the USA, worked at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. Winner of the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.

Key discoveries

  • Special theory of relativity (1905) — showed that time and space are relative and the speed of light is constant; introduced the concept of spacetime.
  • General theory of relativity (1915) — described gravity as the curvature of spacetime by matter and energy, predicted black holes and gravitational waves.
  • Photoelectric effect — explained the ejection of electrons by light, assuming the quantum nature of light (photons), which was a key step in the development of quantum mechanics.
  • Quantum theory of heat capacity — applied the quantum hypothesis to explain the heat capacity of solids at low temperatures.
💡 As a child, Einstein started speaking so late that his parents feared he might be developmentally delayed. And later, he never learned to drive a car.
