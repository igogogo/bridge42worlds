👨‍🔬 Albert Einstein

1879–1955 · Theoretical physics, Cosmology
Albert Einstein was a German theoretical physicist, creator of the special and general theories of relativity, who explained the photoelectric effect and contributed to quantum mechanics. His famous equation E=mc² showed the equivalence of mass and energy. Einstein also predicted gravitational waves and the expansion of the universe, which were later confirmed.

Biography

Einstein graduated from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich, worked at the patent office in Bern, then as a professor in Zurich, Prague, and Berlin. After the Nazis came to power, he emigrated to the United States and worked at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.

Key discoveries

  • Special theory of relativity: the postulate of the constancy of the speed of light and the relativity of simultaneity, leading to the Lorentz transformations.
  • General theory of relativity: gravity as curvature of spacetime, explaining the anomaly in Mercury's orbit and predicting black holes.
💡 Einstein declined an offer to become president of Israel, stating that he had no aptitude for governance.
