👨‍🔬 Isidor Isaac Rabi

1898–1988 · Quantum mechanics, Atomic physics, Nuclear magnetic resonance
American physicist, Nobel laureate. He developed the magnetic resonance method for studying atomic nuclei, and discovered Rabi oscillations — coherent transitions between quantum states under the action of a resonant field. Rabi oscillations are the basic mechanism for controlling qubits in modern quantum processors.

Biography

Born in Austria-Hungary, he emigrated to the USA. He studied at Cornell and Columbia Universities. He worked at Columbia University, where he built a strong school of atomic physics. During the war, he participated in radar development and the Manhattan Project. He won the Nobel Prize in Physics (1944) for his resonance method for measuring nuclear magnetic moments. He served as a science advisor to the US President.

Key discoveries

  • Magnetic resonance method: developed a technique for precise measurement of nuclear magnetic moments (1937).
  • Rabi oscillations: theoretically described and experimentally observed periodic quantum transitions in two-level systems, underlying the operation of qubits.
  • Atomic clocks: his methods led to the creation of the first atomic frequency standards.
💡 Rabi was one of the few scientists who insisted on the peaceful use of atomic energy and opposed the arms race. His student Julian Schwinger became one of the creators of quantum electrodynamics.
