Andrei Linde

! scientist · # tag · @ author
род. 1948 · Cosmology, theoretical physics, quantum field theory
Soviet-American theoretical physicist, one of the creators of inflationary theory, which explains the homogeneity of the Universe right after the Big Bang. His model of chaotic inflation offered an elegant solution to many cosmological problems.

Biography

Born in 1948 in Moscow. He studied at Moscow State University, where he received his doctorate in 1975. He worked at the Lebedev Physical Institute of the USSR Academy of Sciences and then at CERN. In 1990, he emigrated to the United States, where he became a professor at Stanford University. He is known for developing new forms of inflationary scenarios. He is a recipient of many awards, including the Gruber Prize in Cosmology.

Key discoveries

  • Development of the theory of cosmic inflation
  • in particular
  • the chaotic inflation model (1983)
  • which describes
  • how quantum fluctuations during inflationary expansion generate primordial inhomogeneities for galaxy formation
  • the scenario of eternal inflation and the multiverse
  • pioneering work on phase transitions in the early Universe.
💡 Linde became interested in cosmology after his teacher, Yakov Zeldovich, suggested he solve the monopole problem. It is said that when he first saw the formula for chaotic inflation, he was at his dacha in the woods and thought: 'Now I know how the Universe works.'
Quote: "Our Universe is only a small part of a much larger cosmos, where new inflationary regions are constantly born, and this process has no end."
