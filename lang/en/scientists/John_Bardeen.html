👨‍🔬 John Bardeen

1908-1991 · condensed matter physics, quantum theory, electronics
American physicist, the only person to receive two Nobel Prizes in physics. The first for the invention of the transistor (the foundation of modern electronics), the second for the BCS theory of superconductivity. His works underlie many quantum devices.

Biography

Born in Madison, Wisconsin. Studied at the University of Wisconsin, PhD at Princeton. Worked at Bell Labs, then professor at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Member of the National Academy of Sciences. Nobel laureate (1956, 1972).

Key discoveries

  • BCS theory of superconductivity (1957) (jointly with Cooper and Schrieffer)
  • explaining the quantum mechanism of superconductivity
  • transistor effect (1947) with Brattain and Shockley
  • surface states in semiconductors.
💡 Bardeen was very modest and avoided the limelight; upon hearing of his second Nobel Prize, he first went to play golf.
Quote: "The main thing is to understand how little we actually know. That opens the door to discoveries."
