👨‍🔬 Henri Poincaré

1854–1912 · Theoretical physics, Mathematics, Celestial mechanics
Poincaré laid the foundations of the theory of relativity, independently of Einstein formulating the principle of relativity and Lorentz transformations. He also predicted gravitational waves and contributed to quantum mechanics by proposing the idea of action quantization. His work on system dynamics led to the discovery of chaos and bifurcation theory. Poincaré also developed topology and homology theory, which became the foundation of modern mathematical physics.

Biography

He studied at the École Polytechnique and the École des Mines in Paris. He worked as a professor at the Sorbonne and the University of Paris, and as a member of the Bureau of Longitudes.

Key discoveries

  • Principle of relativity: formulated the postulate that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames, which became the basis of special relativity.
  • Chaos theory: discovered that deterministic systems can exhibit unpredictable behavior (butterfly effect) while studying the three-body problem.
💡 Poincaré was the last universal mathematician: he made fundamental discoveries in all areas of mathematics of his time, including algebra, analysis, geometry, and topology.
Quote: "Science is built up of facts, as a house is built of stones; but an accumulation of facts is no more a science than a heap of stones is a house."
