👨‍🔬 Kip Thorne

1940– · Gravitational physics, General relativity, Astrophysics
Kip Thorne is an American theoretical physicist, one of the founders of gravitational-wave astronomy. He made key contributions to the creation of the LIGO detector, which in 2015 first detected gravitational waves from the merger of black holes. Thorne is also known for his work on black hole theory, wormholes, and time travel. His research connects general relativity with astrophysics.

Biography

He studied at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) and Princeton University. He spent his entire career as a professor at Caltech, where he led the gravitational physics group.

Key discoveries

  • Design and construction of LIGO: the first gravitational wave detector, opening a new window on the universe.
  • Theory of wormholes: analysis of the possibility of traversable wormholes in general relativity.
  • Modeling of black hole mergers: numerical calculations of gravitational wave signals.
💡 Thorne was the scientific consultant for the film 'Interstellar' and even wrote a book about the science in the film.
Quote: ""Gravitational waves are a new era in astronomy, like the invention of the telescope.""
