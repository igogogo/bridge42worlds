Lisa Randall

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1962–present · theoretical physics, particle physics, cosmology
American theoretical physicist, who together with Raman Sundrum created the model of warped extra dimensions (the Randall–Sundrum model), which offers a solution to the hierarchy problem in particle physics. Her work links particle physics, cosmology, and string theory, in particular explaining the weakness of gravity.

Biography

Lisa Randall was born in 1962 in New York. She received her education at Harvard University, earning her PhD there. She taught at MIT and Princeton, and is currently a professor at Harvard. Author of popular physics books. Member of the National Academy of Sciences. Actively involved in popularizing science.

Key discoveries

  • Randall–Sundrum model (1999) – suggests the existence of one (RS-1) or more (RS-2) extra spatial dimensions
  • warped along anti-de Sitter space. The warped geometry allows gravity to vary significantly across the extra dimension
  • explaining
  • why it is weaker than the other interactions. The model predicts excitations with masses at the TeV scale
  • accessible at colliders.
💡 Lisa Randall is one of the most cited theoretical physicists in the world and often appears on lists of influential women in science. She even appeared as herself in the TV series "The Big Bang Theory".
Quote: "Extra dimensions might prove to be not just a mathematical fantasy, but a reality hidden from our eyes."
