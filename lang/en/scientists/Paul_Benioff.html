Paul Benioff

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1935-2022 · quantum computing, theoretical physics, quantum information
American theoretical physicist, the first to describe a quantum mechanical model of a computer. In 1980 (simultaneously with Yuri Manin) he showed that quantum systems can be used to perform computations, and introduced the concept of a quantum Turing machine.

Biography

Born in New York. Educated in chemistry and physics, PhD in nuclear chemistry. Worked at Argonne National Laboratory. Despite modest fame, his work laid the foundation for quantum computing. He was active until the end of his life, publishing works on quantum information.

Key discoveries

  • Concept of a quantum computer as a quantum mechanical system
  • capable of simulating a classical Turing machine (1980)
  • development of a quantum Turing machine model
  • research on quantum algorithms and complexity.
💡 He came to quantum computing from nuclear chemistry, which was atypical; his pioneering work long went unnoticed until the rise of interest in quantum computers.
Quote: "Quantum mechanics not only describes the world, but can also be used to process it."
