Michel Mayor

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1942–present · astrophysics, astronomy
Swiss astrophysicist, Nobel Prize laureate in Physics (2019) for the discovery of the first exoplanet orbiting a sun-like star – 51 Pegasi b. His work opened a new era in astronomy and led to the development of methods for detecting and studying the atmospheres of distant worlds.

Biography

Michel Mayor was born in 1942 in Lausanne, Switzerland. He received his PhD from the University of Geneva, where he worked for most of his career. Before discovering exoplanets, he studied binary stars and Galactic dynamics. Together with Didier Queloz, he developed the ELODIE spectrograph, which made it possible to detect the faint wobbles in the star's radial velocity.

Key discoveries

  • 51 Pegasi b (1995) – first planet outside the Solar System
  • orbiting a main-sequence star. It is a 'hot Jupiter'
  • with an orbital period of about 4 days. The radial velocity method
  • used for the discovery
  • became standard. Development of high-precision spectrographs (CORALIE
  • HARPS)
  • which allowed the discovery of hundreds of other exoplanets
  • including Earth-type planets.
💡 When Mayor and Queloz first saw the planet's signal, they thought it was an instrument error and spent months checking before believing the discovery.
Quote: "We have discovered a new world, and this is only the beginning of a great journey."
