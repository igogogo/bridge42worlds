👨‍🔬 Niels Bohr

1885–1962 · quantum physics, atomic physics
Danish theoretical physicist, creator of the quantum atomic model that explained the spectral lines of hydrogen (Bohr model). He made a decisive contribution to the development of quantum mechanics and its interpretation (Copenhagen interpretation). Debated with Einstein on the completeness of quantum theory.

Biography

He studied at the University of Copenhagen, then worked at the Manchester laboratory under Rutherford. Founded the Institute for Theoretical Physics in Copenhagen. Nobel laureate in physics (1922).

Key discoveries

  • Planetary model of the atom with quantized orbits (1913)
  • Correspondence principle
  • Copenhagen interpretation of quantum mechanics
💡 During World War II, Bohr was secretly taken out of occupied Denmark on an American bomber, where he had to sit in the bomb bay.
Quote: "The opposite of a correct statement is a false statement. But the opposite of a profound truth may be another profound truth."
