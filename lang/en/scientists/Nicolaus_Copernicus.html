👨‍🔬 Nicolaus Copernicus

1473–1543 · Astronomy, Mathematics, Economics
Polish astronomer, mathematician, economist and canon, author of the heliocentric system of the world, which initiated the scientific revolution of the 16th century. He placed the Sun at the center of the planetary system and made the Earth one of the planets, thus destroying Ptolemy's geocentric model. His work "On the Revolutions of the Heavenly Spheres" became a turning point in the history of science.

Biography

Born in Toruń. He studied at the University of Krakow, then in Italy (Bologna, Padua, Ferrara), where he studied law, medicine and astronomy. After returning, he worked as a canon and administrator in Frombork, simultaneously engaging in astronomical observations and mathematical calculations.

Key discoveries

  • Heliocentric system — proposed a model in which the Earth and other planets revolve around a stationary Sun, explaining the diurnal and annual motions of celestial bodies.
  • Explanation of retrograde motion of planets — showed that the apparent retrograde motion of planets is a consequence of the Earth's motion, not a real deceleration.
  • Calendar reform — participated in the preparation of the Gregorian calendar reform.
  • Copernicus' economic law — formulated the principle later known as Gresham's law: "bad money drives out good".
💡 Copernicus was a versatile person: he served as a physician, managed the economic affairs of the diocese, developed dam projects, and even wrote poetry.
Quote: ""The Sun sits on a throne, governing the family of planets revolving around it.""
