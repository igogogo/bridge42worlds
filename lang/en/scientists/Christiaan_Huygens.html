Christiaan Huygens

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1629–1695 · Optics, Mechanics, Astronomy, Mathematics
Dutch mathematician, physicist and astronomer, one of the founders of theoretical mechanics and optics. He created the wave theory of light and explained double refraction. He invented the pendulum clock and constructed an improved telescope, with which he discovered Saturn's rings and its moon Titan. He contributed to probability theory and mathematical physics.

Biography

Born in The Hague. He studied at Leiden University and Breda University. He worked at the Paris Academy of Sciences, where he enjoyed the patronage of Louis XIV. He returned to Holland at the end of his life. He maintained extensive correspondence with contemporaries, including Newton and Leibniz.

Key discoveries

  • Wave theory of light — proposed that light propagates as waves, and successfully explained reflection, refraction, and double refraction in Iceland spar.
  • Invention of the pendulum clock — first applied a pendulum to regulate the movement of a clock, significantly increasing its accuracy, and received a patent.
  • Discovery of Saturn's rings and Titan — using a telescope he made himself, he correctly described the rings as a system of solid particles and discovered Saturn's largest moon.
  • Theory of centrifugal force — derived the formula for centrifugal acceleration and investigated the motion of a pendulum along a cycloid.
  • Huygens' principle — each element of a wavefront is a source of secondary waves, which underlies modern wave optics.
💡 Huygens was not only a scientist but also a musician; he invented a clavichord with 31 keys per octave for playing "pure" musical intervals.
Quote: ""The world is my country, science is my religion.""
