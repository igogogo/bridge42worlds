👨‍🔬 Galileo Galilei

1564–1642 · Astronomy, Physics, Mathematics
Italian physicist, astronomer, and mathematician, the 'father of modern science'. He was the first to systematically use the experimental method and mathematical description in natural science. He improved the telescope and made revolutionary astronomical discoveries, confirming the Copernican heliocentric system. He formulated the principle of relativity and the law of inertia, laying the foundations of classical mechanics.

Biography

Born in Pisa. Studied at the University of Pisa, then taught mathematics in Padua and later was court mathematician in Florence. For supporting heliocentrism, he was tried by the Inquisition and forced to recant. Author of 'Dialogue Concerning the Two Chief World Systems'.

Key discoveries

  • Astronomical discoveries with the telescope — discovered mountains on the Moon, sunspots, the four largest moons of Jupiter (Galilean moons), the phases of Venus, which proved its rotation around the Sun.
  • Law of falling bodies — showed that in the absence of air resistance, bodies fall with the same acceleration regardless of mass.
  • Principle of relativity (Galilean transformations) — stated that the laws of mechanics are the same in all inertial frames of reference.
  • Invention of the thermoscope — a precursor to the thermometer.
  • Theory of tides — attempted (incorrectly) to explain tides by the motion of the Earth.
💡 Galileo was a skilled lute player and passed his love of music to his son, who became a composer.
Quote: "'And yet it moves!' (attributed)."
