👨‍🔬 David Bohm

1917–1992 · quantum mechanics, plasma physics, philosophy of science
American-British theoretical physicist whose work spans quantum mechanics, plasma theory, and the philosophy of mind. He proposed an interpretation of quantum mechanics with hidden variables (pilot-wave theory) and together with Y. Aharonov predicted the Aharonov–Bohm effect.

Biography

Born in Wilkes-Barre, USA. PhD from the University of California, Berkeley. He worked at Princeton University but due to McCarthy-era persecution left the United States. He taught at the University of São Paulo (Brazil) and Birkbeck College in London. His ideas were long rejected but later gained recognition.

Key discoveries

  • Pilot-wave theory (de Broglie–Bohm) — a deterministic interpretation of quantum mechanics in which particles have trajectories guided by the wave function.
  • Aharonov–Bohm effect — the influence of electromagnetic potential on a quantum particle even in the absence of a field (predicted in 1959, experimentally confirmed).
  • Bohm diffusion — anomalously fast transport of particles across a magnetic field in plasma.
💡 Due to political persecution, Bohm could not attend the defense of his own dissertation; his advisor Robert Oppenheimer represented him.
Quote: "Quantum theory requires a new view of reality, in which the whole determines the behavior of the parts, not the other way around."
