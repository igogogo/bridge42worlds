Nancy Grace Roman

! scientist · # tag · @ author
1925–2018 · astronomy, astrophysics, space sciences
American astronomer known as the 'Mother of Hubble' for tirelessly championing the idea of space telescopes. She played a key role in planning and implementing the Hubble project, as well as in programs to study galaxies and stellar populations.

Biography

She studied at Swarthmore College and the University of Chicago. She worked at NASA, where she became the first woman in a leadership position in the field of astronomy. She contributed to the development of satellite observatories.

Key discoveries

  • Pioneering work on the study of the chemical composition of stars depending on their position in the Galaxy
  • Role in the creation of the Hubble Space Telescope
💡 Roman was interested in astronomy from childhood and even organized an astronomy club at school, despite a lack of support.
Quote: "If I hadn't become an astronomer, I would probably have become a lawyer — I always had a gift for persuasion."
