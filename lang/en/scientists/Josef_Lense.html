👨‍🔬 Josef Lense

1890-1985 · General Relativity, Astrophysics, Mathematics
Austrian physicist and mathematician, co-author of the Lense–Thirring effect — the prediction of the dragging of inertial reference frames by a rotating massive body in general relativity.

Biography

Born in Vienna. Studied at the University of Vienna, where he later became a professor of mathematics. In 1918, together with Hans Thirring, published a paper on the influence of rotating massive bodies on spacetime metric. Worked in the field of differential geometry and mathematical physics.

Key discoveries

  • Together with Hans Thirring calculated the effect of dragging inertial frames (Lense–Thirring effect) within the framework of general relativity.
💡 The effect was long considered too small to measure, but was confirmed by the Gravity Probe B satellite in 2004.
