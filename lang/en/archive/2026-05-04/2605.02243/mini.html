Atmosphere found on a small icy body beyond Neptune ⚡ экспресс

Original: "The first detection of an atmosphere on a trans-Neptunian object beyond Pluto"
· Ko Arimatsu, Fumi Yoshida, Tsutomu Hayamizu, Satoshi Takita, Katsumasa Hosoi, Takafumi Ootsubo, Jun-ichi Watanabe
arXiv:2605.02243 · 2026-05-04 · CC BY · ⏱ 1 min · Exoplanets
For the first time beyond Neptune, besides Pluto, an atmosphere has been detected on an icy body.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

A tiny icy world beyond Pluto, the size of an asteroid, unexpectedly showed signs of an atmosphere. When it passed in front of a star, the light refracted — that's how astronomers realized: even small celestial bodies can hold onto gases. This discovery rewrites ideas about where to look for active worlds in deep space.

🎯 The shadow of this object on Earth was as wide as a small town; it was caught thanks to dozens of volunteer telescopes.
