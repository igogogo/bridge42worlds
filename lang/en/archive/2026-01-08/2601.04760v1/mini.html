Original: "Probing Trans-Planckian Signatures in the Early Universe: A Bayesian Analysis of the Generalized Sasaki-Mukhanov Equation"
A new perspective on the ripples of the newborn Universe, with a tiny addition, solves the mystery of the cosmic 'quiet zone'.

The early Universe inflated like a bubble, leaving ripples. But on the map of the cosmic microwave background, a strange 'quiet zone' gapes. A new theory with a tiny correction (just 0.0001) explains this puzzle without breaking the standard model. Perhaps we’ve caught the tip of a thread leading to quantum gravity.

🎯 The first moments of inflation are shrouded in quantum fog, but their echoes stretched to the sizes of galactic superclusters — a quiet whisper became the architect of the cosmic web.
