Original: "Probing the Fermi Sea Topology in a Quantum Gas"

Physicists have measured for the first time the shape and "holiness" of the Fermi sea lurking in an ultracold lithium gas.

Quantum particles lined up into a sea that holds the secret of its shape. Scientists managed to decipher it through rare encounters of atoms, like a detective from clues. This method will allow peering into the interiors of neutron stars without leaving the laboratory.

🎯 Without the Pauli principle, electrons in atoms would roll down to the lowest level, and all chemical elements would become indistinguishable — the world would turn into a homogeneous mixture.
