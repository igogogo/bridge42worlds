Original: "Novel Realizations of Warp Drive Spacetimes as Solutions of General Relativity"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

The classic Alcubierre warp drive model involves a bubble moving faster than light by warping space. The authors analyzed its flaws in a covariant 3+1 formalism (separate description of space and time) and derived equations for Natário class metrics. It turned out that the warp field is unstable: just as a fast river current generates eddies, coordinate vorticity (rotation of space) causes instability. A new approach is proposed, merging warp field dynamics with cosmological models (Szekeres solutions), paving the way toward physically meaningful warp drives.
