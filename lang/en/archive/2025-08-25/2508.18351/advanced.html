Abstract

Analysis of AB Aurigae observations with MUSE/VLT in the Hα line revealed emission from the protoplanet AB Aur b. Emission was recorded at 6558.88–6560.13 Å (shift -100 km/s) and absorption at 6562.8–6565.1 Å (+75 km/s). The spectrum does not match the star, disk, or protoplanets PDS 70 b and c; instead, it resembles an inverse P Cygni profile—an indicator of infalling cold gas in accreting T Tauri stars. Other sources of the signal unrelated to accretion cannot be formally ruled out. AB Aur b is the second system detected in Hα and the first with an inverse P Cygni profile. Future modeling and new optical data are needed to determine whether the signal is caused by accretion onto the planet, scattered light, or another mechanism.
