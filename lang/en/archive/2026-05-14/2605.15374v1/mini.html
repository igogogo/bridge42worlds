Original: "The cosmological inflation inside the cyclic model of the universe"
Inflation is not a single event, but a recurring phase that inflates the Universe after each contraction cycle, like bellows fanning a forge.

The Universe did not explode once — it breathes like blacksmith’s bellows. Compression to Planck-scale sizes and a bounce ignite inflation, like a blast of oxygen into a furnace. Two scalar fields — the handle and the valve — forge a new cosmos from the ashes, and gravitational waves from past cycles may already be trembling in the polarization of the cosmic microwave background.

🎯 If inflation indeed repeats, then the observed homogeneity of our Universe may be the echo of inflationary phases in countless previous cycles, and gravitational waves from those eras may have left a faint imprint in the polarization of the cosmic microwave background.

🎬 Cyclic cosmology with repeated acts of creation has also inspired science fiction writers: in Paul Anderson's novel 'Worlds Without End,' the protagonists witness the collapse of the cosmos and a new Big Bang.
