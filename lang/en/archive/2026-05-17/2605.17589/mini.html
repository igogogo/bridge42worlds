Original: "Impact of the axion-like self-interactions in gravitational atoms for LISA"
The LISA detector will hear how dark matter changes the hum of colliding black holes.

Dark matter might cloak black holes in quantum clouds, like fog around a lamp. The future space detector LISA will catch the distortions in gravitational waves – and then we'll finally know what the hidden mass of the universe is made of.

🎯 Dark matter particles in such clouds can be billions of times lighter than the electron, but their collective pressure can change the trajectory of an entire black hole.
