An Invisible Intruder Smashed Into the Milky Way: The Impact Scar Found in Stars express

Original: "Signature of a Dark Ultra-compact Dwarf Galaxy Transiting the Milky Way Disk"
· Ken Udagawa, Tomoharu Oka, Hiroki Yokozuka, Tatsuya Kotani
arXiv:2508.19643 · 2025-08-27 · CC BY 4.0 · 1 min
Galaxies
A stellar 'dent' has been discovered in the Milky Way — a telltale mark left by a dark galaxy invisible to telescopes.
1

Stars in the Milky Way are 'dipping' downward due to an invisible dwarf galaxy's flyby. This dark 'underachiever' never ignited a single star but left its mark on stellar motion. Findings like this shed light on how dark matter works.

🎯 If dark matter were visible, the night sky would be dotted with hundreds of tiny ghost galaxies circling the Milky Way.

🎬 'Dark galaxies' are like the ghost ships of science fiction — massive but invisible.
