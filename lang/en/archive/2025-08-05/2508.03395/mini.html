Inside the remnants of the famous 1987A explosion, the Webb telescope spotted a neutron star that received a powerful 'kick'.

When the supernova exploded in 1987, no one knew if a neutron star was left behind. The James Webb Telescope has now spotted it in the cloud's center — it's racing away at 500 km/s, as if it got a mighty kick. This may be key to understanding the most destructive explosions in the universe.

🎯 If this neutron star were heading toward Earth, it would reach the Moon in just 13 minutes.
