Original: "Memory effects in pulsed optomechanical systems"

Abstract

Physicists have demonstrated that an optomechanical system (where light interacts with mechanical vibrations) under laser pulses can work as a programmable memory element. Specially chosen pulse shapes—Gaussian and sinusoidal—induce hysteresis, quantized transitions, and selective energy accumulation. The authors derived analytical and numerical criteria for these effects and introduced a dimensionless geometric factor that allows quantitative assessment of 'memory capacity.' This is compatible with existing platforms and promises a new class of devices for quantum computing.
