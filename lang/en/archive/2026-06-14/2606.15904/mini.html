Original: "Towards the Detection of Thermal Solar Neutrinos"
Dark matter detectors have been repurposed for the first time to snare nearly ghostly solar neutrinos.

A dark matter detector unexpectedly caught traces of solar neutrinos. Like an ultrasensitive microphone, it picked out a whisper amid the noise. This trick will open a new window into stellar physics.

🎯 Neutrinos flee the Sun’s core in a couple of seconds, while the light we see takes tens of thousands of years to struggle out.
