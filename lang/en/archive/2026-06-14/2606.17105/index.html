Original: "Mass Extinctions by Gravitational Tides"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

In the outer reaches of the solar system, there likely exists a multitude of planetary-mass objects. Gravitational tugs occasionally fling them into the inner regions, where they can pass dangerously close to Earth. Such close encounters—more frequent than direct collisions—trigger powerful tidal effects: giant tsunamis, volcanic outbursts, receding seas, and abrupt climate shifts. These catastrophes could be behind several mass extinctions over the past 600 million years. Interestingly, such events leave 'chemical signatures' in the form of a global layer of meteoritic dust.
