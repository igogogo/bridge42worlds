Original: "Constraining Gravitational Wave Memory with Hierarchical Inference"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Gravitational waves carry not only oscillations but also a permanent displacement of space — the so-called memory effect, predicted by the nonlinear nature of general relativity. Instead of controversial Bayesian factors, a hierarchical Bayesian analysis of the GWTC-5.0 catalog was used here to estimate the memory contribution from all events. The measured memory amplification factor was 0.26 with wide uncertainty — compatible with Einstein's value of 1. However, confident detection will require about 2000 black hole mergers. Just as a trampoline remains stretched after a jump, spacetime "remembers" past gravitational bursts.
