Original: "Earth-lens telescope for distant axion-like particle sources with stimulated backward reflection"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Earth's gravitational field can act as a lens, focusing dark matter particles (axion-like particles, ALPs) into a cosmic focal point. By aiming a laser beam there, we can stimulate their decay and detect the return signal. Simulations assuming ALP speeds of 520 km/s (from the S1 stellar stream) place the focus at about 9.6 billion meters (~25 lunar distances), with a sensitivity of g/M ~ 10⁻²² GeV⁻¹ for a source at 8 kpc. This lays the groundwork for future space telescopes capable of detecting dark matter beyond the Milky Way.
