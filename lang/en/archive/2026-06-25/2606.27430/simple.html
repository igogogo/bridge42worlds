The Hidden Cosmic Traveller: Why Did the Blazar Conceal Its Superfast Jet? ✦

Original: "A hidden ultra-relativistic spine in the jet of a neutrino-associated blazar"
· Y. Y. Kovalev, F. Eppel, D. C. Homan, A. V. Plavin, P. Benke, A. K. Erkenov, J. L. Gomez, M. Kadler, K. I. Kellermann, P. I. Kivokurtseva, Yu. A. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, V. A. Makeev, A. V. Popkov, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen, Yu. V. Sotnikova, S. V. Troitsky
arXiv:2606.27430 · 2026-06-25 · CC BY · 1 min · High Energy Galaxies
In blazar TXS 0506+056, a hidden core within its jet has been found, moving at an apparent speed of 21 times the speed of light — without breaking physics.
Abstract

Black holes launch jets. The source TXS 0506+056 initially seemed too slow to produce neutrinos. But new radio observations found a hidden disturbance racing at 21 times the speed of light. It's like a flashlight beam in fog: a fast inner flow illuminates a slow sheath, explaining the delay between neutrino and radio flares. So, where are neutrinos born?

Galaxy TXS 0506+056 is an active galactic nucleus with a supermassive black hole at its centre, surrounded by a swirling disk of gas. From the poles erupts a jet of superheated plasma, shooting almost directly at Earth. Due to the expansion of the Universe, its light has been redshifted — an effect first described by Maarten Schmidt. In 2017, a neutrino arrived with the punch of a hockey puck, but observations from the VLBI network showed the jet crawling at just 1–2 times the speed of light — way too slow for such a neutrino.

Re-analysing 15 years of radio data, scientists stopped tracking individual blobs and noticed a hidden wave zipping along at an apparent speed of 21 times the speed of light — this superluminal trick arises from the laws of relativity of Einstein and Lorentz. The jet works like a nesting doll: inside lurks an invisible high-speed spine, and wrapped around it is a sluggish outer shell. The neutrino is born in the spine instantly, but the radio flare only becomes apparent years later, when the shock wave hits the shell and snaps the magnetic field lines back together. In 2021, the pattern repeated — meaning nesting-doll jets are everywhere.

🎯 Blazars can create the illusion of movement up to 50 times the speed of light, but in TXS 0506+056, the record-breaking wave was hidden behind a slow-moving shell. It was found only when they stopped chasing individual clumps.
