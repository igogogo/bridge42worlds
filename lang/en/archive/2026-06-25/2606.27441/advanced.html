Collider Probes of Dark Energy Microphysics ✦

Original: "Collider Probes of Dark Energy Microphysics"
· Chaitanya Bashyam, Alfredo Gurrola, Andres Florez, Cristian Rodriguez
arXiv:2606.27441 · 2026-06-25 · CC BY · 3 min · HEP Phenomenology Cosmology General Relativity HEP Experiment
Resonance measurements of new particles at the Large Hadron Collider can shed light on the nature of dark energy, including its sound speed.
Abstract

The nature of dark energy is unknown. Although observations constrain the equation of state w, many models reproduce the same expansion history. The key distinguishing feature — the speed of sound of perturbations — has so far remained unconstrained. A method of collider measurements of BSM mediator resonances is proposed. An effective field theory is constructed: a dynamical scalar dark energy field is coupled through derivative interactions to a pseudoscalar mediator in the 2HDM+a model. This induces invisible decays and alters the mediator's propagation in the dark energy background, distorting its resonance properties (widths, decay channels, kinematics) at the LHC. The distortions are sensitive to the speed of sound of fluctuations, enabling the distinction of models indistinguishable by cosmological data. Colliders provide direct access to the microphysics of dark energy, linking the micro world with cosmology.

Context

The discovery of the accelerated expansion of the Universe pointed to the dominance of mysterious dark energy, whose microscopic origin is unknown. Existing probes, including studies of the cosmic microwave background and large-scale structure, have precisely measured its equation of state (w), but this is not enough to choose between theoretical models. The decisive discriminator is the sound speed of perturbations, which so far remains poorly constrained. Meanwhile, colliders like the Large Hadron Collider open a window into physics beyond the Standard Model, and the idea of unifying these two frontiers offers unprecedented opportunities.

Methods

The researchers construct a unified effective field theory where the dark energy scalar ϕ couples via derivative interactions to a pseudoscalar mediator a from the 2HDM+a model (an extension of the Higgs sector with an additional pseudoscalar). Thanks to approximate shift symmetry, by Noether's theorem, the leading operators take the form a(∂ϕ)^2 and (∂ϕ)^2(∂a)^2. By analogy with phonons in a medium, the background kinetic energy of the ϕ field renormalizes the mediator wavefunction, while fluctuations are produced as real particles at the collider. Decay width calculations are performed using the standard S-matrix formalism, accounting for the modified phase space due to the nontrivial dispersion relation ω = c_s k.

Results

It is found that the partial width of the invisible decay a→ϕϕ acquires a pronounced dependence on the sound speed of dark energy c_s: Γ ∝ (1+c_s^2)^2 / c_s^3, leading to a sharp enhancement for c_s ≪ 1. An additional parameter Z_a, related to the kinetic mixing operator, induces correlated shifts in the physical mass and coupling constants of the mediator. Analysis using numerical simulations (MadGraph_aMC@NLO and MadAnalysis5) shows that for mediator masses near the top-quark pair threshold (around 365 GeV), even small deviations of Z_a from unity or a decrease in c_s radically alter the transverse momentum distribution and the resonance shape. For instance, with c_s^2 = 0.001 and Z_a = 0.4, the resonance broadens and the peak shifts, markedly different from standard expectations for narrow resonances.

Implications

The results mean that collider experiments can directly test the microphysics of dark energy, including its sound speed—a parameter fundamentally inaccessible to traditional cosmology. This blurs the boundary between laboratory high-energy physics and cosmology, offering a concrete path to test theories beyond ΛCDM. In particular, measurements of widths and branching ratios of resonances similar to axion-like particles can distinguish canonical quintessence scenarios from more exotic k-essence models, even if they yield the same equation of state parameter w.

Future development

Prospects include studying ultraviolet completions of the theory, accounting for screening effects on cosmological scales, and extending to other classes of effective field theories of dark energy. Of particular interest is the influence of the same interactions on phase transitions in the early Universe and accompanying gravitational-wave signals, which would close the collider–cosmology loop. Planned experiments at the High-Luminosity Large Hadron Collider could impose the first constraints on the sound speed c_s by using precision measurements of new resonance properties.

Impact

The work impacts collider physics, cosmology, and astrophysics by offering a unified language to describe dark sector interactions. It motivates a revision of new physics search strategies at the LHC, taking into account signatures sensitive to the sound speed.

Next steps

Immediate next steps include full accounting for initial-state radiation and hadronization effects in simulations, as well as the development of specialized triggers and analysis methods to isolate distorted resonance shapes against the Standard Model background.

Key open problems

The research directly tackles the unsolved problem of the physical nature of dark energy and proposes a new approach to the question of connection between the micro- and macro-worlds. It also touches on the hierarchy problem, as it requires explaining the smallness of the observed dark energy density within field theory.

🎯 The sound speed of dark energy could be much smaller than the speed of light: theoretically, it can drop to 10⁻⁴ c, at which point dark energy would start clumping like ordinary matter, forming 'sound shadows' in the cosmos.

🎬 The idea of instrumentally probing dark energy echoes Liu Cixin's novel 'The Three-Body Problem,' where a supercivilization manipulates physical laws through hidden dimensions—similar to how a collider uncovers the microphysics of cosmic acceleration here.

Key numbers

  • Typical pseudoscalar mediator mass: ~365 GeV (near the top-antitop threshold)
  • Proton collision energy at the LHC (Run 3): 13.6 TeV
  • Dark energy equation of state w: ~ -1 (according to Planck data)
  • Sound speed c_s^2: experimentally unconstrained, can be ≪ 1
  • Derivative coupling constant C_5/Λ_5: 10⁻⁴ GeV⁻¹ (typical working value)
