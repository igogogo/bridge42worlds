The prototype LST-1 telescope has successfully tracked the pulsation of gamma rays from distant space beacons — blazars.

The Universe’s beacons — blazars — flicker in gamma rays, powered by supermassive black holes. The LST-1 telescope has for the first time traced in detail how this pulse changes. Such observations bring us closer to solving nature’s ultimate accelerator.

🎯 Observing blazars lets us peer billions of years into the past: light from some of them has been traveling to Earth almost since the universe was born.
