Quantum noise doesn't hinder, it helps create thermal states — just like shaking speeds up dissolving.

Quantum noise is usually seen as the enemy of precise calculations. But it turns out it can accelerate the creation of thermal states, just as shaking speeds up the dissolving of sugar. Instead of a nuisance, noise becomes a tool — bringing the practical benefits of quantum computers closer.

🎯 In some quantum models, without noise, the system freezes forever in its initial state — adding noise literally "turns on" its evolution.
