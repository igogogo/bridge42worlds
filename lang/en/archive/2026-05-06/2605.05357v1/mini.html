The tiny density of dark energy — 10⁻¹²⁰ — has teased physicists for decades with its unnatural smallness. Now it turns out: it arises on its own, like a crispy waffle baked between hot plates of fluxes. Analysis of hundreds of millions of extra-dimensional geometries has shown: our Universe is not a strange anomaly, but a typical cell in the string landscape. And somewhere in the multiverse, the next giant leap may already be ripening.
