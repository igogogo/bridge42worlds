Waffle Structure of the Vacuum: From String Fluxes to Dark Energy ✦

Original: "Small Vacuum Energy and Tunneling in a Modified Bousso-Polchinski Model"
· James Halverson, Justin Khoury, Cody Long
arXiv:2605.05357v1 · 2026-05-06 · CC BY 4.0 · ⏱ 1 min · HEP Theory Cosmology General Relativity
A new model shows that the tiny density of dark energy naturally arises from the geometry of extra dimensions, like a crisp waffle baked between hot plates of fluxes.

The tiny density of dark energy — 10⁻¹²⁰ — has teased physicists for decades with its unnatural smallness. Now it turns out: it arises on its own, like a crispy waffle baked between hot plates of fluxes. Analysis of hundreds of millions of extra-dimensional geometries has shown: our Universe is not a strange anomaly, but a typical cell in the string landscape. And somewhere in the multiverse, the next giant leap may already be ripening.
