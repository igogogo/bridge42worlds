The Neupert Effect in the Corona of an Active Galactic Nucleus: First Direct Detection of Magnetic Reconnection ✦

Original: "Observation of A Solar Like Magnetic Reconnection Event in an AGN Corona with XRISM"
· Gal Vardi, Ehud Behar, Liyi Gu, Jelle Kaastra, Matteo Guainazzi, Missagh Mehdipour, Keigo Fukumura, Jon Miller, Ari Laor, Erin Kara, Megan E. Eckart, Misaki Mizumoto, Christos Panagiotou, Chen Li, Ogawa Shoji, Matilde Signorini, Keqin Zhao
arXiv:2606.25636 · 2026-06-24 · CC BY 4.0 · 4 min
High Energy Stellar
A flare in the nucleus of galaxy NGC 3783 demonstrated the Neupert effect, for the first time directly linking heating of the active nucleus corona with magnetic reconnection.
Abstract

We present the first observational evidence of a magnetic reconnection flare in the corona of an active galactic nucleus. Based on simultaneous observations of NGC 3783 by XRISM/Xtend and XMM-Newton/EPIC-PN, an X-ray flare with different temporal evolution in the soft (<2 keV) and hard (>2 keV) bands and a registered ultra-fast outflow (UFO) was detected. The flare exhibits the Neupert effect: the hard emission light curve correlates with the time derivative of the soft emission light curve, indicating that it is powered by magnetic reconnection — similar to solar flares, where coronal mass ejections play the role of UFOs. An upper limit on the height of the magnetic loop is 30 gravitational radii. From total energy considerations, the annihilated magnetic field strength is estimated as B > 1.3×10⁴ G, and from dynamical considerations, B ≈ 500 G at the reconnection time. This is the first direct confirmation of magnetic reconnection as a heating source for AGN coronae.

Context

The coronae of active galactic nuclei and stars share common features: they are orders of magnitude hotter than the underlying photosphere and accretion disk and exhibit rapid variability. The solar corona is heated by magnetic reconnection, and it has long been hypothesized that a similar mechanism should operate in accretion disks around supermassive black holes; however, direct observational evidence was lacking. Magnetic reconnection in disks is a key process capable of explaining heating, launching jets and outflows, and even facilitating black hole growth. Therefore, obtaining its observational confirmation is a fundamental step in understanding accretion physics.

Methods

Data from a ten-day campaign in July 2024 involving the XRISM, XMM-Newton, NuSTAR, Swift, and other satellites were used. Light curves were extracted in the soft (0.4–2.0 keV) and hard (7.2–12.0 keV) X-ray bands using the XRISM/Xtend and XMM-Newton/EPIC-PN cameras; cross-calibration was performed to exclude instrumental effects, and iron lines (6.0–7.2 keV) were excluded from the hard band. Properties of the ultra-fast outflow (UFO) were extracted from high-resolution X-ray spectra. The optimal energy boundaries for the Neupert effect were determined by analyzing the ratio of light curves in various slices. The significance of the correlation was assessed with a t-test corrected for time series autocorrelation. Spatial scales were calculated from time lags assuming particle motion at sub-light speed.

Results

A single flare (No. 2) was detected, in which hard X-rays preceded soft by about 30 thousand seconds, and the correlation between the hard light curve and the derivative of the soft one is statistically significant (p = 0.002). Other flares during the observation period did not show the effect. The optimal energy boundaries—1.2–2.0 keV (soft) and 7.2–12.0 keV (hard)—indicate the characteristic energies of non-thermal electrons and heated plasma, respectively. The maximum lag gives an upper limit on the height of the magnetic loop of 30 gravitational radii (about 1.2×10^14 cm). An ultra-fast outflow (UFO) with a velocity of 0.2c was observed simultaneously with the flare. From the kinetic energy of the outflow, using the law of conservation of energy, a lower limit on the magnetic field strength B > 1.3×10^4 G (with 10% efficiency—up to 4×10^4 G) was obtained. From the equation of motion based on the law of conservation of momentum, the instantaneous field during reconnection is found to be about 500 G. The ultraviolet light curve lags by ~60 ks, corresponding to a distance to the illuminated accretion disk of ~400 gravitational radii.

Implications

The discovery proves that heating of AGN coronae and the solar corona has a common nature—magnetic reconnection. It links together X-ray flares, the launch of ultra-fast outflows, and particle acceleration, showing that outflows are extragalactic analogs of coronal mass ejections. For the first time, a direct method for measuring magnetic fields near the event horizon of a black hole becomes available. As demonstrated by Rashid Sunyaev's work on Comptonization, X-ray spectroscopy probes the inner regions of the accretion flow, and the Neupert effect becomes an additional diagnostic tool. The result also strengthens the hypothesis that jets may be powered by energy released during reconnection.

Future development

Future X-ray missions (Athena, LEM) and systematic campaigns with XRISM will allow us to gather statistics on the Neupert effect in AGN and study its dependence on black hole parameters. Simultaneous radio- and X-ray observations may reveal a correlation similar to solar radio bursts, uncovering the distribution of non-thermal particles. Numerical modeling incorporating observational constraints will refine the three-dimensional structure of magnetic fields near black holes. Theories developed by Kip Thorne predict complex field dynamics in the ergosphere, which can be tested by such flares.

Impact

The result will impact accretion theory, high-energy astrophysics, plasma physics, and the interpretation of multiwavelength variability in active nuclei. It is also important for cosmology when assessing feedback from AGN and the growth of supermassive black holes.

Next steps

Conduct a systematic search for the Neupert effect in NICER archives and future XRISM observations of other AGN. Refine particle acceleration at the reconnection site using polarimetric measurements with IXPE.

Key open problems

The research is linked to the problem of heating accretion disk coronae and particle acceleration in astrophysical plasma. It touches on the extraction of rotational energy from black holes: the process predicted by Roger Penrose may use magnetic fields to transfer energy to matter and radiation. Strong fields (up to 10^4 G) force us to reconsider the mechanisms of jet collimation and outflow launching—one of the key mysteries of relativistic astrophysics.

🎯 The Neupert effect was discovered in 1968 on the Sun and was long considered an exclusively solar phenomenon until it was detected on cool stars. Now it has been found in the vicinity of a supermassive black hole, confirming the universality of magnetic reconnection in cosmic plasma.
