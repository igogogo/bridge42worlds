Original: "Resonant false vacuum decay in two dimensions on a 4000-qubit quantum annealer"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Metastable states decay through the nucleation and growth of competing regions—think of how a supercooled liquid suddenly crystallizes. In a new study, researchers used a quantum annealer with over 4,000 qubits to simulate a false vacuum in a two-dimensional quantum Ising model. They found a regime where the growth of true vacuum bubbles outruns their appearance by orders of magnitude, and achieved nearly ballistic expansion when tuning a resonant magnetic field. The Kardar–Parisi–Zhang universality, usually describing the growth of bacterial colonies or forest fires, now shows up in quantum decay as well. This experiment opens the door to laboratory studies of nonequilibrium processes important for cosmology.
