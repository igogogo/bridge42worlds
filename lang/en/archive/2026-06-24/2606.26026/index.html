Original: "Can giant impacts be directly detected in other star systems?"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Planetary collisions — giant impacts — are a crucial stage in the formation of planets and moons. By modeling them on computers, scientists have shown: after an impact, the object in optical and near-infrared first brightens sharply, then fades over a period from a day to several years. The luminosity can reach a tenth of the Sun's. This means that sky surveys Gaia and LSST will be able to directly detect such catastrophes and estimate their frequency in the galaxy.
