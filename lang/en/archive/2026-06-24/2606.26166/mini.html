Brane Shadows: Searching for Hidden Dimensions on the Canvas of the Universe ✦

Original: "Images of Braneworld black holes with radiatively inefficient accretion flows"
· Chengjia Chen, Yin Hao, Zelin Zhang, Qiyuan Pan, Jiliang Jing
arXiv:2606.26166 · 2026-06-24 · CC BY · 1 min · General Relativity High Energy HEP Theory
Simulations of hot plasma around brane black holes uncover nearly imperceptible shadow distortions—0.1%, but next-generation telescopes can catch the whisper of extra dimensions.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Black holes are not abysses, but windows. In a multidimensional universe, they can pull back the veil on hidden layers of reality. Picture a canvas: our 3D world is only its surface, and only gravity is not confined to it. Simulations show that the shadow of M87* shimmers by 0.1%—an echo of brane tension. Future telescopes like BHEX promise to catch this whisper, and we may, for the first time, touch what lies beyond the edge.
