Quantum Tide from the Future: Accelerating the Universe Without Dark Energy ✦

Original: "Teleocosmology and quantum post-selection"
· Paul C. W. Davies, João Magueijo
arXiv:2606.02514v1 · 2026-06-01 · CC BY 4.0 · ⏱ 1 min · General Relativity Cosmology
Quantum post-selection explains cosmic acceleration without invoking new fields or vacuum energy.

Is the Universe accelerating due to dark energy? A new approach says: no, it's being pulled by a quantum condition from the future. Like the final chord of a symphony making the orchestra play faster to reach the last note. A Chern-Simons soliton, hidden in the distant future, warps the expansion history. Perhaps we live inside a huge quantum post-selection. Traces of this surge can be sought in the cosmic microwave background. And if we find them, we'll have to admit: the future is more real than the past.

🎯 In the lab, post-selection allows measuring an electron spin of 100 or detecting a particle before it was emitted. Cosmological acceleration is the gravitational echo of this procedure: the final wave function selects histories where we speed up, as if the future dictates the present.

\Psi(b,T)=\int d\phi\, A(\phi) e^{i(P(b;\phi,m)-\phi T)}
