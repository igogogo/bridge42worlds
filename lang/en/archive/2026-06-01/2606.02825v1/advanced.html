High-Performance Combining Panel for Broadband Radio Interferometers

Original: "A High-Bandwidth Backplane for Wideband Radio Interferometers and Integration with the CHORD Telescope Correlators"
A new combining panel architecture delivers stable synchronization and high throughput for the next generation of radio interferometers.

Context

Modern radio interferometers like CHORD process tens of terabits of data per second, requiring high phase coherence. The proposed combining panel provides a scalable solution with precise synchronization, overcoming the limitations of commercial alternatives.

Methods

The authors designed a passive combining panel with 25 Gbps lines using low-cost substrate and solderless connectors. Timing is distributed via LVDS with a 10 MHz reference signal and IRIG-B. BER tests, delay measurements via GCC-PHAT, and thermal tests under full crate load were conducted.

Results

Error-free transmission of over 10 Tbit per channel was achieved. Inter-board delays are stable with 200–500 fs jitter, and temperature drift is predictable. A full crate consumes 1152 W, with FPGAs not exceeding 52°C. The system is ready for integration with CHORD.

Implications

The architecture allows scaling without firmware changes, critical for large arrays like SKA. It is also applicable in 21-cm cosmology for studying dark matter and the expansion of the Universe.

Future development

Integration with CHORD is planned by the end of 2026, along with adaptation for other instruments: TES/MKID spectrometers and RFI monitors.

Impact

Radio astronomy, cosmology, signal processing.

Next steps

Conduct extended environmental tests and on-sky observations with CHORD.

Key open problems

Solves the synchronization and throughput problem for large interferometers needed to map the large-scale structure of the Universe.
