Mass of the Neutron Star in 4U 1820–30: A Fresh Perspective

Original: "The mass of the neutron star in 4U 1820-30 revisited"
Analysis of gravitational redshift, pulsations, and bursts reveals contradictions in neutron star mass estimates.

Neutron stars are among the densest objects in the universe, and precisely determining their mass and radius is critical for understanding the equation of state of superdense matter. The 4U 1820–30 system is a unique laboratory: it contains a neutron star and a light white dwarf with an orbital period of just 11 minutes, allowing multiple independent methods to probe the accreting object's properties.

🎯 The neutron star in 4U 1820–30 orbits the white dwarf every 11 minutes — one of the shortest orbital periods among known binary systems.
1+z = \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 R}\right)^{-1/2}
