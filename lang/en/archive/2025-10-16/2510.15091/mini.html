Original: "Unusual dependence on the angle of magnetic field for the spin Hall magnetoresistance of monodomain epitaxial BiFeO3 thin films"
In thin films, a strange shift reveals the hidden behavior of magnetic arrows.

You turn on a current in a thin magnetic film, and the resistance draws a map of spins. Usually the map is simple, but in this new material it's gone awry—as if a compass points away from north. Such anomalies promise a breakthrough in spintronics.
