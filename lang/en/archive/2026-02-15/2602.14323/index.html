Original: "Dirac Spin Liquid Candidate in a Rydberg Quantum Simulator"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Scientists on a quantum simulator of 114 atoms in a kagome lattice (a frustrated geometry that prevents spins from aligning harmoniously) gently prepared a disordered correlated state — a quantum spin liquid. The measured spin correlations matched well with the Dirac spin liquid model, and the entropy density turned out to be close to that observed in solid frustrated magnets at liquid nitrogen temperature. This is the first direct comparison of a 'liquid' state of atoms with theory and an important step toward understanding quantum disorder.
