Original: "Higher-order Symmetric Quantum Mpemba Effect in Fragmented Systems"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

The quantum Mpemba effect: the more symmetry is broken, the faster the recovery. The question: does it survive when the system fragments due to charge and dipole moment conservation? The authors used tensor networks and simulations and found that the effect doesn't vanish but splits: charge and dipole asymmetries disappear at different rates. Mechanism: frozen sectors preserve residual asymmetry, while active ones drive relaxation. Fragmentation turns the effect into a multi-layered process, like the fading of different instruments in an orchestra.
