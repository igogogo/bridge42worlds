A new method directly links the mass and radius of a neutron star to its internal structure—without any extra hypotheses.

How do you find out what’s inside a neutron star? Scientists have come up with a new way: look directly at its size and mass, instead of guessing about the matter’s properties. It’s like judging a ball’s bounciness by squeezing it, rather than poking inside.

🎯 Collisions of neutron stars are the only known places where precious metals like gold and platinum are born.
