The MAGIC telescopes caught a record-breaking gamma-ray burst from a black hole that lived 7 billion years ago.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

In December 2017, the MAGIC telescopes spotted a distant quasar flaring in gamma rays, becoming 50 times brighter than the Crab Nebula. The light traveled 7 billion years from a black hole shrouded in dust. Such flares are rare windows into an era when the universe was half its current age.

🎯 A single gamma-ray photon from this quasar carries energy comparable to a mosquito bite — but concentrated into a volume smaller than an atomic nucleus. If such a photon hit a person, it would knock out a whole cascade of particles.
