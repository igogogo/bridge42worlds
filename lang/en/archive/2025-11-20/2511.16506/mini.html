Neutrinos in a supernova team up, which speeds up their flavor changes and could alter the story of a star’s explosion.

Every second, trillions of neutrinos zip through us. But in the heart of a supernova, these ghost particles become sociable: they swap properties and reach equilibrium faster. New modeling opens the door to unlocking the secrets of stellar explosions.

🎯 From supernova 1987A, Earth’s detectors caught just 25 neutrinos—but that was enough to confirm the theory of stellar collapse.
