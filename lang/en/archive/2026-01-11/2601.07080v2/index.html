Abstract

Lava worlds are rocky planets with a dayside melted by stellar heat. It turns out that even a slightly eccentric orbit (with an eccentricity of a few percent) generates powerful tides that create a deep magma ocean. Tidal waves interfere, producing chaotically wandering hot spots and irregular brightness from the planet. This explains the strange variability of some exoplanets and is reminiscent of ocean tides on Earth, only with liquid rock instead of water.
