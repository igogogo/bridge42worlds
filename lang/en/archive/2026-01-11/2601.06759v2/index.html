Abstract

NASA's SPHEREx space observatory has re-observed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS after its perihelion passage. The object's activity surged dramatically: water ice vaporized, and water gas emissions became 40 times brighter. Spectra revealed new lines from cyanide and organics, while a carbon dioxide cloud stretched over 3 arcminutes. It's like a layered pie: the top water layers vanished, uncovering a carbon-rich filling from another star system.
