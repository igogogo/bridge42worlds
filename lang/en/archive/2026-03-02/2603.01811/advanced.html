Abstract

Superpositions of mass distributions can entangle with spacetime geometry. It is shown that macroscopic mass distributions face a natural resistance to forming such superpositions. The macroscopic superposition is modeled as a Schrödinger's cat state. The resistance appears as a dip in the total energy of this state depending on the distance between the superposition branches. This energy dip creates a counteracting force that hinders the formation of the superposition. A generalization of this phenomenon related to the measurement problem is discussed.
