Original: "How Common Are Common Envelopes? Quantifying Their Role in Forming Gravitational-Wave Sources"
· Floor S. Broekgaarden, Ana Lam, Sasha Levina, Jakub Klencki, Kyle A. Rocha, Lieke van Son, Steffani M. Grondin, Monica Gallegos-Garcia, Brian D. Metzger, Enrico Ramirez-Ruiz, Angela Twum, Melanie Santiago, Julia Haynes, Tyler B. Smith, Amedeo Romagnolo, Edo Berger, Lucas M. de Sá

You can’t reconstruct their history from the number of black hole collisions: different scenarios lead to the same dish.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Black holes can inspiral without the fiery cocoon of a common envelope — new data cast doubt on a key stage of binary star evolution. Unraveling their history will require not just counting mergers, but detailed study of each event.

🎯 The common gas cocoon phase lasts from hundreds to tens of thousands of years — on a universal scale, it’s a blink: if you compress the Universe’s entire history into one year, it would take less than a second.

🎬 In science fiction, binary stars are a backdrop for adventures, but their true final act — collision and merger into a single black hole — is far more dramatic than fiction.
