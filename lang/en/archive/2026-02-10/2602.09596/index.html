Original: "Device-independent quantum key distribution over 100 km with single atoms"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Device-independent quantum key distribution (DI-QKD) has been realized for the first time between two atomic nodes over a 100-kilometer optical fiber. To boost the speed, they employed single-photon interference, quantum frequency conversion to combat losses, and Rydberg emission that suppresses atomic recoil. High-fidelity entanglement and positive key rates were achieved over distances up to 100 km; at 11 km over 624 hours, 1.2 million Bell pairs were accumulated, yielding a final secure key rate of 0.112 bits per event against general attacks. The result blurs the line between demonstration quantum networks and real-world applications.
