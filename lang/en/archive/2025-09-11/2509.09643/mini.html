Original: "Ultralight Boson Ionization from Comparable-Mass Binary Black Holes"
Two swirling stars gather dark matter into a giant cloud that resembles a molecule.

Two stars dance, and an invisible giant molecule of dark matter is born between them. When their orbits wobble, this cosmic creation sheds particles, just as a shaken atom loses electrons. This process could leave a unique imprint in the ripples of spacetime—a key to unlocking the mystery of dark matter.

🎯 The radius of such a 'gravitational molecule' can exceed the distance from the Sun to Pluto, even though the very concept of a radius comes from the microworld.
