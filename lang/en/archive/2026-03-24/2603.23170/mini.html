Original: "Tentative Detection of the Glycine Isomer Glycolamide in Hot Molecular Core"
In the fire of a newborn star, they found a molecule—an 'anagram' of an amino acid—a surviving draft of life.

In the very forge of a star being born, astronomers spotted the glycolamide molecule — a relative of an amino acid. It turns out, complex organics don't just survive in the fire of stellar nurseries, but also retain the 'recipe proportions' from the cold cloud. Thus, life's chemistry travels through cosmic storms.

🎯 Glycolamide is an isomer of glycine: swap two atoms, and you have the amino acid that makes up our proteins. Nature seems to have mixed up just one bond, creating a molecular draft.

🎬 Fred Hoyle, mentioned above, in his novel 'The Black Cloud' described an intelligent interstellar cloud. Now, in real clouds, organics have been found that withstand stellar heat and retain their molecular signature.
