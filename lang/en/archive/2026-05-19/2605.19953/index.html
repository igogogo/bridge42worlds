Abstract

Studying ancient light — the cosmic microwave background (CMB) — scientists searched for imprints of the universe's geometry before inflation. Analyzing data from the Planck satellite, they found a possible violation of spatial parity (asymmetry) in large-scale correlations, which could point to the existence of six extra dimensions curled up even before the Big Bang. It's like discovering a hidden pattern on an antique world map. Future measurements of B-mode polarization will test this hypothesis with unprecedented precision.
