Abstract

Fresh DESI observations hint that dark energy may be dynamic, recently dipping below the phantom divide (w < –1). Researchers have proposed a model in which fermionic dark matter and a tachyon field (with Born–Infeld dynamics) engage in a Yukawa interaction. This triggers a double phantom transition in the effective equation of state, while the field itself stays stable and obeys physical laws. A joint analysis of DESI, Planck, and supernova data supports this scenario and, under natural assumptions, points to ultralight dark matter with a mass of around 0.002 eV — a tantalizing prospect for future tests.
