Fast Quantum Links: Delivered Before They Melt ⚡ экспресс

Original: "Piecemaker: a resource-efficient entanglement distribution protocol"
· Luise Prielinger, Kenneth Goodenough, Guus Avis, Stefan Krastanov, Don Towsley, Gayane Vardoyan
arXiv:2508.14737 · 2025-08-20 · CC BY 4.0 · ⏱ 1 min · Quantum Physics
A new protocol instantly assembles quantum pairs, like a courier delivering ice cream in portions, and cuts noise by 45%.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Quantum connections are like ice cream — they melt from the heat of noise. Imagine a courier who doesn’t wait for the full order, but immediately delivers portions, mixing them into a perfect dish. This trick reduces spoilage by 45% and makes multipartite quantum networks more real.

🎯 Today’s record entangled ensemble is 20 qubits. The new method targets 50+, reducing melting and bringing the quantum internet closer.
