Abstract

Theoretical research shows that Bose–Einstein condensates (quantum states where many particles merge into a single wave) arise in ultra-strong gravitational fields, such as in neutron stars. The critical condensation temperature is not just a constant—it changes with the curvature of spacetime, which is important for understanding the early universe. If dark matter consists of axions, it forms a cosmic condensate with a coherence length on the order of light-days, naturally explaining the observed galaxy profiles. Quantum order might be everywhere in the universe.
