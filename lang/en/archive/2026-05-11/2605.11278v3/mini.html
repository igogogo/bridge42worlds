Cosmic Hydrogen Catches Gravitational Waves ⚡ экспресс

Original: "Absorption of Gravitons and Photon Luminosities of Interstellar/Intergalactic Hydrogen Atoms"
· George Savvidy, Pavlos Savvidis
arXiv:2605.11278v3 · 2026-05-11 · CC BY · ⏱ 1 min · HEP Phenomenology Cosmology Galaxies General Relativity
Hydrogen atoms in interstellar space can serve as natural receivers of gravitational waves.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Stars emit invisible gravitational waves, and their frequencies precisely match the 'favorite' frequencies of hydrogen atoms. Vast clouds of this gas in space could serve as passive gravitational receivers. By decoding their glow, we could gauge the strength of gravitational shocks in different corners of the Universe and build a gravitational map.

🎯 Gravitons twist spacetime twice as much as photons, which is why gravitational waves interact so weakly with matter.

🎬 In the film 'Interstellar', the characters communicate via gravity. The new method shows that we can pick up the gravitational whispers of distant stars using ordinary hydrogen.
