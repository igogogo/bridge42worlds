Original: "Born in the Dark: The Catastrophic Collapse of Fuzzy Dark Matter Solitons as the Origin of Little Red Dots"
The James Webb telescope found compact red objects—possibly growing galaxies hidden in cocoons of gas and exotic dark matter.

The James Webb telescope found mysterious “red dots”—distant objects glowing brightly in hydrogen lines but nearly invisible in X-rays. Perhaps this is the moment when an adolescent galaxy greedily swallows gas hidden behind a dense curtain. The answer may point to a new type of dark matter—ultralight wave-particles that create gravitational funnels.

🎯 To create such a curtain, more hydrogen falls into this tiny volume than exists in an entire small galaxy—this region is about the distance from the Sun to the nearest stars.

🎬 If light dark matter is real, space is permeated by a wave-like fog that shapes galaxy growth—an idea Ken Liu toyed with in his story “Waves,” where the structure of the Universe hinges on quantum fluctuations.
