Mysterious radio pulsar beats every 36 minutes express

Original: "Discovery of a 36-minute long-period transient ASKAP J142431.2-612611"
· Joshua Pritchard, Tara Murphy, Dougal Dobie, Emil Lenc, Akash Anumarlapudi, Manisha Caleb, Sophia Grainger, Natasha Hurley-Walker, David L. Kaplan, Samuel J. McSweeney, Jackson Mitchell-Bolton, Kovi Rose, Rahul Sengar, Ziteng Wang, Jayde Willingham, Andrew Zic
arXiv:2603.07857 · 2026-03-09 · CC BY · 1 min · High Energy
Astronomers detected a cosmic radio beacon that signaled every 36 minutes — but only for eight days.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Radio waves from space pulsed every 36 minutes for just a week, then vanished. Their beam was one hundred percent polarized and rotated like light in a crystal. Such invisible beacons might fill the entire Galaxy.

🎯 A 36-minute period is so vast that an ordinary pulsar would need longer than the age of the Universe to slow down that much.
