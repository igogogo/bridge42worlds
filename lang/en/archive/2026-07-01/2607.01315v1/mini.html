Cosmic Carving: Birth of a Bloated Neptune from a Jupiter

Original: "Discovery of an Inflated Hot Neptune and Its Formation from Jovian Mass Loss"
· Grant C. Weldon, Samuel W. Yee, Bradley M. S. Hansen, Smadar Naoz, Joel D. Hartman, Joshua N. Winn, R. Paul Butler, Jeffrey D. Crane, Phil Evans, Tianjun Gan, Steve B. Howell, Michelle Kunimoto, David Osip, David Rapetti, Stephen A. Shectman, Keivan G. Stassun, Johanna K. Teske, Roberto Zambelli, George Zhou, Carl Ziegler
arXiv:2607.01315v1 · 2026-07-01 · CC BY · ⏱ 1 min · Exoplanets
TOI-2195 A b, a bloated hot Neptune on a polar orbit, hints at an evolutionary path from cold Jupiters to Neptunes through mass loss and tidal heating.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

A planet that was once a cold Jupiter went through a gravitational meat grinder and lost almost everything except a bloated hydrogen envelope and a rocky heart. Now, like a balloon, it circles its star on a polar orbit. Perhaps half of all hot Neptunes are 'gutted' Jupiters. Peering into their atmospheres means reading the history of violence that shaped worlds.

🎯 TOI-2195 A b's radius is almost equal to Jupiter's, while its mass is only 1.5 times that of Neptune. If placed in water, it would float like a giant balloon.

r_t = R_p \left(\frac{M_\star + m_p}{m_p}\right)^{1/3}
