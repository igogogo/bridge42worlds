Abstract

We examine a stationary BTZ spacetime in the context of modified gravity featuring an inverse Ricci tensor (where the function f depends on the Ricci scalar, anti-curvature, and the square of the anti-tensor). It is demonstrated that BTZ is an exact solution in this theory, with the cosmological constant altered by coupling constants. f(R) gravity is also analyzed. A complete integration of the geodesic equations for BTZ in inverse-Ricci gravity is carried out; the solutions are expressed through elementary functions. Geodesics are classified for three scenarios: (i) M≠0, J≠0; (ii) massless BTZ (M=0, J=0); (iii) AdS₃ type (M=-1, J=0). The findings are compared with general relativity predictions.
