Original: "An unidentified absorption feature at 5.11 $$\mu$$m on the surface of Titan and Pluto from JWST spectroscopy"
The James Webb Telescope has discovered an unexplained chemical trace on Titan and Pluto.

On Titan, hidden by a dense orange haze, the James Webb Telescope spotted a mysterious dark speck in infrared light. It resembles no known ice and may point to an unknown substance on the surface. A similar trace was also found on Pluto. Whose chemical signature it is remains a mystery.

🎯 Absorbing just 6% is like noticing the faintest dimming from the clearest sunglasses. James Webb caught it from a billion and a half kilometers away.

🎬 In Arthur C. Clarke's novel 'Imperial Earth', Titan supplies humanity with methane. Today, real science uncovers mysterious chemical fingerprints on it, preserving a chronicle of planet birth.
