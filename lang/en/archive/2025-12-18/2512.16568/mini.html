A Middleweight Black Hole Exposed by a Torn Star ⚡ экспресс

Original: "A Tidal Disruption Event from an Intermediate-mass Black Hole Revealed by Comprehensive Multi-wavelength Observations"
· Jialai Wang, Mengqiu Huang, Yongquan Xue, Ning Jiang, Shifeng Huang, Yibo Wang, Jiazheng Zhu, Shifu Zhu, Lixin Dai, Chichuan Jin, Bin Luo, Xinwen Shu, Mouyuan Sun, Tinggui Wang, Fan Zou
arXiv:2512.16568 · 2025-12-18 · CC BY · ⏱ 1 min · High Energy Galaxies
Astronomers caught a prolonged flare from a star torn apart by a medium-sized black hole in a dwarf galaxy.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

A star got too close to an invisible black hole in a dwarf galaxy and was torn apart. The flare lasted for years and pointed to the "missing link"—a medium-sized black hole. This will help us understand how supermassive monsters grow.

🎯 Intermediate-mass black holes could have been the "seeds" from which supermassive black holes at the centers of galaxies grew.

🎬 In the movie "Interstellar," the characters dive into a wormhole, but if their ship had gotten too close to a black hole, it would have met the same fate: tidal forces would have stretched and torn it into cosmic spaghetti.
