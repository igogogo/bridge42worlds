An electron rides the waves of emptiness like a surfer on the ocean: it’s all about matching rhythms.

Amazing but true: classical physics with zero-point radiation produces 'quantum' electron orbits. Emptiness isn’t empty—it hums with electromagnetic noise that keeps the atom from collapsing. This could reshape our understanding of the border between classical and quantum physics.

🎯 Without this invisible surf, all electrons in the universe would crash into nuclei in billionths of a second—stars would never ignite, and we simply wouldn’t exist.
