Abstract

The Lorentzian type IIB matrix model is a promising candidate for a nonperturbative formulation of superstring theory. The eigenvalue distribution of bosonic matrices A_μ (μ=0,…,9) of size N×N generates spacetime, dynamically determined in the large-N limit. Numerical simulations using the complex Langevin method with matrix sizes up to 128 were performed, overcoming the sign problem. To suppress singular drift caused by the Pfaffian after integrating out fermionic matrices, the model is deformed in analogy to the supersymmetric deformation used to define the 'polarized type IIB model' in the Euclidean case. A phase was discovered in which an expanding (3+1)-dimensional spacetime with smooth and real coordinates emerges.
