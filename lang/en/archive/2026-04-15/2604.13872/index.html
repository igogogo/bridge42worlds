Original: "Experimental realisation of topological spin textures in a Penning trap"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Researchers have for the first time deterministically created topological spin textures—skyrmions (vortex-like magnetic structures)—in a two-dimensional crystal of over 150 trapped ions. Using globally applied spin-dependent forces, they shaped skyrmion configurations and reconstructed the full spin vector field with single-ion resolution. The key parameter—the winding number—was 0.99±0.02, and the local reproduction fidelity reached 87%. They also demonstrated control of individual ions to create domain walls. This platform paves the way for exploring non-equilibrium dynamics in long-range interacting systems, much like a swirling dance of particles coalescing into a stable vortex.
