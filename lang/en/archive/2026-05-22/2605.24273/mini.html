Original: "Plume Segmentation from MethaneSAT with Cross-Sensor Transfer Learning and Physics-Informed Postprocessing"
The bloodhound-like MethaneSAT satellite is trained to sniff out invisible methane and find leaks missed by humans.

The MethaneSAT satellite looks at Earth and sees an invisible gas. The smart program learns to spot methane clouds even where humans missed them. It turns out many 'errors' the computer makes are real leaks that nobody noticed. Now we can find and fix gas leaks faster than ever before.

🎯 Over 20 years, methane traps 80 times more heat than carbon dioxide.

🎬 Someday, such orbital bloodhounds will sniff the atmospheres of exoplanets in search of life.
