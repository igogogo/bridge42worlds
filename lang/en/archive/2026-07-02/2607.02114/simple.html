Left- and Right-Handed Magnetic Waves ✦

Original: "Plaid-Like Spin Splitting and Chirality of Magnon Bands in Antiferromagnetic MnTe$$_2$$"
· Dirk Wulferding, Daehyeon An, Jiwon Choi, Dongmin Mun, Youngsu Choi, Sivasakthi Kuppusamy, Sritharan Krishnamoorthi, Raman Sankar, Myung Joon Han, Se Kwon Kim, Kwang-Yong Choi
arXiv:2607.02114 · 2026-07-02 · CC BY 4.0 · 1 min · Strongly Correlated Materials
In a MnTe2 crystal, magnetic waves twist like a corkscrew thread, so their movement depends on the direction of rotation.
Abstract

Some magnets look ordinary on the outside, but inside, their spins form a pattern like a chessboard. Scientists used light to see that magnetic waves in such a material twist like a corkscrew—left or right depending on the direction. This discovery could lead to ultra-fast electronics that run on twisted magnetic signals. Imagine a computer without heating, where information is carried by spin vortices.

Links in the knowledge graph 1

An ordinary screw only goes into wood if you turn it the right way. A similar rule applies in a crystal of MnTe2 — a close relative of “fool’s gold.” It’s here that physicists discovered magnetic waves can be twisted left or right, and their movement depends on the direction of rotation.

By scanning the crystal with a laser (spectroscopy) and measuring the twist of reflected light (polarimetry), scientists confirmed with computer simulations that magnon waves split into two types. The effect arises from a superposition of vibrational states. In the future, such “unidirectional” waves will be useful for quantum computing: they can transmit signals without interference. At low temperatures, where quantum laws of heat come into play, magnons behave even more strangely, and with quantum optics they can be controlled with high precision. The groundwork for this research was laid by Werner Heisenberg and Lev Landau.

🎯 MnTe2 has the same crystal lattice as pyrite — “fool’s gold.” But unlike the latter, it doesn’t fool physicists; instead, it opens up new horizons.
