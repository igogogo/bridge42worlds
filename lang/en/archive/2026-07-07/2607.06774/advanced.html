Phantom Dark Energy from Z_N Axion: A Natural Mechanism Without Ghosts ✦

Original: "Natural Phantom Dark Energy from a $$\mathbb{Z}_N$$--Axion"
· Cédric Delaunay, Admir Greljo
arXiv:2607.06774 · 2026-07-07 · CC BY 4.0 · 4 min · HEP Phenomenology Cosmology
Cosmological evolution of an axion locked by the density of dark pions leads to an effective equation of state with w<-1 without violating physical principles.
Abstract

A phantom dark energy model: an axion in N copies of a two-flavor dark QCD with a Z_N permutation symmetry. The Z_N symmetry exponentially suppresses the axion potential down to the dark energy scale. Breaking Z_N when one sector reheats restores the axion's periodicity, while dark pions account for the relic abundance of dark matter. Initially, a pion gas keeps the axion away from its vacuum; as the universe expands, it dissipates, the axion rolls, creating effective phantom behavior (w < –1) without fine-tuning. The model parameters simultaneously yield the correct relic density of dark matter and the dark energy magnitude, satisfy observational constraints, and qualitatively reproduce DESI's preference for an evolving equation of state.

Context

The cosmological constant in the ΛCDM framework remains a theoretical puzzle due to the scale problem. Recent measurements of baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO) by the DESI collaboration have shown a preference for evolving dark energy with a possible phantom divide crossing (w<-1). Such behavior is extremely difficult to realize in canonical general relativity, described by Einstein's equations and expanding according to Hubble's law with dynamics governed by Friedmann equations, without violating the null energy condition. This motivates the search for nontrivial physics of interacting dark sectors. Models with ultralight scalar fields like the axion, whose idea traces back to the work of Helen Quinn, offer a technically natural way out: shift symmetry protects the field mass, and coupling to dark matter can mimic phantom dynamics without introducing ghosts.

Methods

We construct an effective field theory with a single axion coupled to N identical copies of a dark QCD (gauge group SU(3) with two quark flavors). The Z_N exchange symmetry among sectors leads to an exponential suppression of the axion vacuum potential ∼ z_{ud}^N, where z_{ud} is the dark quark mass ratio, naturally generating a dark energy scale of meV^4. Introducing a reheaton field ϕ with selective coupling to one 'zero' dark sector after inflation reheats only that sector, creating a population of dark pions — candidates for dark matter. This same interaction softly breaks Z_N, inducing a leading vacuum term with period 2πf, necessary for late-time dynamics. Through numerical simulation of coupled cosmological background equations, we obtain the evolution of the axion field and the effective dark energy equation of state, including the backreaction of dark pion density via their field-dependent mass.

Results

It is shown that for a sufficiently large initial density n_π ∼ ϵ_b m_π f_π^2 (ϵ_b is the Z_N breaking parameter), the axion is trapped in a metastable state near φ=πf. As the Universe expands and the density dilutes (proportional to redshift z), the vacuum potential becomes dominant, and the field is released around z ≃ 0.17 for a benchmark parameter set: V_0=0.37 H_0^2 M_P^2, Λ_b=19 H_0^2 M_P^2, z_{ud}=0.05, f=0.065 M_P. The effective equation of state w_eff(z) exhibits a transition from w>-1 in the past to w<-1 at present, qualitatively agreeing with the DESI reconstruction based on cosmic microwave background and large-scale structure data. The model predicts a suppression of matter perturbation growth by a few percent relative to ΛCDM, testable via redshift-space distortions. On the (m_π, f_π) plane, a region is identified that jointly satisfies constraints from warm dark matter, self-interactions, nucleosynthesis, and chiral control, with the dark pion mass lying in the range 10-100 keV.

Implications

This work demonstrates that apparent phantom dark energy can arise from nonperturbative effects of a QCD-like dark sector without introducing fundamental scalar fields with negative kinetic energy. This opens a new class of microscopically motivated dynamical dark energy models where dark matter (discovered thanks to Vera Rubin) and dark energy (whose acceleration was found by Saul Perlmutter and others) interact through confinement physics. The connection of cosmological evolution to reheating physics and specific parameters makes the model falsifiable not only by astrophysical but also by collider experiments.

Future development

A natural continuation will be constructing specific ultraviolet completions within the Standard Model and its extensions, explaining the emergence of a large discrete Z_N symmetry. Analysis of the thermal history of the dark sector, including pion cannibalism effects, will allow tighter parameter constraints. An important step will be simulating nonlinear cosmological perturbations in this model to predict signatures in weak lensing and galaxy clusters.

Impact

The proposed mechanism connects observational cosmology with particle physics at confinement scales ∼10–100 MeV, potentially testable in dark photon search experiments. It also offers a new explanation for the coincidence between the dark energy scale and the present-day acceleration without fine-tuning.

Next steps

First, perform a full Bayesian analysis of joint BAO, supernova, cosmic microwave background, and weak lensing data to quantitatively assess the model's preference over ΛCDM. Second, investigate in detail the nonstandard thermal history of dark pions.

Key open problems

The model directly addresses the cosmological constant problem, replacing a static Λ with a dynamical mechanism. It also proposes a solution to the 'cosmic coincidence' and links the nature of dark matter to the reheating dynamics after the Big Bang.
