The James Webb telescope caught one planet in Kepler-51 running late, betraying an unseen fourth dancer.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Three puffy planets were dancing in perfect sync until one suddenly stumbled out of step. The gravity of an invisible fourth planet gave it away—proving that even well-studied systems can hide surprises. The James Webb Space Telescope helped expose this cosmic sleight of hand.

🎯 The Kepler-51 planets are so light, with densities under 0.1 g/cm³, they wouldn't sink even in fresh water—they'd bob on the waves like beach balls.
