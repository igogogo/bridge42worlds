Original: "A Fourth Planet in the Kepler-51 System Revealed by Transit Timing Variations"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

The sun-like star Kepler-51 hosts three transiting planets with periods from 45 to 130 days and radii 6–9 times that of Earth. Previous measurements of transit timing variations (small deviations from a strict schedule due to mutual gravitational pulls) gave extremely low densities for all three planets — less than 0.1 g/cm³. However, new observations by James Webb 10 years later showed that the transit time of the outermost planet Kepler-51d no longer matched predictions. This discrepancy was explained by adding a fourth, more distant planet — Kepler-51e — to the model. Its mass and orbital period are not yet precisely known, but all solutions found by brute-force search indicate that the inner planets remain very light (less than 10 Earth masses).
