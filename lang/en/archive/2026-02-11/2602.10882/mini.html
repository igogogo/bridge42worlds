A powerful laser makes a semiconductor emit squeezed and entangled light—ideal raw material for quantum technologies.

It turns out that an ordinary laser can turn a tiny crystal into a factory of squeezed and entangled light. This is a step toward quantum microchips that will transform computing and communication.

🎯 The highest-frequency harmonic flickers so fast that in one second it manages to blink more times than the universe has lived years.

🎬 In science fiction, entangled particles have long served as a bridge for instantaneous communication across galaxies—and this experiment takes a step toward such technology.
