Original: "Testing the problem of time with cold atoms"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

An experimental setup based on cold atoms was created for the quantitative verification of relational models of time. A well-isolated Bose condensate evolves in a conservative trap split by a thin optical barrier into an observable and an unobservable sector, with negligible dissipation on the experimental timescales. Drawing inspiration from relational approaches within the Wheeler–DeWitt formalism, we explore the possibility of ordering the dynamics of the observable sector solely through internal degrees of freedom. To this end, an entropic time is constructed from experimentally determined coarse-grained entropy; it is demonstrated to robustly order events in the observable sector over repeated cycles of expansion and contraction. Finally, an effective Schrödinger equation parameterized by this internal time is derived and shown to reproduce the measured evolution. The results establish a controlled experimental environment for quantitative testing of relational time constructs.
