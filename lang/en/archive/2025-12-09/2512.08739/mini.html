Original: "Fluctuation-Induced Supersolidity at the Superfluid-Solid Interface"
Special ripples on liquid helium’s surface freeze it solid—without altering what’s underneath.

Supersolid matter—ice that flows without friction—is usually made by tweaking the forces between atoms. But it turns out, just taking liquid helium and a solid wall is enough: waves at the boundary will turn the surface layer into a quantum crystal all on their own.

🎯 The idea of supersolidity is over half a century old, but it wasn’t until 2017 that it was realized by trapping ultracold atoms in a laser cage.
