Original: "HOPS-288: A Laboratory for Complex Organics in Proto-multiple Systems"

Abstract

Using the ALMA radio telescope, scientists studied the binary protostellar system HOPS-288, where two stars are forming from a common gas-dust cloud. Around each, they discovered 'hot corinos' – compact warm regions rich in complex organic molecules (COMs), which are considered the building blocks of prebiotic chemistry. Interestingly, a small cloud of methanol was also found between the stars – possibly a shock front or material flowing from one to the other. Surprisingly, the set and concentration of key organic compounds in both 'sisters' turned out to be very similar, hinting that in multiple systems the chemical inheritance is distributed roughly equally, like a family fortune.
