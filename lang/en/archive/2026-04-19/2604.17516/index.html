Full version coming soon
Abstract

When we put Kardashev's hypothesis to the test with 60 years of data using Bayesian inference, it turns out global energy consumption grows at about 2% a year, not 1% as Kardashev thought. Extrapolating to a Type II civilization (mastering star energy) creates a paradox: a linear path takes forever, an exponential one crashes into heat-dissipation limits. So Dyson spheres seem unlikely, which neatly explains why we haven't spotted any. The solution? Add an information dimension: a new measure, 'energy per hash,' that ties power to computing work.
