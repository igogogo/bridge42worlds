Original: "A Renormalizable and Unitary Approach to Quantum Gravity"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Quantizing gravity runs into the problem of infinite corrections. The authors proposed introducing a Lagrange multiplier field (an additional variable that restricts quantum fluctuations), which at the one-loop level suppresses uncorrectable divergences. As a result, the model becomes renormalizable (finite) and unitary (preserving probabilities), while reproducing Einstein's equations in the classical limit. This trick resembles temporary scaffolding that lets you build a structure and then is discreetly removed.
