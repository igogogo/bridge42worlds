Original: "A post-Newtonian Gravitational Collapse Model from Linearized Gravity"
The gravitomagnetic field, gravity’s magnetic twin, unexpectedly emerged as a destroyer of quantum superpositions—a new twist in the hunt for quantum gravity.

Pulsars—cosmic quantum laboratories: their rotation at 15% of lightspeed spawns gravitomagnetic noise that kills quantum superpositions. Gravity, like a lathe, shears uncertainty off spinning masses. This new decoherence mechanism paves the way to testing quantum gravity—we might witness how the classical world is born.

🎯 The fastest man-made rotors—levitating nanospheres—spin at 6 GHz, with a surface speed of a tiny 0.0016% of lightspeed. Meanwhile, nature’s record holder, the millisecond pulsar PSR J1748-2446ad, spins 716 times per second, with its surface racing at nearly 15% of lightspeed. Nature gives us ready-made quantum labs on a cosmic scale!

🎬 In Robert L. Forward’s novel Dragon's Egg, life on a neutron star is governed by monstrous gravity and furious rotation. Perhaps such worlds are natural testbeds for the post-Newtonian quantum effects predicted in this paper.
