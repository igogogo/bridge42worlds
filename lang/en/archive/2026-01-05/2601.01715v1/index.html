Original: "Comparison of MOND and Verlinde's emergent gravity in dwarf spheroidals"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Scientists compared two theories of gravity without dark matter — MOND and Verlinde's emergent gravity — using 23 dwarf galaxies. The emergent theory, where gravity arises from microscopic information, better describes the movements of stars in 21 out of 23 cases. The overall statistical advantage was 5.2σ, which strongly favors the emergent approach. It's like the hunch that heat isn't a special fluid but the motion of particles: then the analogue of dark matter would be phlogiston.
