Abstract

The IceCube Neutrino Observatory has detected an excess of high-energy neutrinos from Seyfert galaxies, but without accompanying gamma rays. This suggests that the neutrinos are born in a dense 'corona' around the supermassive black hole, which is opaque to gamma radiation. By modeling particle acceleration in shock waves, researchers showed that protons are efficiently accelerated to energies of ~100 TeV, gaining about 10% of the shock wave's kinetic energy, even at low Mach numbers (~2). Electrons, on the other hand, are barely accelerated (<1%), explaining the gamma-ray deficit and supporting hadronic models of neutrino production in active galactic nuclei.
