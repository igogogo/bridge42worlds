Original: "Three ways to find comfort with the Bell proof and the results of the Bell experiments"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Bell's theorem states: quantum experiments demand we abandon familiar notions of locality and reality. Three authors, ruling out conspiracy theories, propose three paths: from accepting irreducible randomness to a geometric model where the degree of violation of the quantum inequality depends on the number of spatial dimensions — as if our three-dimensional world sets a limit to 'quantum weirdness'. These debates force a rethink of the foundations of physics.
