Original: "What does it mean to have a quantum gravitational theory of de Sitter Space?"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Scientists have shown that if de Sitter space (an accelerating universe) is described by a finite quantum system, then any model of such a space is fundamentally ambiguous. However, if our Universe on large scales tends to such a state and can be embedded in a chain of models converging to a unique superstring model in flat space, then a mathematically rigorous theory can be constructed. But even the most sensitive local experiments can measure only a tiny fraction of the system’s quantum bits. It’s like trying to reconstruct a movie from a few random frames.
