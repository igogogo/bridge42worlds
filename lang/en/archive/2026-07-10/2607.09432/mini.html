The Rocket Effect of Domain Walls: Mass Asymmetry Propels Vacuums ✦

Original: "Dynamics of Biased Domain Walls: The Rocket Effect"
· R. B. Vilhena, P. P. Avelino, C. dos Santos
arXiv:2607.09432 · 2026-07-10 · CC BY 4.0 · 1 min · Cosmology General Relativity HEP Phenomenology HEP Theory
Differences in scalar field excitation masses turn domain walls into microscopic rockets: anisotropic thrust destroys the cosmological defect network.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Invisible films as thin as an atomic nucleus could have torn the Universe apart. But nature engaged a rocket engine: if the masses of quanta on either side differ, the wall emits asymmetric radiation and drifts like a rocket, expanding the light vacuum and destroying the network. Gravitational waves from these cataclysms will bring us information about physics beyond the Standard Model.

🎯 If the network of domain walls had survived to this day, its gravitational influence would have warped time so drastically that clocks on opposite sides of a wall would tick at different rates.

\omega_k^2 = k^2 + m^2
Radiation propagates only if its frequency exceeds the field mass in that vacuum.
v = \frac{P}{\sqrt{\sigma^2+P^2}}
The velocity v is determined by the accumulated momentum P and the wall tension σ.
