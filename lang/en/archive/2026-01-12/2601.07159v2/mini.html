The Climate Swings of Ancient Mars ⚡ экспресс

Original: "Limit cycles and the climate history of Mars"
· Jacob Haqq-Misra
arXiv:2601.07159v2 · 2026-01-12 · CC BY · ⏱ 1 min · Exoplanets
The planet periodically heated up and was briefly covered with rivers and lakes.

Today Mars is an icy desert, but 4 billion years ago streams babbled there. The paradox is that the Sun was dimmer back then. The solution lies in climate swings: the planet periodically heated up due to carbon dioxide. This explains how water appeared on a cold Mars.

🎯 Mars' axis wobbled greatly, sometimes exposing ice to direct sunlight—this added sharpness to the climate swings.

🎬 This picture echoes images of ancient Mars from science fiction, where a dying civilization struggled against encroaching drought.
