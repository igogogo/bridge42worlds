Beneath the hydrogen envelope of most known sub-Neptunes, an eternal ocean of liquid magma likely churns.

Almost all known Neptune-sized planets may never solidify. Beneath hydrogen skies, eternal oceans of molten magma churn—and telescopes can spot their traces.

🎯 Sub-Neptunes are the most common planet type in our galaxy, yet none exist in our Solar System.
