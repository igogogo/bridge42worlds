A New Measurement of the Hubble Constant Using Lensed Supernovae Requiem and Encore ✦

Original: "A new $$H_0$$ measurement with SNe Requiem and Encore using $$\texttt{Gravity.jl}$$"
· L. Bazzanini, G. Di Rosa, A. Acebron, P. Bergamini, M. Lombardi, P. Rosati, G. Angora, G. B. Caminha, S. Ertl, G. Granata, A. M. Koekemoer, S. H. Suyu, J. Pierel, A. B. Newman, C. Grillo, S. Schuldt, M. Bulla, S. Cha, M. D'Addona, J. M. Diego, M. Fogliardi, B. L. Frye, M. J. Jee, P. S. Kamieneski, M. Meneghetti, A. Mercurio, E. Vanzella
arXiv:2606.25205 · 2026-06-23 · CC BY-SA 4.0 · 3 min
Cosmology Galaxies
Analysis of strong gravitational lensing of two Type Ia supernovae in the MACS J0138 cluster yields an independent estimate of the Universe's expansion rate.
1

Context

The Hubble constant characterizes the expansion rate of the Universe, but current measurements from the early and late Universe diverge by nearly 5σ — this is the so-called Hubble tension. Edwin Hubble discovered the expansion, and Hubble's law relates redshift to distance. Gravitational lensing, predicted by Einstein in general relativity and developed by Fritz Zwicky, allows an alternative measurement of H0 based on time delays between lensed images of variable sources. Gravitational lenses in galaxy clusters offer a unique opportunity: time delays on the order of years, which reduces the relative measurement error.

Methods

The scientists used data from the Hubble and JWST telescopes, along with MUSE spectroscopy, to identify 23 multiple images from 8 background sources with known redshifts in the MACS J0138 cluster. Lens mass modeling was performed using the Gravity.jl package, parameterizing dark matter with a NIE profile and galaxies with scaling relations. Dark energy was incorporated via the ΛCDM cosmology. Time delays for the supernovae were measured from light curves with an accuracy of a few days.

Results

The best model reproduces image positions with an rms deviation of 0.24″. Predicted delays: for SN Encore, Δt1b,1a = −38+3−3 days (consistent with observations), the long-delayed image 1d will appear after Δt1d,1a = 3177+78−59 days. For SN Requiem, the deferred image 2d is expected after Δt2d,2a = 3938+90−77 days (≈2027). Joint analysis of the delays yielded a measurement of the Hubble constant: H0 = 67.0+9.3−7.8 km/s/Mpc. This value matches estimates from the early Universe and confirms the tension with local measurements.

Implications

This result strengthens the Hubble tension, showing that the independent gravitational lensing method gives an H0 value close to the cosmological one, not the local one. The model confirms the predictability of lensed supernovae as standardizable candles, allowing new image appearances to be predicted with high accuracy. Incorporating delays into a joint Bayesian mass+H0 analysis lays the foundation for time-delay cosmography with galaxy clusters.

Future development

Future observations require long-term monitoring of the expected images. Adding the surface brightness of arcs to the lens model will refine the potential near the images and reduce systematics. With the advent of the JWST and Rubin observatories, the statistics of lensed supernovae will dramatically increase, turning the method into a routine cosmological tool.

Impact

Resolving the Hubble tension and testing ΛCDM. The method is applicable for testing dark matter and dark energy models, as well as calibrating supernovae as standard candles.

Next steps

Await the reappearance of the SN Requiem image in 2027 and measure its delay with ~1% accuracy. Implement full modeling of arc surface brightness in Gravity.jl.

Key open problems

The work directly addresses the Hubble tension — the discrepancy between the H0 value from the early Universe (CMB, BAO) and local supernovae. It also touches on the nature of dark matter through cluster profiles and dark energy, which determines the global geometry.
