An Invisible Line Revealed a Magnetic Neutron Star express

Original: "Discovery of a Candidate 2 keV Cyclotron Resonance Scattering Feature in the HLX NGC 3583 X-1"
· Kiran M. Jayasurya, Aman Upadhyay, Vikram Rana
arXiv:2606.08598 · 2026-06-07 · CC BY 4.0 · 1 min · High Energy
From a barely noticeable dark line in X-ray light, astronomers identified a magnetar — a neutron star with a magnetic field 400 trillion times stronger than Earth's.
Abstract

We analyzed archival data from XMM-Newton, NuSTAR, Chandra, and long-term Swift/XRT monitoring of the transient hyperluminous X-ray source 2SXPS J111416.1+481833 in the galaxy NGC 3583. The source sporadically reaches luminosities above 10^41 erg/s and drops to a deep low state (attenuation factor >45). The broadband spectrum reveals a break at 5–6 keV, modeled as a combination of a soft thermal component and Compton scattering in an optically thick medium or an advection-dominated disk. XMM-Newton spectra show a statistically significant (>3.9σ) absorption line at 1.97±0.04 keV with a width σ≈74±40 eV, which is interpreted as a candidate proton cyclotron resonance scattering feature (CRSF). This points to a local magnetic field strength B~4×10^14 G. A search for coherent X-ray pulsations yielded no significant signal: upper limits on the pulsed fraction are 19.3% (0.3–10 keV) and 36.3% (3–15 keV) at 90% confidence. The combination of extreme luminosity, hard spectrum, and probable cyclotron line supports a highly magnetized neutron star as the accretor.

In the galaxy NGC 3583, telescopes noticed a strange X-ray source: it flares up and then almost fades away. Breaking its light into a spectrum (a rainbow of energies), astronomers found a narrow dark dip — like a single note plucked from a chord.

This 'note' is created by protons trapped in a monstrous magnetic field. Like a string pressed on a fret, they vibrate at exactly one frequency. The pitch of the note revealed: the field is 400 trillion times stronger than Earth's. Before us is a neutron star with record magnetism — a magnetar.

Such a line is a precise tuning fork: by its sound, scientists measured the star's invisible power without getting close to it.

A field of this strength not only tears atoms apart, stretching them into needles, but also turns the surrounding void into something like crystal, bending light rays.

🎯 A chunk of neutron star the size of a grain of sand weighs more than an aircraft carrier.

E \approx 0.5 \times \frac{B}{10^{14} \text{ G}} \text{ keV}
