JADE: The AI Detective That Reads the Cosmic Web in Light Trails ✦

Original: "Joint inference of weak lensing convergence map and cosmology with diffusion models"
· Benjamin Remy, Chihway Chang, Rebecca Willett
arXiv:2606.31988 · 2026-06-30 · CC BY 4.0 · 1 min · Cosmology Instrumentation
The JADE method, based on diffusion models, simultaneously reconstructs dark matter maps and measures the Universe's parameters from weak gravitational lensing data.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Every distant galaxy is a witness at a cosmic crime scene. The gravity of dark matter distorts its appearance, like a funhouse mirror. JADE is an algorithm that interrogates billions of such witnesses and reconstructs a portrait of the invisible. This approach brings closer the era when the map of the invisible Universe will be as detailed as a star chart.

🎯 Training the model required only 100,000 simulations, while a single MCMC run for one observation demands roughly a million simulator calls. Generating one posterior sample takes just 0.2 seconds.

🎬 Like in Peter Watts' "Blindsight", where aliens reveal themselves only as a ripple against the background, JADE fishes out dark matter from the background ripple of lensing.
