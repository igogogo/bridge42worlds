A Two-Color Chord That Cracks Magnetic Symmetry

Original: "Giant perpendicular Edelstein polarization in 2D compensated magnets via bichromatic Floquet driving"
· Mohsen Yarmohammadi, Daegeun Jo, Marco Berritta, Libor Šmejkal, James K. Freericks, Peter M. Oppeneer
arXiv:2606.31867 · 2026-06-30 · CC BY · 1 min
Materials Mesoscale Strongly Correlated Quantum Physics
Combining Rashba spin-orbit coupling and two-color Floquet pumping, physicists have for the first time achieved giant perpendicular spin and orbital polarization in symmetry-forbidden d-wave altermagnets.
1

In altermagnets, current cannot produce transverse magnetization—a symmetry ban, like a conductor forbidding notes to sound. But physicists found a workaround: a two-color laser chord at frequencies ω and 2ω temporarily vetoes the veto, giving birth to giant polarization. The orbital moment dominates, and the field inside the material rivals that of a neutron star. Thus we edge closer to memory where information is written not by magnets, but by light.

🎯 The orbital polarization here arises from the self-rotation of an electron wave packet in momentum space—a purely geometric quantum effect akin to the Berry phase, predicted by [scientist:Paul Dirac]Dirac[/scientist] and [scientist:Lev Landau]Landau[/scientist].

🎬 The idea of writing information with light, like the holographic memory in Star Wars, gains a real physical foundation: two-color laser pumping turns an altermagnet into an optically controlled data carrier.

H_{\text{eff}} \approx H_0 + \frac{[H_{-1}, H_{+1}]}{\hbar \omega}
The effective static Hamiltonian under high-frequency pumping. The commutator of the Floquet harmonics H_{-1} and H_{+1} describes the interference of one- and two-photon processes, creating a symmetry-breaking Zeeman field.
B_{\text{eff}} \propto \frac{(e E_0)^2}{\hbar \omega} \sin(\Delta\phi)
Magnitude of the effective planar Zeeman field arising from two-color pumping. It depends on the square of the laser’s electric field amplitude and the phase difference between harmonics, allowing smooth control of polarization.
