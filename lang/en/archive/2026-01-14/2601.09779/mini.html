Time Crystal Matryoshkas: A New Layer of Order ⚡ экспресс

Original: "Hierarchical time crystals"
· Jan Carlo Schumann, Igor Lesanovsky, Parvinder Solanki
arXiv:2601.09779 · 2026-01-14 · CC BY 4.0 · ⏱ 1 min · Quantum Physics
The union of two types of time crystals unexpectedly gave rise to a hierarchy: rhythms nested within each other that weren't in the original rules.
Links in the knowledge graph 1

Time crystals are eternally 'ticking' quantum clocks without mechanisms. And now scientists have created a double clock: by combining two types of time crystals, they obtained a new, hierarchical rhythm. It turned out one half starts pulsing by rules that weren't originally there. Like a third part suddenly emerging in an orchestra. This is a step toward understanding how complex order arises from chaos, and a hint at the quantum memory of the future.

🎯 The first time crystal (2017) consisted of just five ytterbium ions cooled nearly to absolute zero — a tiny ensemble that gave rise to eternal motion.

🎬 In Hamilton's novel 'Stolen Fire,' time crystals serve as an energy source for interstellar ships, and in Hannu Rajaniemi's 'The Quantum Thief,' they are used as currency and memory storage.
