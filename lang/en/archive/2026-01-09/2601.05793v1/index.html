Original: "Towards Accurate Gravitational Wave Predictions: Gauge-Invariant Nucleation in the Electroweak Phase Transition"

Abstract

Vacuum decay via a first-order phase transition in the early Universe must be described without relying on an arbitrary gauge (choice of reference frame in the mathematical description). The study showed that using an effective field theory for the Standard Model in three dimensions and the Nielsen identity, one can obtain gauge-invariant bubble nucleation rates and transition parameters. It’s akin to how bubbles in boiling water look the same from any angle. This approach improves the accuracy of gravitational wave predictions from cosmological phase transitions, which are crucial for the search for new physics.
