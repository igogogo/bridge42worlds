Original: "Gravitational Ionization by Schwarzschild Primordial Black Holes"
Full version coming soon
Abstract

Primordial black holes (PBHs) in the asteroid-mass range (10^17–10^23 g) are dark matter candidates, but their detection is hampered by weak Hawking radiation. This work explores the tidal gravitational effects of PBHs, which can ionize neutral hydrogen or destroy nuclei. It is shown that today, gravitational ionization is masked by Hawking radiation, but during the recombination epoch (z≈1090) for PBHs with masses 5×10^21–10^23 g, gravitational heating dominated. Tidal forces also overcome the strong interaction: dissociating deuterons (critical for primordial nucleosynthesis) and inducing fission of heavy nuclei. For PBHs of 10^14–10^16 g, gravitational deuteron breakup surpasses photodisruption from Hawking radiation. The phenomenon of gravitationally induced nuclear fission via tidal deformation is unveiled.
