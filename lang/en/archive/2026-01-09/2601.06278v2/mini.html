Original: "VLBI Observations of SN 2012au Reveal a Compact Radio Source a Decade Post Explosion"
In a distant galaxy, after a star explosion, astronomers may be witnessing the birth of a pulsar — a cosmic lighthouse, possibly for the first time.

Ten years after the supernova explosion, astronomers saw how its 'heart' — a tiny neutron star — ignited a radio nebula around it. If the hunch is correct, this is the first extragalactic pulsar nursery available for study.

🎯 Some pulsars spin up to 700 times per second — faster than the crankshaft of a Formula 1 racing car.
