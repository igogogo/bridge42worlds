Two Quantum Sensors Peek Inside a Living Cell ⚡ экспресс

Original: "A Pulsed Live-Cell Quantum Microscope for Entangled Solid State and Biological Qubits"
· Javier Noé Ramos-Silva, Sangjun Noh, Minghao Jiang, Parisa Aghaei, Ayla Hazrathosseini, Jocelyn Leon, Philip Hemmer, Peter J. Burke
arXiv:2607.03552 · 2026-07-03 · CC BY 4.0 · ⏱ 1 min · Quantum Physics
A new microscope allows simultaneous tracking of protein and diamond sensors inside a cell.

For the first time, two quantum sensors have worked simultaneously inside a living cell: one made of protein, the other of diamond dust. A laser microscope orchestrates them like a conductor, allowing us to hear the magnetic whispers of cellular processes. This is a step toward quantum reading of neuron signals and the creation of ultra-precise biosensors.

🎯 Some proteins in the eyes of migratory birds can sense Earth's magnetic field, acting as natural quantum compasses.

🎬 Reminiscent of the medical tricorder from 'Star Trek': it peers into cells and deciphers their magnetic whispers.
