Angular momentum is a measure of rotational motion of a body. It depends on mass, velocity, and distance from the axis of rotation. Imagine a figure skater spinning: when they pull their arms in, their rotation speeds up — this is conservation of angular momentum.

History

The concept of angular momentum was developed in Newtonian mechanics, but its conservation was recognized even by Leonardo da Vinci. In quantum mechanics, angular momentum is quantized: it can only take discrete values. Spin is the intrinsic angular momentum of particles.

How it works

In a closed system, angular momentum is conserved. For example, planets orbit the Sun, and their orbital angular momentum remains nearly constant. When a star collapses into a neutron star, its radius decreases and its rotation speed dramatically increases due to conservation of angular momentum.
