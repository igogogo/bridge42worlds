Dark Energy

📊 0 articles
A mysterious form of energy permeating all space and causing the accelerated expansion of the universe. It constitutes about 68% of the total energy of the universe. Imagine the universe not just expanding, but accelerating, as if something is pushing it from within.
💡 If dark energy were stronger in the early universe, galaxies could not have formed; if weaker, the universe might have collapsed.
A mysterious form of energy permeating all space and causing the accelerated expansion of the universe. It constitutes about 68% of the total energy of the universe. Imagine the universe not just expanding, but accelerating, as if something is pushing it from within.

History

The discovery of the accelerated expansion of the universe was made in 1998 by two groups: the Supernova Cosmology Project (Perlmutter) and the High-Z Supernova Search Team (Riess, Schmidt). They observed type Ia supernovae and found they were dimmer than expected, indicating acceleration. This discovery earned the Nobel Prize in 2011.

How it works

Dark energy manifests as negative pressure, which counteracts gravity. Its nature is unknown; the simplest model is the cosmological constant Λ (vacuum energy). Other models include quintessence — a dynamic field. Dark energy has dominated the dynamics of the universe for the last 5 billion years.
