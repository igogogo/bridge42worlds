Higgs Boson

📊 0 articles
A fundamental particle responsible for the mechanism by which other particles acquire mass. It is the quantum of the Higgs field, which permeates all space. Imagine the Higgs field as a thick molasses, and particles as balls: some move easily, others get stuck, giving them mass.
💡 The Higgs boson is sometimes called the 'God particle' — a name physicists dislike, but it has stuck in popular culture.
A fundamental particle responsible for the mechanism by which other particles acquire mass. It is the quantum of the Higgs field, which permeates all space. Imagine the Higgs field as a thick molasses, and particles as balls: some move easily, others get stuck, giving them mass.

History

Peter Higgs and other physicists independently proposed the mechanism in 1964. The Higgs boson was discovered in 2012 at the Large Hadron Collider (CERN), confirming the Standard Model. Peter Higgs and François Englert received the Nobel Prize in 2013.

How it works

Particles interact with the Higgs field, and the stronger the interaction, the greater the mass. Analogy: a celebrity in a crowd — their surroundings (field) slow them down, creating an effect of mass. Without the Higgs field, all particles would be massless and move at the speed of light.

Open problems & fun facts

Why is the Higgs boson mass what it is? This is related to the hierarchy problem. It is also unclear whether the Higgs boson is elementary or composite.
