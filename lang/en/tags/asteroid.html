Asteroid

📊 0 articles
A small rocky or metallic body orbiting the Sun, usually in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. Asteroids are the building remnants from which planets formed. Imagine rocks of various sizes left over after building a house.
💡 The asteroid Bennu, studied by the OSIRIS-REx mission, contains organic molecules and water, supporting the theory that water was delivered to Earth by asteroids.

History

The first asteroid, Ceres, was discovered in 1801 by Giuseppe Piazzi. Initially considered a planet, but later many similar objects were found. In the 1990s, missions to asteroids began: NEAR Shoemaker landed on Eros, and Japan's Hayabusa mission returned samples from Itokawa.

How it works

Asteroids move in orbits around the Sun, their sizes range from a few meters to hundreds of kilometers. They can collide with each other, forming smaller fragments. Some asteroids have their own moons. Analogy: it's like rocks in a washing machine drum — they collide, break apart, and sometimes fly out.
