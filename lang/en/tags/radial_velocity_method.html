radial velocity method

A method of detecting exoplanets by the star's wobble caused by the planet's gravity. Like swinging a weight on a string: your hand also moves slightly. The star's radial velocity is measured via Doppler shift of its spectrum.
💡 The radial velocity method led to the discovery of most early exoplanets, including 'hot Jupiters'.
A method of detecting exoplanets by the star's wobble caused by the planet's gravity. Like swinging a weight on a string: your hand also moves slightly. The star's radial velocity is measured via Doppler shift of its spectrum.

History

First proposed in 1952. The first exoplanet around a Sun-like star (51 Pegasi b) was discovered by this method in 1995. It is now one of the main methods.

How it works

The planet and star orbit a common center of mass. The star undergoes a small motion, manifesting as periodic shifts in spectral lines (Doppler effect). Amplitude and period give the planet's mass and orbit.
