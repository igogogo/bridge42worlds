An accelerator where beams of particles collide at high energy. Like billiards, but the balls are elementary particles, and their collision allows us to peer into the microcosm and recreate the conditions of the early Universe.

History

The first colliders appeared in the 1960s. The Large Hadron Collider (LHC) was launched in 2008. In 2012, the Higgs boson, predicted by Peter Higgs, was discovered there.

How it works

Particles (e.g., protons) are accelerated in a ring accelerator to near-light speeds and collide. Detectors record the collision products. Data analysis allows studying fundamental interactions and searching for new particles.
