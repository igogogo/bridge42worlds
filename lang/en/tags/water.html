Water

📊 1 articles
Water (H₂O) is a simple compound of hydrogen and oxygen, but the basis of life on Earth. It exists in three states: liquid, solid (ice), and gaseous (vapor). Imagine drinking a glass of water: it is colorless, odorless, but life is impossible without it.
💡 Water is one of the few substances whose solid phase (ice) is less dense than the liquid phase, so ice floats, preventing water bodies from freezing solid.

History

Water was recognized as a chemical compound in the late 18th century. Antoine Lavoisier and Henry Cavendish showed that water consists of hydrogen and oxygen. In the 20th century, water became an object of search on other planets, as its presence is considered a key condition for life.

How it works

Water is a universal solvent due to the polarity of its molecules. It participates in chemical reactions and regulates temperature due to its high heat capacity. In space, water has been found as ice on comets, moons of giant planets, and in molecular clouds.
