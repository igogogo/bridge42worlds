Transit Method

📊 1 articles
A method of detecting exoplanets by the periodic dimming of a star's light when a planet passes in front of its disk. Like a fly flying in front of a lamp: the light momentarily dims.
💡 The Kepler space telescope discovered over 2600 exoplanets using the transit method.
A method of detecting exoplanets by the periodic dimming of a star's light when a planet passes in front of its disk. Like a fly flying in front of a lamp: the light momentarily dims.

History

First proposed in 1952 by Otto Struve. The first exoplanet discovered by this method (HD 209458b) was found in 1999. The transit method is now the primary method for finding exoplanets, especially thanks to the Kepler mission.

How it works

If the planet's orbit is aligned so that it passes between the star and the observer, each transit causes a small drop in brightness. The depth and period of the transit reveal the planet's size and orbital period.
