History

Carbon has been known since ancient times in the form of charcoal and diamonds. In 1779, Antoine Lavoisier showed that diamond and graphite consist of the same element. In the 20th century, new forms of carbon were discovered: fullerenes, graphene, and carbon nanotubes.

How it works

Carbon forms four chemical bonds, allowing it to create long chains and rings. In stars, carbon is synthesized from helium in the triple-alpha process. On Earth, carbon is a component of all organic molecules: carbohydrates, fats, proteins, and DNA.
