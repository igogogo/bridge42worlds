A system of two stars orbiting a common center of mass. Imagine two dancers waltzing, holding hands—except gravity binds them instead of hands.

History

The first binary star (Mizar) was discovered in 1650. In 1802, William Herschel proved they are gravitationally bound pairs. Many types are known: visual, spectroscopic, eclipsing.

How it works

The stars orbit the center of mass in elliptical orbits. If the orbital plane aligns with the line of sight, they periodically eclipse each other, allowing measurement of their parameters.
