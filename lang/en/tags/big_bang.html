History

The idea of an expanding universe was derived from general relativity equations by Alexander Friedmann (1922) and Georges Lemaître (1927, 'primeval atom'). Edwin Hubble (1929) discovered that galaxies are receding. The discovery of the cosmic microwave background (Penzias and Wilson, 1965) was decisive confirmation. The hot Big Bang theory was developed by Gamow et al.

How it works

In the initial moment, the universe was infinitely dense and hot. Then it expanded and cooled. At 10⁻⁴³ s after the Planck epoch, inflation began. Gradually, elementary particles formed, then atoms, stars, and galaxies. The cosmic microwave background is the 'echo' of the Big Bang, cooled to 2.7 K.
