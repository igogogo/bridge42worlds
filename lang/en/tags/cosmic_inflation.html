A theory that in the first moments after the Big Bang, the universe expanded exponentially fast, increasing in size by billions of billions of times in a tiny fraction of a second. Imagine a balloon instantly inflating from the size of a pea to the size of a football stadium — that is inflation.

History

The idea of inflation was proposed by Alan Guth in 1980 to solve the horizon and flatness problems of the universe. Later, Andrei Linde and others developed various models of inflation. The theory received indirect confirmation from observations of the cosmic microwave background.

How it works

Inflation is explained by the presence of a scalar field — the inflaton, which creates negative pressure, causing space to expand with acceleration. Analogy: if you suddenly start blowing up a balloon, its surface expands uniformly in all directions. After inflation, the field decayed, giving birth to particles and radiation.
