Lepton

📊 1 articles
A class of fundamental particles that do not participate in the strong interaction. Leptons include the electron, muon, tau lepton, and corresponding neutrinos. They are fermions with spin 1/2. Imagine leptons as solo dancers who do not form groups, unlike quarks.
💡 Every second, about 100 billion neutrinos from the Sun pass through your body, but you don't notice them.
A class of fundamental particles that do not participate in the strong interaction. Leptons include the electron, muon, tau lepton, and corresponding neutrinos. They are fermions with spin 1/2. Imagine leptons as solo dancers who do not form groups, unlike quarks.

History

The electron was discovered first (1897). The muon was found in 1936, the tau lepton in 1975. Neutrinos were predicted by Wolfgang Pauli in 1930 and discovered in 1956. The Standard Model includes three generations of leptons.

How it works

Leptons participate in electromagnetic and weak interactions. Analogy: leptons are different types of balls: electron—small, muon—medium, tau—large, and neutrinos—almost invisible. Neutrinos interact very weakly with matter, passing through Earth almost unimpeded.

Open problems & fun facts

Why are there three generations of leptons? This is the fermion hierarchy puzzle. Also, the nature of neutrino mass is unclear.
