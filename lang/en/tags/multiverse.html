Multiverse

📊 0 articles
A hypothetical set of multiple universes, including ours, which may have different physical laws and constants. The idea arises from various theories: quantum mechanics (many-worlds interpretation), cosmology (eternal inflation), and string theory (landscape). It is like an infinite library where each book is a separate universe.
💡 If the multiverse exists, in some universes you might be president, and in others you might never be born; but we can never verify it.

History

The many-worlds interpretation of quantum mechanics was proposed by Hugh Everett (1957). The idea of eternal inflation was developed by Andrei Linde (1980s). String theory predicts a huge number of possible vacua (landscape). The anthropic principle is used to explain the fine-tuning of physical constants. There is no direct evidence for the multiverse.

How it works

In eternal inflation, different regions of spacetime stop expanding at different times, forming 'pocket universes' with different properties. In the many-worlds interpretation, each quantum measurement splits the universe into branches. String theory allows up to 10^500 different vacua. Testing the hypothesis is difficult because other universes may be unobservable.
