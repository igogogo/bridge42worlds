Neutron

📊 1 articles
A neutral subatomic particle found in atomic nuclei together with protons. Neutrons are stable only inside the nucleus; a free neutron decays in about 15 minutes. They consist of one up quark and two down quarks. Imagine a neutron as the 'glue' that helps protons coexist in the nucleus without flying apart.
💡 Neutron stars are remnants of supernova explosions, consisting mostly of neutrons; their density is such that a teaspoon of matter weighs billions of tons.

History

James Chadwick discovered the neutron in 1932 by bombarding beryllium with alpha particles. This discovery allowed understanding of the nucleus and led to the creation of the nuclear reactor. Enrico Fermi used neutrons to induce radioactivity.

How it works

Neutrons participate in the strong nuclear interaction, holding the nucleus together. Analogy: in a group of friends (protons), there is a peacemaker (neutron) who smooths over conflicts. Neutrons are also used to initiate the chain reaction of uranium fission.
