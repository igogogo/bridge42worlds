History

The ancient Greeks called it 'milk' because of the misty band in the sky. In 1610, Galileo discovered it was a multitude of stars. In the 1920s, Edwin Hubble proved that the Milky Way is just one of many galaxies.

How it works

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy rotating around its center, where a supermassive black hole resides. Stars move in orbits like planets around the Sun, but on a galactic scale.
