Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory—a detector capable of sensing ripples in spacetime from catastrophic events. Like a microphone listening to the Universe in gravitational waves.

History

Idea proposed in the 1970s. Construction began in 1994. First direct detection of gravitational waves occurred on September 14, 2015 (black hole merger). The 2017 Nobel Prize was awarded for this discovery.

How it works

Two perpendicular arms 4 km long. A laser beam is split, reflected by mirrors, and interferes. A passing gravitational wave changes the arm lengths by a tiny amount, detected as a change in interference.
