Amino acids

📊 0 articles
Amino acids are organic molecules that are the building blocks of proteins. They consist of an amino group, a carboxyl group, and a side chain. Imagine proteins as long chains of colorful beads, where each bead is an amino acid.
💡 In the Murchison meteorite, which fell in Australia in 1969, more than 70 different amino acids were found, including some not used by terrestrial life.
Amino acids are organic molecules that are the building blocks of proteins. They consist of an amino group, a carboxyl group, and a side chain. Imagine proteins as long chains of colorful beads, where each bead is an amino acid.

History

The first amino acid (asparagine) was discovered in 1806. By the early 20th century, 20 standard amino acids were identified. In 1953, Stanley Miller conducted an experiment showing that amino acids could form under conditions of early Earth. In space, amino acids have been found in meteorites.

How it works

Amino acids join via peptide bonds to form proteins. In living organisms, they perform many functions: catalysis (enzymes), transport, structural support. In space, amino acids can be synthesized in interstellar clouds under the influence of ultraviolet radiation and cosmic rays.
