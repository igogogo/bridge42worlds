Energy is a scalar quantity characterizing a body's ability to do work. It comes in various forms: kinetic (energy of motion), potential (energy of position), thermal, chemical, and others. Imagine a charged battery: it stores chemical energy that can be converted into electrical energy.

History

The concept of energy developed gradually. In the 19th century, James Prescott Joule experimentally demonstrated the equivalence of heat and mechanical work. Hermann von Helmholtz formulated the law of conservation of energy. Einstein discovered the equivalence of mass and energy: E=mc².

How it works

Energy is neither created nor destroyed — it only transforms from one form to another. For example, when a stone falls, its potential energy converts into kinetic energy, and upon impact, into heat and sound. In nuclear reactions, mass converts into energy.
