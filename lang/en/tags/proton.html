Proton

📊 1 articles
A stable subatomic particle with a positive electric charge, found in atomic nuclei. The number of protons determines the chemical element. Protons consist of two up quarks and one down quark. Imagine a proton as a tough nut, inside which three quarks are hidden, held together by the strong interaction.
💡 Protons are stable, but some theories predict their decay with a half-life of more than 10^34 years—this has not yet been observed.

History

Ernest Rutherford discovered the proton in 1919 by bombarding nitrogen with alpha particles. He suggested that the hydrogen nucleus (proton) is part of other nuclei. Later, James Chadwick discovered the neutron, and the model of the nucleus became clear. In the 1960s, it was established that the proton consists of quarks.

How it works

Protons are held in the nucleus by the strong nuclear force, which overcomes electrical repulsion. Analogy: several magnets attracted to each other despite repelling—so protons are bound by the strong force. The proton's size is about 0.84 femtometers.
