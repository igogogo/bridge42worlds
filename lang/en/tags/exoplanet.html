History

The first confirmed discovery of an exoplanet around a Sun-like star was made in 1995 by Michel Mayor and Didier Queloz, for which they received the Nobel Prize in 2019. Earlier, in 1992, planets were found around a pulsar, but that was a special case. Now over 5,000 exoplanets have been discovered, and detection methods are constantly improving.

How it works

Exoplanets are detected mainly by two methods: the transit method (the planet passes in front of the star, slightly dimming its brightness) and the radial velocity method (the planet's gravity causes the star to wobble slightly). Analogy: imagine trying to notice a fly flying in front of a bright spotlight — the fly is barely visible, but the light flickers slightly.
