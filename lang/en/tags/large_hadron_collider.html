The world's most powerful particle accelerator, located at CERN. Like a giant microscope, allowing us to peer into the deepest depths of matter and recreate the conditions of the first moments after the Big Bang.

History

Construction began in 1998, launch in 2008. In 2012, the Higgs boson was discovered, predicted 50 years earlier. An upgrade to HL-LHC is planned.

How it works

Protons are accelerated in a 27 km ring to an energy of 6.5 TeV and collide. Detectors (ATLAS, CMS) record collision products. Data analysis allows testing the Standard Model and searching for new physics.
