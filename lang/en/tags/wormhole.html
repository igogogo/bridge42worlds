Wormhole

📊 0 articles
PopularSimpleAdvanced
A hypothetical tunnel in spacetime connecting two distant points, possibly in different universes or times. Imagine folding a sheet of paper in half and poking it with a pencil — a wormhole allows traveling from one point to another faster than across the surface.
💡 In the movie 'Interstellar' (2014), physicist Kip Thorne was a consultant on wormholes, showing that theoretically a traversable wormhole could be created, but it would require matter with negative mass.
A hypothetical tunnel in spacetime connecting two distant points, possibly in different universes or times. Imagine folding a sheet of paper in half and poking it with a pencil — a wormhole allows traveling from one point to another faster than across the surface.

History

The idea first appeared in the work of Ludwig Flamm (1916) and Albert Einstein with Nathan Rosen (1935) as an 'Einstein-Rosen bridge'. John Wheeler popularized the term 'wormhole' in 1957. Later, Kip Thorne and his colleagues (1980s) showed that a traversable wormhole requires exotic matter with negative energy.

How it works

In general relativity, a wormhole is a solution of the field equations connecting two asymptotically flat regions. The throat is held open by exotic matter. Traveling through a wormhole may be faster than the ordinary path but requires violation of energy conditions. They have not been detected and are considered extremely unstable.
