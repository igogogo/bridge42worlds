Photometry

📊 1 articles
A method of measuring the intensity of light from astronomical objects. Like measuring the brightness of a light bulb, but for stars and galaxies. It allows determination of distances, sizes, and brightness changes.
💡 Photometry helped discover the first exoplanet transiting its star (HD 209458b).
A method of measuring the intensity of light from astronomical objects. Like measuring the brightness of a light bulb, but for stars and galaxies. It allows determination of distances, sizes, and brightness changes.

History

Ancient astronomers estimated star brightness by eye (magnitudes). Photographic methods emerged in the 19th century. Modern photometry uses CCDs and precise filters.

How it works

Light is collected by a telescope and recorded by a detector. The number of photons is measured in different filters. Comparison with standard stars yields accurate brightness. Light curves are used to study variable stars and exoplanet transits.
