An instrument for collecting and focusing electromagnetic radiation from distant objects. Like a spyglass, but much larger and more complex. There are optical, radio, X-ray, and other types.

History

The first telescope was invented by Hans Lippershey in 1608. Galileo improved it and turned it to the sky. Newton built the first reflecting telescope. Since then, sizes have grown from a few centimeters to 10 meters.

How it works

A telescope collects light from a large area (lens or mirror) and focuses it into an image. The larger the diameter, the more light and higher resolution. Modern telescopes use adaptive optics to compensate for the atmosphere.
