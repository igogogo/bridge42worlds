The James Webb Space Telescope—the most powerful infrared telescope, launched in 2021. Like a time machine, allowing us to look back at the era of the first stars and galaxies.

History

Development began in 1996, launch was repeatedly delayed. After successful launch and deployment in 2022, JWST began scientific observations. It is the successor to Hubble.

How it works

JWST observes in the infrared range, allowing it to see through dust and study very distant objects. Its 6.5-meter mirror consists of 18 segments. The telescope is located at the L2 Lagrange point.
