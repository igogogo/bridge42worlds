Silicon

📊 0 articles
Silicon is a semiconducting element, the basis of modern electronics. It is the second most abundant element in the Earth's crust after oxygen. Imagine your smartphone: its processor is made of silicon, which controls all computations.
💡 Silicon is considered a possible basis for silicon-based life, although its chemistry is less diverse than that of carbon.
Silicon is a semiconducting element, the basis of modern electronics. It is the second most abundant element in the Earth's crust after oxygen. Imagine your smartphone: its processor is made of silicon, which controls all computations.

History

Silicon was isolated in pure form by Jöns Jacob Berzelius in 1824. In the mid-20th century, the development of semiconductor technology made silicon a key material for transistors and microchips. In astrophysics, silicon is important as a product of stellar nucleosynthesis.

How it works

Silicon forms a crystal lattice that can be doped with impurities to create semiconducting properties. In stars, silicon is synthesized during the silicon-burning process, preceding the formation of iron. On Earth, silicon is a component of sand (SiO₂) and many minerals.
