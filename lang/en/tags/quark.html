Quark

📊 0 articles
A fundamental particle that makes up protons, neutrons, and other hadrons. Quarks have fractional electric charge and participate in the strong interaction. There are six types (flavors): up, down, strange, charm, bottom, and top. Imagine quarks as bricks from which all matter particles are built.
💡 The heaviest quark—top—weighs as much as a gold atom but lives only 10^-25 seconds.
A fundamental particle that makes up protons, neutrons, and other hadrons. Quarks have fractional electric charge and participate in the strong interaction. There are six types (flavors): up, down, strange, charm, bottom, and top. Imagine quarks as bricks from which all matter particles are built.

History

The quark hypothesis was independently proposed by Murray Gell-Mann and George Zweig in 1964. Gell-Mann introduced the term 'quark' from the novel 'Finnegans Wake'. Experimental confirmation of quarks was obtained in 1968 at the Stanford Linear Accelerator.

How it works

Quarks never appear alone due to confinement—they are always bound in hadrons. Analogy: try to separate one magnet from a chain—you cannot get a single magnet, only pairs. Quarks are held together by gluons, particles of the strong interaction.

Open problems & fun facts

Why do quarks not exist freely? The mechanism of confinement is not fully understood. Also, it is unclear why there are exactly six quark flavors.
