Carbon dioxide

📊 0 articles
Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is a gas composed of carbon and oxygen. It is a product of respiration and combustion, and an important greenhouse gas. Imagine exhaling: the exhaled air contains carbon dioxide, which plants use for photosynthesis.
💡 Venus has a dense atmosphere of CO₂, creating a surface temperature of about 460°C — hotter than Mercury, even though Venus is farther from the Sun.
Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is a gas composed of carbon and oxygen. It is a product of respiration and combustion, and an important greenhouse gas. Imagine exhaling: the exhaled air contains carbon dioxide, which plants use for photosynthesis.

History

Carbon dioxide was discovered by Joseph Black in the 1750s, who called it 'fixed air'. Antoine Lavoisier showed that it consists of carbon and oxygen. In the 19th century, its role in the greenhouse effect was predicted by Svante Arrhenius.

How it works

Carbon dioxide absorbs infrared radiation, trapping heat in the atmosphere — the greenhouse effect. In space, CO₂ has been detected in the atmospheres of Mars and Venus, creating a strong greenhouse effect there. On Earth, its concentration is rising due to the burning of fossil fuels.
