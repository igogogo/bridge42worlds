Ammonia

📊 0 articles
Ammonia (NH₃) is a compound of nitrogen and hydrogen, a gas with a pungent odor. It is widely used in fertilizers and household chemicals. Imagine the smell of ammonia solution: that is ammonia dissolved in water.
💡 Ammonia has a high heat of vaporization, so it is used in some refrigeration systems, although it is toxic.
Ammonia (NH₃) is a compound of nitrogen and hydrogen, a gas with a pungent odor. It is widely used in fertilizers and household chemicals. Imagine the smell of ammonia solution: that is ammonia dissolved in water.

History

Ammonia was known to alchemists, but first isolated in pure form by Joseph Priestley in 1774. In 1909, Fritz Haber developed a process for synthesizing ammonia from nitrogen and hydrogen (Haber-Bosch process), enabling industrial-scale fertilizer production.

How it works

Ammonia is formed in nature from the decomposition of organic matter. In space, ammonia has been detected in the atmospheres of giant planets (Jupiter, Saturn) and in interstellar clouds. It plays a role in the chemistry of prebiotic molecules and may be an indicator of life.
