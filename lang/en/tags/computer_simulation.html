Creating mathematical models of physical processes and solving them numerically on a computer. Like a virtual laboratory where you can conduct an experiment impossible in reality—for example, a galaxy collision or the evolution of the Universe.

History

The first computer calculations appeared in the 1950s. With the development of supercomputers, it became possible to simulate galaxy formation, climate, nuclear reactions. Simulations are now an integral part of science.

How it works

Laws of physics are written as equations (hydrodynamics, gravity). Space and time are divided into cells, and the computer calculates the system's evolution step by step. Results are compared with observations.
