Electromagnetism

📊 0 articles
A fundamental interaction between electrically charged particles. It is responsible for light, radio waves, chemical bonds, and electronics. Imagine electricity and magnetism as two sides of the same coin: moving charges create a magnetic field, and a changing magnetic field generates an electric field.
💡 Light is an electromagnetic wave, and different colors correspond to different frequencies of oscillation of electric and magnetic fields.

History

James Clerk Maxwell unified electricity and magnetism into a single theory in the 1860s, predicting electromagnetic waves. Heinrich Hertz experimentally confirmed their existence in 1887. Later, quantum electrodynamics (QED) described the interaction at the quantum level.

How it works

The electromagnetic interaction is mediated by photons. It acts on charged particles such as electrons and protons. Analogy: two magnets can attract or repel — similarly, electric charges behave. Electromagnetism is billions of times stronger than gravity.
