Inertia is the property of bodies to maintain a state of rest or uniform rectilinear motion unless acted upon by external forces. Imagine riding a bus that suddenly brakes: your body continues moving forward — that's inertia.

History

The concept of inertia was formulated by Galileo Galilei in the 17th century and later incorporated by Isaac Newton into his first law of motion. Previously, it was believed that a force was necessary to maintain motion, as taught by Aristotle.

How it works

Inertia manifests in that a force must be applied to change a body's velocity. The greater the mass, the greater the inertia: a heavy truck is harder to accelerate or stop than a light bicycle.
