Inflation

📊 1 articles
This is a brief period of super-fast expansion of the universe right after the Big Bang, when it increased in size by billions of billions of times in a tiny fraction of a second.
💡 Inflation explains why the universe is so homogeneous and flat, although it could have been curved and inhomogeneous initially.
Inflation is a hypothetical stage of exponential expansion of the universe in the first 10^-35 seconds after the Big Bang. It was proposed to solve the horizon, flatness, and magnetic monopole problems. Inflation smoothed out inhomogeneities and created the primordial density fluctuations that later became galaxies.

History

In 1981, Alan Guth proposed the inflationary model to explain why the universe looks so homogeneous. Later, Andrei Linde and others developed more realistic versions, such as chaotic inflation.

How it works

Imagine blowing up a balloon: at first it is small and wrinkled, but then it suddenly inflates, becoming smooth and large. Inflation is such a rapid 'inflation' of the universe.

💡 Inflation lasted only 10^-35 seconds, but during that time the universe expanded by a factor of 10^26—more than in all the subsequent 13.8 billion years.
Inflation is a period of accelerated expansion of the universe, described by a scalar field (the inflaton). During inflation, the scale factor increased exponentially, smoothing out any initial inhomogeneities and curvatures. Quantum fluctuations of the inflaton generated the primordial density perturbations that became the seeds of large-scale structure. Inflation also predicts a specific spectrum of gravitational waves.

History

The idea of inflation was proposed by Alan Guth in 1981 to solve problems of the standard Big Bang model. However, his model had a 'graceful exit' problem. In 1982, Andrei Linde, as well as Andreas Albrecht and Paul Steinhardt, independently developed 'new inflation' models. Later, Linde proposed chaotic inflation, which became the most popular.

How it works

The inflaton is a field with high potential energy that slowly rolls down to a minimum. During rolling, the field causes repulsive gravity, leading to exponential expansion. When the field reaches the minimum, inflation ends, and the field's energy turns into particles (reheating). Quantum fluctuations of the field create inhomogeneities seen in the cosmic microwave background.

Open problems & fun facts

The nature of the inflaton—which field is responsible for inflation—is unknown.; The problem of eternal inflation: in some models, inflation never ends globally, leading to a multiverse.
