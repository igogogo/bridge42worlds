Meteorite

📊 0 articles
A solid body of cosmic origin that has fallen to the Earth's surface. Meteorites are fragments of asteroids, comets, or planets. Imagine a rock that came from space and landed in your yard — that is a meteorite.
💡 The meteorite ALH84001, found in Antarctica, is believed to be from Mars and contains structures that some scientists interpret as fossilized bacteria.

History

Meteorites have been known since antiquity, but their cosmic origin was proven only in 1803 after the fall of the L'Aigle meteorite in France. Ernst Chladni in 1794 suggested that stones fall from the sky. In the 20th century, analysis of meteorites provided clues to the composition of the Solar System and the age of Earth.

How it works

Meteorites are remnants of meteoroids that did not burn up in the atmosphere and reached the surface. Upon entering the atmosphere, they heat up, forming a bright fireball. Meteorites are classified as stony, iron, and stony-iron. Analogy: it is like throwing a stone into water — it flies through the air (atmosphere) and sinks to the bottom.
