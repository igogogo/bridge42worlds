Radio Astronomy

The study of cosmic objects through their radio emission. Like listening to the Universe instead of looking at it. Radio waves penetrate dust and gas, revealing what is invisible in the optical range.

The study of cosmic objects through their radio emission. Like listening to the Universe instead of looking at it. Radio waves penetrate dust and gas, revealing what is invisible in the optical range.

History

Began in 1932 when Karl Jansky discovered radio emission from the Milky Way. Post-WWII radar development gave impetus. Giant radio telescopes like Arecibo and FAST were built.

How it works

Radio telescopes collect radio waves using antennas. The signal is amplified and processed. Radio astronomy allows study of pulsars, quasars, cosmic microwave background, molecular clouds, and active galactic nuclei.
