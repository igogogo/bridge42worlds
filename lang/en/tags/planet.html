History

In antiquity, five planets were known: Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn. Copernicus placed Earth among the planets. Uranus was discovered in 1781, Neptune in 1846. In 2006, the International Astronomical Union redefined the term 'planet', excluding Pluto as a dwarf planet. Thousands of exoplanets are now known.

How it works

Planets form from a protoplanetary disk around a young star: dust clumps into planetesimals, which grow by attracting gas. Rocky planets form closer to the star where it is warm, while gas giants form farther out where ice exists. Planets move in elliptical orbits under the star's gravity. Analogy: it is like water droplets gathering on glass: small droplets merge into larger ones.
