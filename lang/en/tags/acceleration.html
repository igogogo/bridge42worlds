Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity of a body. If you press the gas pedal in a car, the car accelerates, meaning its speed increases — that's acceleration. Even braking is acceleration, only negative.

History

Galileo first mathematically described uniformly accelerated motion by studying falling bodies. Newton then linked acceleration to force and mass in his second law. Later, Einstein in general relativity showed that acceleration is indistinguishable from gravity (equivalence principle).

How it works

Acceleration occurs when a force acts on a body. For example, if you push a cart, it will start moving with acceleration. The greater the force and the smaller the mass, the greater the acceleration. In everyday life, we feel acceleration as a jolt or g-force.
