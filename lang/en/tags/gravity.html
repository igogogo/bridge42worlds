A fundamental interaction that attracts all bodies with mass and energy. It governs the motion of planets, the fall of an apple, and the structure of the universe. Imagine spacetime as a stretched rubber sheet, and massive objects dent it, causing other bodies to roll into the depressions.

History

Isaac Newton formulated the law of universal gravitation in 1687. Albert Einstein replaced it with general relativity in 1915, where gravity is the curvature of spacetime. In 2015, gravitational waves were discovered, confirming Einstein's prediction.

How it works

Gravity acts at any distance, but its strength decreases with the square of the distance. Analogy: the farther you are from a light source, the dimmer it is — similarly, gravity weakens. In GR, massive objects curve spacetime, and bodies move along geodesics.

How it works

Gravity acts at any distance, but its strength decreases with the square of the distance. Analogy: the farther you are from a light source, the dimmer it is — similarly, gravity weakens. In GR, massive objects curve spacetime, and bodies move along geodesics.

F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
The gravitational force between two bodies is proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Open problems & fun facts

Gravity does not fit into quantum mechanics. Creating a quantum theory of gravity is one of the main challenges of modern physics.
