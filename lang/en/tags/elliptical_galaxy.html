Elliptical Galaxy

📊 0 articles
This is a galaxy shaped like an elongated ball, without spiral arms, resembling a giant cloud of old stars.
💡 Elliptical galaxies range from nearly round to highly elongated, like a football.
An elliptical galaxy is a smooth, almost featureless cluster of stars with almost no gas and dust. Imagine a stadium filled with elderly people—there are few young people (new stars). Such galaxies usually contain many old red stars and very little interstellar matter.

History

Edwin Hubble included elliptical galaxies in his classification in 1926. They are thought to form from the merger of spiral galaxies.

How it works

Stars in an elliptical galaxy move chaotically, like bees in a swarm, rather than orderly in circles. Due to the lack of gas, new stars are almost never born, so the galaxy consists mainly of old stars.

💡 The largest galaxies in the universe are elliptical, such as IC 1101, with a diameter of about 4 million light-years.
Elliptical galaxies have shapes ranging from spherical to elongated ellipsoid, without spiral arms or bars. They contain little interstellar gas and dust, so star formation has almost ceased. Stars are mostly old, with low metallicity, and move on random orbits. Elliptical galaxies are divided into classes from E0 (nearly round) to E7 (highly elongated). They are often found in the centers of galaxy clusters.

History

Edwin Hubble introduced elliptical galaxies into his sequence in 1926. For a long time, they were thought to form from spirals after gas depletion. Modern models show they arise from mergers of spiral galaxies, confirmed by observations of merging systems.

How it works

Elliptical galaxies form through mergers of smaller galaxies, during which gas and dust are consumed in a starburst and then disappear. The remaining stars move chaotically, maintaining the elliptical shape through gravity. Without gas, new stars are not born, so the galaxy gradually dims. Dark matter plays an important role in holding the stars together.

Open problems & fun facts

It is unknown why some elliptical galaxies rotate and others do not.; The role of black holes in the centers of elliptical galaxies is not fully understood.
