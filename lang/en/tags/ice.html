Ice

📊 2 articles
Ice is the solid phase of water. It forms when water freezes at 0°C and has the unique property that its density is lower than that of liquid water, so it floats. Imagine an ice cube in a glass: it does not sink but floats on the surface.

History

Ice has been known to humanity since ancient times. In the 19th century, Michael Faraday studied the processes of melting and regelation (freezing under pressure). In the 20th century, various crystalline forms of ice (polymorphs) were discovered under high pressures. In astronomy, ice has been detected on many bodies in the Solar System.

How it works

Ice forms when water loses heat and its molecules arrange into a hexagonal crystal lattice. In space, ice is a major component of comets, icy moons (Europa, Enceladus), and the outer parts of protoplanetary disks. Ice can contain gas impurities (clathrates).
