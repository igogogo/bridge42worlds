Measurement of precise positions and motions of celestial objects. Like cosmic geodesy: we determine the coordinates of stars and their movements across the sky with incredible accuracy.

History

Ancient astronomers measured star positions for calendars. In 1838, Bessel measured a star's parallax, first determining its distance. Modern astrometry uses space telescopes like Gaia.

How it works

Telescopes with high-precision detectors record object coordinates. Comparing images over years measures proper motions and parallaxes. This allows building a 3D map of the Galaxy.
