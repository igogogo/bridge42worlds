Boson

📊 0 articles
A particle with integer spin, obeying Bose-Einstein statistics. Bosons are carriers of fundamental interactions: photon (electromagnetism), gluons (strong), W and Z (weak), graviton (hypothetical). The Higgs boson is a scalar boson. Imagine bosons as mail carriers delivering messages between particles.
💡 Bosons can form a Bose-Einstein condensate—a state of matter where all particles behave as a single giant wave.

History

Statistics for bosons were developed by Satyendra Nath Bose and Albert Einstein in 1924. The photon as a boson was known earlier. W and Z bosons were discovered in 1983, the Higgs boson in 2012. Gluons were postulated in the 1970s.

How it works

Bosons can occupy the same quantum state, leading to phenomena such as lasers and Bose-Einstein condensates. Analogy: in a hall, bosons are people who can sit on the same chair without conflict. Fermions are like people each requiring a separate seat.

Open problems & fun facts

Does the graviton exist? Its detection would require a detector the size of a planet. Also, it is unclear why the Higgs boson has such a small mass.
