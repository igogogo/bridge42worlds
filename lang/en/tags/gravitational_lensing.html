Gravitational Lensing

📊 0 articles
The bending of light from distant objects by the gravity of massive bodies. Like a glass lens, but gravity plays the role of the lens: it can magnify, distort, and even create multiple images.
💡 Gravitational lensing allows us to see galaxies that existed 500 million years after the Big Bang.
The bending of light from distant objects by the gravity of massive bodies. Like a glass lens, but gravity plays the role of the lens: it can magnify, distort, and even create multiple images.

History

Predicted by Einstein's general theory of relativity in 1915. First observation: deflection of starlight during the 1919 solar eclipse. Strong lensing (Einstein rings) was first detected in 1979.

How it works

A massive object (galaxy, cluster) warps spacetime. Light from a source behind it follows curved paths, creating a magnified or distorted image. The effect allows study of very distant and faint objects.
