Quantum Field

📊 0 articles
A fundamental concept in which each particle is an excitation of a corresponding field that permeates all space. For example, an electron is an excitation of the electron field, a photon of the electromagnetic field. Imagine an ocean: waves (particles) are disturbances of the water (field).

A fundamental concept in which each particle is an excitation of a corresponding field that permeates all space. For example, an electron is an excitation of the electron field, a photon of the electromagnetic field. Imagine an ocean: waves (particles) are disturbances of the water (field).

History

Quantum field theory (QFT) emerged in the 1930s from the work of Dirac, Pauli, Feynman, Schwinger, and Tomonaga. It unified quantum mechanics and special relativity. Feynman developed diagrams for calculating interactions. The Standard Model is a quantum field theory for all known particles.

How it works

In QFT, particles are quanta of fields. The field can be in a ground state (vacuum) or an excited state (particle). Interactions occur through the exchange of virtual particles. For example, two electrons repel by exchanging virtual photons. The vacuum is not empty: virtual particle pairs constantly appear and disappear.
