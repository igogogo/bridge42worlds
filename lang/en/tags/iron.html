Iron

Iron is a heavy metal that plays a key role in stellar evolution and life on Earth. Its nucleus is one of the most stable in nature. Imagine holding a nail in your hand: it is strong, heavy, and can rust — that is iron.

Iron is a heavy metal that plays a key role in stellar evolution and life on Earth. Its nucleus is one of the most stable in nature. Imagine holding a nail in your hand: it is strong, heavy, and can rust — that is iron.

History

Iron has been known to humanity since ancient times; the Iron Age began around 1200 BCE. In the 19th century, it was established that iron is the most abundant element in the composition of the Earth (by mass). In astrophysics, iron is the final product of thermonuclear fusion in massive stars.

How it works

In stars, iron is formed through the fusion of silicon and other elements. Further fusion of iron into heavier elements requires energy rather than releasing it, so a star with an iron core collapses, triggering a supernova explosion. On Earth, iron is a component of hemoglobin, which carries oxygen in the blood.
