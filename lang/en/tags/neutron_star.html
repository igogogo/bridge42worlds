Neutron Star

📊 6 articles

History

The existence of neutron stars was predicted in 1934 by Walter Baade and Fritz Zwicky, shortly after the discovery of the neutron. In 1967, Jocelyn Bell Burnell discovered pulsars — rapidly rotating neutron stars emitting radio pulses. This discovery confirmed their existence and earned her supervisor a Nobel Prize.

How it works

When a star with a mass between 8 and 30 solar masses explodes as a supernova, its core collapses, and protons and electrons merge into neutrons. Gravity compresses matter to nuclear density, and the neutron star stabilizes due to degenerate neutron pressure. Analogy: it's like trying to compress a soccer ball to the size of a grain of sand — it would become incredibly hard and dense.
