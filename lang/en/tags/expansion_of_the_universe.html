Expansion of the Universe

📊 2 articles
A fundamental property of the universe, consisting of the increase in distances between galaxies over time. It is not the motion of galaxies in space, but the expansion of space itself. Imagine raisins in rising dough: the raisins (galaxies) move apart from each other, although they themselves do not move.
💡 The expansion of the universe does not mean Earth is moving away from the Sun: gravity holds the solar system together, and expansion manifests only on intergalactic scales.

History

Discovered by Edwin Hubble in 1929 based on observations of galactic redshifts. Theoretically predicted by Alexander Friedmann (1922) and Georges Lemaître (1927). In 1998, it was discovered that the expansion is accelerating (dark energy). Modern measurements of the Hubble constant give different values (Hubble tension).

How it works

Hubble's law: the recession velocity of a galaxy is proportional to its distance. Redshift arises from the stretching of light waves as space expands. The expansion has no center: all points move away from each other. On small scales (within galaxies), gravity overcomes expansion, so they do not expand.
