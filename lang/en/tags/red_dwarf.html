A red dwarf is a star the size of Jupiter, with hydrogen burning slowly inside. It will almost never cool down.
💡 Red dwarfs live for trillion years, longer than the entire Universe.
A red dwarf is the most common type of star. It smolders like slow coal rather than blazing fiercely.

History

The class was first identified by Einar Hertzsprung in the early 20th century. Systematic study of red dwarfs — by Willem Luyten.

How it works

In the interior, helium is slowly synthesized from hydrogen. Convection carries heat outward, like in a boiling pot.

A red dwarf is a low-mass main-sequence star of spectral class M. Less than 0.6 solar masses, surface temperature 2500–4000 K. Fully convective, which ensures complete burning of hydrogen. Lifetime exceeds the age of the Universe.

History

Einari Hertzsprung pointed out the difference between giants and dwarfs in 1905. The classification of red dwarfs as class M was developed later. The study of their spectra and luminosities continues.

How it works

Thermonuclear reactions of the proton-proton chain occur at low temperatures. The star is in hydrostatic equilibrium. Energy transfer is by convection. Due to their low luminosity, they are hard to observe.
