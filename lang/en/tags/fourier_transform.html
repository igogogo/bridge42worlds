Fourier Transform

📊 0 articles
The Fourier transform is a mathematical method that breaks down any complex signal (e.g., music) into simple sinusoidal waves of different frequencies.
💡 MP3 files use the Fourier transform for audio compression: they discard inaudible high frequencies.
The Fourier transform is an integral transform that decomposes a function (signal) into a sum of sinusoidal components with different frequencies. It converts a function from the time domain to the frequency domain. For example, an audio signal can be represented as a set of frequencies (notes). The inverse Fourier transform reconstructs the original signal.

History

Jean Baptiste Joseph Fourier introduced Fourier series in 1822 to solve the heat equation. Later, the integral Fourier transform was developed. In the 20th century, the fast Fourier transform (FFT) became key for digital signal processing.

How it works

Imagine you are listening to an orchestra. The Fourier transform is like an 'auditory analyzer' that decomposes the sound into individual notes: violin plays A 440 Hz, trumpet plays C 523 Hz, etc. Mathematically, it is the integral of the product of the signal and a complex exponential e^{-iωt}.

💡 The fast Fourier transform (FFT) is one of the most important algorithms of the 20th century, used in image compression (JPEG), audio processing, and even cryptography.
The Fourier transform is an integral transform defined as F(ω) = ∫_{-∞}^{∞} f(t) e^{-iωt} dt. It converts a function f(t) from the time domain to the frequency domain F(ω). Properties: linearity, shift, convolution (convolution theorem). Inverse transform: f(t) = (1/2π) ∫ F(ω) e^{iωt} dω. The Fourier transform generalizes to multidimensional functions, distributions, and groups (harmonic analysis).

History

Jean Baptiste Joseph Fourier published 'Analytical Theory of Heat' in 1822, introducing Fourier series. Augustin-Louis Cauchy and other mathematicians developed the integral transform. In 1965, James Cooley and John Tukey published the fast Fourier transform (FFT) algorithm, revolutionizing digital signal processing. Today, the Fourier transform is used everywhere: from physics to finance.

How it works

The Fourier transform exists for functions in L^1(ℝ) and L^2(ℝ). It can be defined on the space of generalized functions (distributions). The discrete Fourier transform (DFT) applies to finite sequences. The FFT is an efficient algorithm for computing the DFT in O(N log N) operations. The Fourier transform is a unitary operator on L^2(ℝ) (up to normalization).

F(\omega) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t) e^{-i\omega t} dt
The Fourier transform converts a function f(t) into a function F(ω), where ω is the angular frequency.
f(t) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} F(\omega) e^{i\omega t} d\omega
Reconstructs the original function f(t) from its spectrum F(ω).

Open problems & fun facts

Development of efficient algorithms for non-harmonic analysis (e.g., wavelet transforms).; Phase retrieval problem: recovering the original signal from the magnitude of its Fourier transform.
