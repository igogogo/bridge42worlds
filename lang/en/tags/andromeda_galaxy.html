The nearest large spiral galaxy to us, located 2.5 million light-years away. It's like a neighboring city visible to the naked eye, but impossible to reach with modern spacecraft.

History

Known since antiquity as a fuzzy patch. In 1923, Edwin Hubble determined its distance, proving it is a separate galaxy, not a nebula in the Milky Way.

How it works

Andromeda contains about a trillion stars and is moving toward the Milky Way at about 110 km/s. Gravity is pulling the galaxies together, and in billions of years they will merge into one elliptical galaxy.
