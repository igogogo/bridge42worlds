Krypton

Krypton (Kr) is an inert, colorless, odorless gas found in the atmosphere in trace amounts (0.0001%). In an electrical discharge, it glows pale violet. Like a 'hidden' element: its name means 'hidden', and it indeed long evaded detection. Used in lamps and lasers.
💡 Krypton is so rare that its total amount in Earth's atmosphere is estimated at only a few billion tons — roughly the mass of a small asteroid.

History

Krypton was discovered in 1898 by William Ramsay and Morris Travers during the evaporation of liquid air. They noticed spectral lines of a new gas, which they named 'krypton' (from Greek 'kryptos' — hidden). Later, krypton was used in the definition of the meter: from 1960 to 1983, the meter was defined by the wavelength of the orange line of krypton-86 isotope.

How it works

Krypton, like other noble gases, does not undergo chemical reactions due to its filled electron shell. However, it can form unstable compounds with fluorine. In lamps, krypton allows for brighter light and greater efficiency compared to argon. It is also used in excimer lasers for surgery and in double-glazed windows for thermal insulation.
