quintessence

# tag · @ author · ! scientist

Quintessence is a hypothetical form of dark energy that can change over time. If dark energy is a sea, then quintessence is a wave that waxes and wanes.

In practice: Helps understand why the Universe is expanding at an accelerating rate and predict its ultimate fate — whether it will be a Big Rip or eternal cooling.
Quintessence is a hypothetical form of dark energy with variable density, described by a scalar field. Unlike Einstein's cosmological constant (Λ), which is unchanging, quintessence can evolve in time and space. The equation of state of such energy is characterized by the parameter w = p/ρ (ratio of pressure to density), which for quintessence lies in the range -1 < w < -1/3. When w = -1, it is indistinguishable from Λ, and values close to -1/3 lead to a slowdown of the acceleration. Observations have not yet confirmed deviations of w from -1, but allow for subtle dynamics.

History

The word 'quintessence' goes back to ancient philosophy — the fifth element after earth, water, air, and fire. In cosmology, it was revived in 1998 when astronomers discovered that the expansion of the Universe was accelerating. Scientists suggested that this was not due to a constant cosmological constant, but to a dynamic field, named quintessence.

How it works

Quintessence is like a spring that slowly unwinds. As the field 'rolls' down the energy slope, its pressure is negative, and it acts like antigravity, pushing galaxies apart. If the field freezes, acceleration stops; if it continues to change, the Universe may face the tearing apart of matter.

💡 The name 'fifth element energy' refers to the ancient Greek notion that the heavens consist of a special substance — ether, or quintessence. Modern physics may be bringing this idea back on scientific grounds.
Quintessence is a special energy that fills space and causes the Universe to expand faster and faster. Unlike ordinary dark energy, which is considered constant (like a steady hum), quintessence can weaken or strengthen in different places and at different times. It's as if the air in a room became denser or thinner, affecting the movement of objects. The idea came from particle physics: there is a field (an invisible substance, similar to a magnetic field) that slowly changes and creates repulsive gravity.

Quintessence in cosmology is a model of dark energy based on a scalar field ϕ with canonical kinetic term and potential V(ϕ). The action for such a field in curved spacetime yields an energy-momentum tensor that, for slowly varying field, provides negative pressure, causing accelerated expansion of the Universe. The Friedmann equation for the scale factor a(t) is modified with quintessence, and the field dynamics determine the evolution of dark energy. Possible potentials include exponential V(ϕ) ∝ exp(-λϕ) or inverse power-law V(ϕ) ∝ ϕ^(-α). The difference from models with a cosmological constant lies in the absence of the 'coincidence' problem (why are the densities of matter and dark energy comparable today) thanks to tracking mechanisms. Observational constraints from supernovae, cosmic microwave background, and baryon acoustic oscillations currently agree with w ≈ -1, but future missions may detect small deviations.

History

After the discovery of the accelerated expansion of the Universe in 1998 by groups led by Saul Perlmutter, Adam Riess, and Brian Schmidt, the question arose about the nature of dark energy. Although the cosmological constant was the simplest explanation, theorists including Paul Steinhardt and Robert Caldwell proposed models of a dynamic scalar field — quintessence. The term became established in the literature as a contrast to the static Λ. The idea is supported by attempts to link dark energy with the inflationary field in the early Universe.

How it works

The dynamics of quintessence obey the Klein-Gordon equation in an expanding Universe: ϕ̈ + 3Hϕ̇ + dV/dϕ = 0, where H is the Hubble parameter. The braking due to expansion causes the field to roll slowly, preserving the potential energy, which dominates and creates negative pressure. The energy density and pressure of the field: ρ_ϕ = ϕ̇²/2 + V(ϕ), p_ϕ = ϕ̇²/2 - V(ϕ). When V(ϕ) ≫ ϕ̇², we get p_ϕ ≈ -ρ_ϕ, which leads to acceleration. Quintessence models must solve the fifth force problem, since field variations could manifest in laboratory experiments, so it is usually assumed that the field interacts very weakly with matter.

w = \frac{p}{\rho}
w — equation of state parameter, p — pressure, ρ — energy density; for quintessence -1 < w < -1/3
