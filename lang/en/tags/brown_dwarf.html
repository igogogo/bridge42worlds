Brown Dwarf

📊 0 articles
A celestial body occupying an intermediate position between a planet and a star. It is too massive to be a planet, but not massive enough to sustain hydrogen fusion. Like a teenager who hasn't grown into an adult but is no longer a child.
💡 Brown dwarfs are not brown—they emit in the infrared and appear reddish.
A celestial body occupying an intermediate position between a planet and a star. It is too massive to be a planet, but not massive enough to sustain hydrogen fusion. Like a teenager who hasn't grown into an adult but is no longer a child.

History

The existence of brown dwarfs was predicted by Shiv Kumar in the 1960s. The first confirmed brown dwarf, Teide 1, was discovered in 1995. Thousands are now known.

How it works

A brown dwarf forms from a gas and dust cloud like a star, but its mass is less than 0.08 solar masses. Deuterium fusion may occur in its interior, but not hydrogen fusion. It gradually cools and dims.
