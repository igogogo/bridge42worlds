A fundamental concept of general relativity, according to which mass and energy curve spacetime, and gravity is a manifestation of this curvature. Imagine a heavy ball on a rubber sheet: it bends it, and light balls roll toward it — that is gravity.

History

Albert Einstein developed general relativity (1915), replacing Newtonian gravity with geometry. Key point — the equivalence principle. First confirmation — deflection of starlight near the Sun during the 1919 eclipse (Eddington). Later, the theory was confirmed by many experiments, including gravitational lensing.

How it works

Mass tells spacetime how to curve, and curvature tells mass how to move. Light also follows geodesics — the shortest paths in curved space. The more massive the object, the stronger the curvature. Near a black hole, curvature is so strong that even light cannot escape.
