Gas Giant

📊 0 articles
This is a huge planet consisting mainly of gas, with no solid surface, like Jupiter and Saturn in our solar system.
💡 Jupiter is so large that more than 1,300 Earths could fit inside it.
A gas giant is a planet composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, with a small rocky core. Imagine a huge ball of gas that becomes denser toward the center. Such planets have no solid surface to land on. They also have powerful atmospheres and systems of rings and moons.

History

Gas giants have been known since antiquity (Jupiter, Saturn). Their nature as planets without a solid surface was understood with the development of astronomy in the 17th–18th centuries.

How it works

Gravity compresses gas in the center, creating high pressure and temperature. Hydrogen can transition to a metallic state, generating a magnetic field. The outer layers form bands and storms, like the Great Red Spot on Jupiter.

💡 Saturn has the fastest winds in the solar system—speeds can reach 1800 km/h.
A gas giant is a giant planet composed predominantly of hydrogen and helium, with a mass greater than 10 Earth masses. It has no solid surface; below the atmosphere, the gas transitions to a supercritical fluid. In the interior, hydrogen under high pressure becomes metallic, generating a powerful magnetic field. Gas giants have many moons and rings. Examples: Jupiter, Saturn, and many exoplanets such as hot Jupiters.

History

Jupiter and Saturn have been known since antiquity. In 1610, Galileo discovered their moons. In the 20th century, the study of gas giants advanced with space probes (Voyagers, Galileo, Cassini). The discovery of exoplanets showed that gas giants are common in other star systems.

How it works

A gas giant forms from a protoplanetary disk, accreting gas onto a solid core. Internal pressure compresses hydrogen, turning it into liquid and then metallic. Convection in the atmosphere creates bands and vortices. The magnetic field is generated in the metallic hydrogen. The planet emits more heat than it receives from the star due to gravitational contraction.

Open problems & fun facts

The exact composition and structure of gas giant cores are unknown.; The formation mechanism of hot Jupiters on close orbits remains unclear.
