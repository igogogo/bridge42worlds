History

In ancient times, the Milky Way was considered a milky road of gods. In the 18th century, William Herschel made the first map of our galaxy. In the 1920s, Edwin Hubble proved that the Andromeda Nebula is a separate galaxy, not a nebula within the Milky Way, expanding the boundaries of the universe.

How it works

Galaxies form from giant clouds of gas and dark matter that collapse under gravity. Stars are born inside galaxies, and their motion obeys the laws of gravity. However, visible mass does not explain the rotation speed of stars at the outskirts — dark matter is needed. Analogy: a galaxy is like a whirlpool, where stars are chips, and dark matter is the water, invisible but creating the current.
