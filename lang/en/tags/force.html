Force is a vector quantity that causes a change in a body's velocity or its deformation. Simply put, it's a push or a pull. When you kick a ball, your foot applies a force to it, and the ball flies.

History

The concept of force was formalized by Isaac Newton in his laws of motion (1687). Before him, Aristotle believed that force was necessary to maintain motion, but Newton showed that force is needed to change motion. In the 20th century, four fundamental interactions were discovered: gravitational, electromagnetic, strong, and weak.

How it works

Force can be contact (e.g., a push by hand) or act at a distance (gravity, magnetism). If multiple forces act on a body, they can be added vectorially. The resultant force determines how the body's motion changes.
