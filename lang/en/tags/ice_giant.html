Ice Giant

📊 0 articles
This is a planet composed mainly of water, methane, and ammonia in the form of ice and liquid, like Uranus and Neptune.
💡 Uranus and Neptune are called ice giants because they contain more 'ices' (water, methane, ammonia) than gases.
An ice giant is a type of giant planet where the main components are water, methane, and ammonia, rather than hydrogen and helium. Imagine a planet where instead of a solid surface there is an ocean of liquid water under high pressure, and the atmosphere contains methane giving a blue color. Uranus and Neptune are examples in the solar system.

History

Uranus was discovered in 1781 by William Herschel, Neptune in 1846 by mathematical calculations. In the 20th century, they were classified as ice giants.

How it works

Below an atmosphere of hydrogen and helium lies a mantle of water, methane, and ammonia in the form of an ionic liquid. The pressure there is enormous, but not enough to make hydrogen metallic. This distinguishes them from gas giants.

💡 Neptune has the strongest winds among the planets in the solar system—up to 2100 km/h.
An ice giant is a giant planet with a mass from 10 to 20 Earth masses, composed mainly of 'ices' (water, methane, ammonia) and having a thick atmosphere of hydrogen and helium. Unlike gas giants, they have no metallic hydrogen; their interiors consist of an ionic liquid. Uranus and Neptune are the only known ice giants in the solar system. They have a bluish color due to methane absorption of red light.

History

Uranus was discovered by William Herschel in 1781. Neptune was predicted by Urbain Le Verrier and discovered by Johann Galle in 1846. In the 1980s, Voyager 2 flew past both planets, providing detailed data. In the 2010s, they were classified as ice giants, distinct from gas giants.

How it works

An ice giant forms from a protoplanetary disk, accreting a significant amount of ices. Below the H2/He atmosphere is a mantle of water, methane, and ammonia in a supercritical state. The pressure in the mantle reaches several million atmospheres, but is insufficient for hydrogen metallization. Internal heat is released by gravitational contraction, but less than in gas giants.

Open problems & fun facts

The internal structure of ice giants is poorly understood due to the lack of orbital missions.; The anomalous magnetic field of Uranus (tilted and offset) has no explanation.

💡 Neptune has the strongest winds among the planets in the solar system—up to 2100 km/h.
