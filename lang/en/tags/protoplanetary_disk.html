A rotating disk of gas and dust around a young star, from which planets form. It's like when dough spins on a table to form a flat cake—only in space, worlds are born from this 'cake'.

History

The idea of such disks originated from the Kant-Laplace theory (18th century). First direct observations became possible in the 1990s with the Hubble Space Telescope. They are now studied with ALMA and JWST.

How it works

Gas and dust in the disk collide and stick together, forming larger bodies—planetesimals. Gravity gathers them into protoplanets, and leftover gas either escapes or becomes atmospheres. The whole process takes millions of years.
