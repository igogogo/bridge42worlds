bridge42worlds in five minutes

Every day, fresh science from arXiv becomes text anyone can read. Here's how it all works — before you dive in.

What this is

Today's science — in plain words

Every day arXiv — the largest open library of new science — publishes hundreds of papers. We take their full text, hand it to an AI, and ask for one honest thing: explain it clearly. Not shorter, not louder — just clear.

Every article comes out in several languages and at four depths. All free, non-commercial, forever. We adapt a paper only when its license allows it.

The core idea

One paper — four depths

Not "short" and "full", but a ladder. Start wherever you're comfortable and go deeper if it grabs you. The depth switch sits at the top of every article.

How to search

Three ways to find what you need

The 🔍 button in the header. Plain text searches articles, and three prefixes go straight to the point:

# tag @ author ! scientist

For example, #black_hole — articles on the topic, @Hawking — by author, !Einstein — linked to a scientist. Next to it, the 📅 Calendar: year → month → day — jump back to any date. And the sections filter narrows the feed to the arXiv fields you want.

The special part

Wander through knowledge, don't just read it

Every article links to topics; topics link to laws and scientists; scientists link back to topics. It's one knowledge graph. On any tag, law, or scientist page there's a mini-map of its surroundings — with a depth dial + / −. And on the graph page — the whole map at once, with filters and search across all three types.

The practical point: you came for one article about neutron stars — and left understanding how they connect to general relativity, to the effect that describes it, and to the people who discovered it.

your own paper

Send us your work

You can send your paper directly to article@bridge42worlds.academy. We check it with AI: whether it is novel and whether it fits a section. If it does, the work may be published here in our processed form. Please format it to the usual standards. We reply either way, and it is free.

Submitted work must carry the same licence we use ourselves: CC BY 4.0.

cadence and feedback

What's next

For now we cover the top 10 papers a day, with a focus on physics. Next comes the full stream, unfiltered.

The project can be adapted to a concrete need: connect new sources where their licence allows it, add a language, tune the selection to a given field. Proposals go to propose@bridge42worlds.academy.

Cooperation with educational institutions is entirely free. We are glad to take your wishes and requirements into account: add new sources, or adapt the feed to your programme, as far as our resources allow. Very specific tasks may also call for your own help with the development, under open-source licences.
