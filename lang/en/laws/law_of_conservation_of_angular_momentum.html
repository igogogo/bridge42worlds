Law of conservation of angular momentum: in a closed system, the vector sum of the angular momenta of the particles does not change over time. Discovery

Johannes Kepler formulated his second law (1609) — the radius vector of a planet sweeps out equal areas in equal times, which is a consequence of conservation of angular momentum. Isaac Newton in the 'Principia' derived the conservation from the laws of motion and gravity. In 1915, Emmy Noether proved a general theorem linking symmetries and conservation laws, in particular, the isotropy of space to the conservation of angular momentum.
