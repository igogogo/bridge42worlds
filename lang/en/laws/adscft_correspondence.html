AdS/CFT correspondenceprinciple

Gravity inside a curved volume is an exact translation of quantum physics on its surface, like a 3D movie projected from a flat screen.

In practice: Provides a tool for calculating strong interactions in nuclear physics and simulating black holes.
In 1997, Argentine physicist Juan Maldacena, while studying string theory, discovered an exact duality between supergravity in AdS5 × S⁵ space and supersymmetric gauge theory on the four-dimensional boundary. The correspondence stunned the scientific world: multidimensional gravity, including black holes, turned out to be 'encoded' in ordinary quantum field theory without gravity. The relation (R/ℓ_s)⁴ = 4πg_sN links the AdS radius (R), the string length (ℓ_s), the coupling constant (g_s), and the rank of the gauge group (N). Thus geometry becomes a language for strong quantum interactions.
💡 Thanks to AdS/CFT, physicists have simulated what a black hole 'sounds' like: its vibrations reflect processes in a quantum liquid.
AdS/CFT correspondence is a surprising connection: a very complex quantum field theory (CFT — conformal field theory, i.e., a theory with scale symmetry where there is no distinguished size) living on the boundary of space is completely equivalent to a theory of gravity in the bulk of that space (AdS — anti-de Sitter space, curved so that parallel rays diverge). Imagine a drum: the sounds and vibrations of its surface convey all information about its interior — just like that, the quantum theory on the boundary 'sounds' like gravity inside.

How it works

This principle is used when a direct solution of quantum field theory is impossible due to strong interactions, but the gravitational problem turns out to be simpler. Example: the properties of quark-gluon plasma at the Large Hadron Collider are calculated via black holes in extra dimensions.

💡 AdS/CFT correspondence made it possible to compute the entropy viscosity for quark-gluon plasma (η/s ≈ 1/4π), which turned out to be close to experimental data from the RHIC collider — the first successful application of string theory to experiment.
AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena duality) is an exact holographic connection between string theory (or quantum gravity) on anti-de Sitter space (AdS) and conformal field theory (CFT) on the conformal boundary of that space. In the simplest example — duality between type IIB superstrings on AdS5 × S⁵ and N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with gauge group SU(N). The key relation (R/ℓ_s)⁴ = 4πg_sN = g²_YM N links geometric parameters (radius R, string length ℓ_s) with gauge ones (coupling constant g_YM, rank N). Strong coupling in CFT corresponds to weak curvature in AdS, which allows computing non-perturbative effects in quantum field theory via classical gravity.

Discovery

The idea of a connection between field theory and gravity goes back to the works of Gerard 't Hooft and Leonard Susskind on the holographic principle (1993–95), as well as studies of black holes in string theory. Juan Maldacena in 1997, analyzing the dynamics of D-branes, formulated the exact AdS/CFT duality. Edward Witten and others developed the mathematical apparatus, proving the consistency of symmetries. Soon, Po Li, A. Strominger, and C. Vafa used it for the microscopic counting of black hole entropy. Today it is one of the most cited ideas in theoretical physics.

How it works

The duality is weak-strong: when gravity in AdS is described classically (weak curvature), the dual CFT interacts strongly. This allows replacing complex quantum calculations with solving Einstein's equations. It is applied to the physics of quark-gluon plasma, condensed matter (AdS/CMT), and even to quantum information. Boundaries: so far it has been proved only for AdS spaces with a negative cosmological constant; generalization to de Sitter space (positive cosm. constant) is an open problem. Also, at finite N and non-zero ℓ_s, deviations from classical gravity arise.

Caveats

How to generalize the duality to realistic de Sitter space (our Universe)?; What happens in the regime of finite N and small AdS radius, where string effects are strong?; Is there a rigorous mathematical proof of the duality beyond supersymmetric cases?
