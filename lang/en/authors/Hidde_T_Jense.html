Axions: How Few of Them Could Be Hiding in the Universe

Axions are ultralight particles that can zip through a light-year of lead. They were considered top candidates for dark matter, but new data almost rule out that possibility. For a certain mass, axions make up less than 1.5%, and a weak hint of a signal is likely just noise.

Fossil Traces of Inflationary Particles in Petrified Light

Power spectra from the Planck satellite and ACT telescope were analyzed for anomalies caused by particle production during inflation. A faint (~2σ) signal was found at scales of 3–10 Mpc, which does not yet reach discovery level but aligns with the discrepancy in spectral index between experiments.
