Cosmic Hide-and-Seek: Webb Spots a Planet Near a Neighboring Star

At Alpha Centauri A, the nearest Sun-like star, Webb briefly picked up a dot called S1 — a planet candidate the size of Jupiter but with a temperature around –20°C. The object hid behind the star — its orbit lasts just 2–3 years, and there’s remarkably little dust around.
