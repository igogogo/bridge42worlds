Noise Gives Birth to Quantum Order

Physicists have discovered that coordinated noise creates a stable pattern of particles moving strictly in one direction. It resembles a stadium wave frozen in one direction. The finding overturns the idea that interference always ruins quantum effects: noise can be the architect of order.
