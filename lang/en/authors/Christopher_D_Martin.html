A Spinning Top at the Galaxy’s Core Snuffs Out Stars

Scientists studied the galaxy VV 340a, where the jet from its central black hole wobbles with a period of about 800,000 years. It sweeps away gas—roughly 19 solar masses per year. The superheated gas leaves the galaxy, and star formation shuts down.
