Record Gamma-Ray Burst: 7 Hours of Invisible Light

GRB 250702B broke all the rules: its 7-hour duration didn't fit old models. The solution? A merger of a black hole with a helium star. This event links ultra-long gamma-ray bursts with supernovae and gravitational waves.
