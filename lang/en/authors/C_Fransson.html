Caught by a Stellar Kick: Webb Finds a Neutron Star

Astronomers have searched for decades for the object left behind by supernova 1987A. Webb finally saw it: a neutron star that is accelerating gas and flying sideways. By measuring its displacement, scientists determined its speed — 500 km/s, as if someone gave it a stellar 'kick'.
