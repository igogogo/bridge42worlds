18 Years Later: A Supernova Lights Up the Radio

Scientists recorded radio emission from supernova SN 2007it, which flared in 2007. The signal arrived 18 years later: all that time, the shock wave was pushing through rarefied space until it hit a dense gas cloud. The discovery clarifies how massive stars lose mass before exploding.
