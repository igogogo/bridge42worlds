Magnetic Mosaic: How Non-Repeating Patterns Trap Tiny Quantum Waves

Scientists placed a model of a quantum spin liquid on a quasicrystal and found that a weak magnetic field forces anyons—particles with fractional charge—to move in closed loops or freeze entirely. This proves that geometric patterns can control exotic particles, promising for quantum computers.
