Алмазный дефект управляет светом и помнит квантовую тайну

A Diamond Defect Steered by Light Remembers a Quantum Secret

Scientists turned a tiny defect in diamond—a nickel atom with missing neighbors—into an all-optical qubit. It's controlled by a laser, emits in the infrared, and holds a quantum state 3400 times longer than usual, running in a standard fridge.
