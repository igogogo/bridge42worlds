Density Staircases Birth Neutrinos Inside Neutron Stars

Just as a strong electric field rips electron-positron pairs out of the vacuum, steep density "steps" deep inside neutron stars create neutrinos and antineutrinos. By catching these messenger particles, scientists can probe superdense matter beyond the reach of any telescope.
