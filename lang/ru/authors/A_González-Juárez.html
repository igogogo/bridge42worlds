Weighing the invisible: how light reveals the secrets of black holes

By comparing the redshift and blueshift of light from two symmetric points in orbit, scientists can calculate the mass and distance to a black hole. Gravity distorts the color, but a pair of beams allows its influence to be isolated. The method works even if the system is moving.
