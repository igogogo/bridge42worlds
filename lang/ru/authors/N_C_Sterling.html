Icy Surprise from a Dying Star: JWST Finds CO2 Crystals

Astronomers using the James Webb Space Telescope have found carbon dioxide ice for the first time in the planetary nebula NGC 6302. A dust ring, like a mitten, protects the crystals from ultraviolet radiation. The discovery rewrites chemical models of dying stars.
