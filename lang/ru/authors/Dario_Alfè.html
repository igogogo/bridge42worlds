Carbon nanotubes with calcium trap hydrogen

Hydrogen fuel is clean, but keeping it in a tank is tricky—molecules are too small and slip away. Scientists found a fix: put calcium atoms inside carbon tubes so they snag hydrogen like Velcro. This brings us closer to lightweight, safe hydrogen tanks for cars.
