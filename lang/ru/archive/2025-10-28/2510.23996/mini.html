Multiple taps on a chip force sound waves into a one-way whisper, slashing noise for ultra-sensitive rotation sensing.

A gyroscope that hears rotation: on a tiny chip, sound waves are forced into a one-way lane, like a whisper traveling in only one direction around a dome. By tapping the wave at several points, noise nearly vanishes, revealing spins so faint they were once hidden. Soon, this quantum trick could steer drones and spacecraft with uncanny precision.

🎯 Sound, like light, comes in packets—phonons. This gyroscope can detect rotation using just a few of them, making it exquisitely sensitive.

🎬 The precision of such gyroscopes could one day guide spacecraft through asteroid fields as smoothly as the navigators in 'The Expanse' plot complex courses, though here it's about real-world physics.
