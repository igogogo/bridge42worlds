A1 Palomar 60-inch telescope
The SEDM on P60 (
Blagorodnova et al. 2018 ) was triggered via
the web interface on Fritz ( van der W alt et al. 2019 ; Coughlin et al.
2023) to obtain spectroscopy of AT 2024wpp almost immediately
after its discovery . Observations begain at MJD 60579.4667 , 2.3
hours after the ﬁrst alert (and within 1 hour after the second alert
caused the candidate to pass the Bright Transient Survey sof tware
ﬁlter). The initial trigger was for spectroscopic observat ions as part of
the Bright Transient Survey . An /u1D45F-band acquisition image of the ﬁeld
using the SEDM Rainbow Camera was obtained before the telesc ope
proceeded to spectroscopy using the central IFU. Additiona l Rainbow
Camera imaging was obtained on subsequent nights in /u1D462, /u1D454, /u1D45F, and /u1D456
ﬁlters.
Imaging observations were automatically processed and ima ge
diﬀerenced against Pan-STARRS referencing imaging using t he P60
pipeline, following the method of Fremling et al. (2016). The ﬂux of
the source was determined via PSF photometry .
The IFU observations were processed and the source spectrum ex-
tracted using the SEDM data reduction pipeline ( Rigault et al. 2019 ;
Kim et al. 2022 ). Unfortunately , the early spectrum was aﬀected by
camera artefacts due to a recurring detector malfunction an d is not
useable.
A2 Liverpool T elescope
A2.1 IO:O
Observations were taken with the IO:O optical imager on the Liver-
pool T elescope (
Steele et al. 2004 ) in the Sloan Digital Sky Survey
(SDSS) /u1D462, /u1D454, /u1D45F, /u1D456, and /u1D467ﬁlters. Reduced images were downloaded from
the telescope archive and processed with custom image-subt raction
and analysis software. Image stacking and alignment is perf ormed
using SW /a.pc/r.pc/p.pc(Bertin 2010 ) where required. Image subtraction on /u1D454,
/u1D45F, /u1D456, and /u1D467observations is performed using a pre-explosion reference
image in the appropriate ﬁlter from Pan-STARRS1. Photometr y in
these bands was measured using PSF ﬁtting methodology relat ive
to Pan-STARRS1 or SDSS standards and is based on techniques
described in
Fremling et al. (2016).
For the SDSS /u1D462-band, the source location was not covered by
SDSS and as a result image subtraction could not be performed .
Aperture photometry was performed using sep (Barbary 2016 ), a
python implementation of SExtractor(Bertin & Arnouts 1996 ). A
2.5" aperture was used to maximise ﬂux measured from the tran sient
whilst avoiding contamination from the nearby host galaxy . The /u1D462-
band airmass coeﬃcient was measured using two observations of
the standard star 114-654 ( Landolt 1992 ) on the night of 2024-09-
30. This standard star was then used to measure the brightnes s of
the star BD-17 520, present in our /u1D462-band images of AT 2024wpp,
which was then used to calibrate subsequent epochs. For our ﬁ nal
two epochs BD-17 520 was saturated. The star SDSS J024201.54 -
165333.8, close to the edge of our frames with photometry mea sured
by SDSS, was visible in the deeper images and used instead. Th e
magnitudes of these secondary standard stars (and others re levant for
calibrating other observations with small numbers of viabl e stars in
the ﬁeld) are provided in T able A1.
A2.2 LIRIC
Near infrared data was taken with LIRIC on the Liverpool T ele-
scope in the FELH1500 ﬁlter and Barr J ﬁlter. The FELH1500 ﬁlt er
combined with the sCMOS detector result in a pseudo H-band ﬁl ter
(
Batty et al. 2022 ).
The observations were dithered using a nine-position large oﬀset
pattern. The ﬁrst set of observations was a 100 ×30s exposure in the
H band taken on MJD 60588.103229. The second set of observa-
tions was taken at MJD 60594.109514 in H band (20×30s) and J
band (16 ×30s). In an attempt to ensure suﬃcient bright stars were
contained within the sequence for photometric calibration , not all
dither positions contained the target.
The data was reduced manually , following the usual steps: th e raw
images were bias subtracted using the available overscan, a nd sky
subtracted using three nearest exposures on each side of an i mage.
They were stacked manually due to the lack of bright sources i n most
of the exposures; the stack shifts were based on known pixel o ﬀsets
per each dither and a model of telescope wobble during the dur ation
of the observations. The aperture photometry was performed with
SExtractorand calibrated using the 2MASS All-Sky Point Source
Catalog (
Skrutskie et al. 2003 ).
For the ﬁrst night, we recover a source of magnitude /u1D43B=
18.34+0.35
−0.30 , for a total exposure of 900s on the source. For the second
night, we obtain 3 /u1D70Eupper limits of /u1D43B >18.67 mag (180 s exposure
on the source) and /u1D43D >16.10 mag. J-band images did not have bright
objects in the dithered ﬁelds around AT 2024wpp and could not be
stacked, so the upper limit is given for a single 30s exposure .
