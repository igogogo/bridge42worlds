Original: "Resolving the black hole sphere of influence in a hyper-luminous obscured quasar at redshift 4.6"
Полная версия готовится
Аннотация

Using high-resolution observations of carbon gas, scientists have directly measured the mass of a supermassive black hole in a galaxy from when the Universe was just 1.3 billion years old. They found the black hole weighs 6.3 billion solar masses, causing gas to move faster closer to the center. This breakthrough was possible because the black hole is shrouded in dust, which protects the carbon gas from being destroyed. It’s like measuring the heft of an invisible dancer by the swirl of their partner’s skirt. Such obscured quasars may be key to mapping early black hole growth.
