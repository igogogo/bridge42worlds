Original: "Training a neural network to rapidly identify candidate gravitational-wave events in the lower mass gap"
A neural network instantly decodes gravitational waves to reveal hidden objects in the mass gap between neutron stars and black holes.

A 'missing link' between neutron stars and black holes has long puzzled astronomers. Now, a neural network can spot these cosmic middleweights in seconds from gravitational waves. Quick detection means telescopes might catch the flash of light from a rare neutron star merger, unlocking secrets of how heavy elements like gold are made.
