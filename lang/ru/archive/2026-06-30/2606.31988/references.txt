A3 Time conditioning
The diffusion time𝑡∈[0,1]is encoded with a sinusoidal frequency
embedding followed by a two-layer MLP, producing a conditioning
vector𝑐(𝑡) ∈R 𝑑. This vector modulates every transformer block
and the field predictor head via adaLN-Zero (Peebles & Xie 2023).
Inside each block, the scale, shift, and residual gate parameters of
the attention and MLP sublayers are predicted as affine functions of
𝑐(𝑡).TheadaLNprojectionsareinitializedtozerosothateachblock
starts as an identity mapping at the beginning of training, which has
been shown to stabilize the optimization of diffusion transformers
(Peebles & Xie 2023).
A4 Initialization
LinearlayersareinitializedwithXavieruniforminitialization(Glorot
& Bengio 2010). Following Peebles & Xie (2023) and Li & He
(2026), the adaLN modulation layers and the final predictor layers
are zero-initialized, so that the network initially predicts the clean
sample equal to the noisy input (and the corresponding velocity is
the identity interpolation), providing a stable starting point for flow
matching training.
