APPENDIX
A.CREATING AN IONIZED ABSORBER MODEL
Previous studies on ionized absorption modeling in AGN X-ray spectra have pointed out the necessity of a realistic
spectral energy distribution (SED) of the source as seen by the absorbers to generate the ionized absorption models
(Laha et al. 2013). The different regions of the SED: UV, soft X-ray (0.3−2 keV) and hard X-ray (2−100 keV),
impact the WA models differently, leading to very different inferences from the data (for details, see Laha et al. 2013).
Therefore, we create a realistic broadband SED to create the warm absorber and UFO models as enumerated in several
previous studies (Laha et al. 2011, 2014b, 2021, 2013). Below we briefly discuss the steps to create the realistic SED
and refer the readers to previous work for details (e.g., Laha et al. 2014b).
CREATING A REALISTIC SED
A typical realistic SED as observed by the ionized clouds near the AGN can be a continuous spectrum from UV
(1 eV) to hard X-rays (100 keV). However, we note that the energy band 13.6 eV−100 eV is not observable because of
the Galactic absorption, and hence some approximations may have to be made (see for e.g., Laha et al. 2013). For 1ES
1927+654, we constructed the SED using the modeldiskbb+bb+expabs×pow, with parameter values provided in Table
10. Wherediskbbdescribes the accretion disk blackbody,bbodyis the blackbody model describing the soft X-ray
excess emission,expabsis a low-energy exponential roll-off, andpowis the power law emission from the corona. The
bbodyandpowparameters were adopted from the best fit to the XMM-Newton PN spectrum as in March 2024 (Laha
et al. 2025), while thediskbb(modeling the UV bump) component parameters were obtained from a previous work
usingSwift-UVOT (Ghosh et al. 2023). We note here that the UV flux did not vary appreciably (<30%) during this
3-year period (2022 - May, 2025), but the source flux in the soft X-ray band varies significantly with time, increasing
by a factor of∼10 in the last 3 years. Even though creating a SED for each observation is ideal, that level of accuracy
is not needed in this work because, as we will find out, we did not detect any ionized absorbers. Figure 9 illustrates the
SED and its various components. We assumed a lower energy cut-off for the power-law emission at 1 eV and higher
energy at 100 keV.
