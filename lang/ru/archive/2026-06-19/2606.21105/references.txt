disks by solving the frequency-dependent radiative transfer equation in a two-dimensional (r, z) configuration. The

C.BALMER BREAK TESTS
We investigate the sensitivity of the Balmer break strength to the viscosity parameter by comparing our fiducial
model (α= 0.1) with a lower viscosity case (α= 0.01). As shown in Figure 9, a smaller viscosity parameter results in
a more pronounced Balmer break by changing the disk vertical structure.
We also compare the nonuniform vertical density profile with a simplified uniform case to test the robustness of
the Balmer break. To do so, we enforce a constant density (ρ= const) in Eq. A1, re-calculate the vertical structure,
and subsequently derive the emergent SED. As shown in Figure 10, the Balmer break remains prominent under the
extreme assumption of a uniform vertical density distribution.
