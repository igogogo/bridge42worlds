APPENDIX A: QUANTIFICATION OF SHAPE
MEASUREMENT UNCERTAINTIES
Wepresentherethefullresultsofourteststoquantifytheuncertainty
in our shape measurements through the analysis of analytic mock
ellipsoids(seeSect.2).Wegenerate3Dellipsoidsofsemi-majoraxis
lengthof1000kpc,axisratios𝑝=0.7and𝑞=0.5,andorientations
defined by by Euler angles(𝛼,𝛽,𝛾)=(30 ◦,30 ◦,30 ◦). We perform
shape measurements on the particles within a shell at𝑟=250kpc
withathicknessofΔ𝑟=50kpc.Wevaryboththenumberofparticles
in each shell,𝑁shell, and the density profile slope,𝛽, over the ranges
103.7 < 𝑁shell <10 5.7 and1.0< 𝛽 <3.5. For each point in this
parameter space, 50 realisations are produced by changing only the
random seed, which changes the locations of individual particles.
The shape tensor (Eqn.1) is then evaluated to recover the axis ratios
andpositionangles.Thefractionalroot-mean-squareofthedeviation
ofthemeasuredvaluesfromtheinputpositionanglesandaxisratios
is displayed in Fig. A1 as our corresponding uncertainty estimates.
The top left and right panels correspond to the uncertainties on𝑝
and𝑞,respectively,andthebottomleftandrighttotheuncertainties
on position angles of the major and minor axes, respectively. Both
𝑁shell and𝛽affect the uncertainty to a similar extent: higher particle
numbers or steeper density slopes result in lower uncertainties. Our
radial bin selection applied to the Hydrangea simulations results
in typical ranges (16th-84thpercentile) of𝑁 shell ≈10 4.2 −10 4.8 and
𝛽≈2.6−3.4fortheICL,and𝑁 shell ≈10 3.5 −106.0 and𝛽≈1.1−2.2
fortheDM.Theseresultinuncertaintiesof≈0.5−1.2percentinICL
axisratiosand∼0.4−1.0degreesinpositionangles,and≈0.6−5.7
percentinDMaxisratiosand≈0.2−3.9degreesinpositionangles.
We additionally test the impact of an offset between the assumed
referencecentreoftheshapemeasurementmethodandthetruemass
distribution. The cluster centres in Hydrangea are defined as the
location of the particle with the lowest potential, which always cor-
responds with the centre of the BCG. Consequently, only the DM
component can be offset from the cluster centre. Using mock 3D
ellipsoids generated as above with the median Hydrangea DM𝑁shell
and𝛽values, we introduced spatial offsets ranging from 1 to 10kpc
andmeasureshapesin30logarithmicallyspacedradialbinsbetween
3 and 1000kpc. Offsets mainly affect the innermost radii, while for
realistic offsets (up to 5kpc; Harvey et al. 2019), beyond a radius of
∼40kpc the absolute differences between the input and measured
values remain below 0.022. Although this value is statistically sig-
nificantcomparedtotheuncertainties,itissubstantiallysmallerthan
the axis ratios of the BCG+ICL and DM, as well as the axis ratio
differences between them. This indicates that, in the ICL-dominated
region,theeffectofrealisticoffsetsonourconclusionsisnegligible.
Wealsoestimatetheuncertaintiesinour2Dshapemeasurements.
Toachievethis,wegeneratemockellipsesofaxisratio𝑝=0.75and
position angle𝜙=30degrees, adopting the same semi-major axis
length of 1000kpc. We vary𝑁shell and𝛽across the ranges10 3.6 <
𝑁shell <10 5.3 and0.5< 𝛽<2.8,respectively.Inaprocessanalogous
to the 3D case, we produce 50 ellipses for each combination and
measuretheshapesoftheparticleswithinanannulusat𝑟=250kpc
with widthΔ𝑟=50kpc. The fractional root-mean-square of the
deviation from the true input values is shown in Fig. A2 which used
to estimate the uncertainties on 2D shape measurements. The left
andrightpanelsshowtheuncertaintieson𝑝and𝜙,respectively.We
observe a similar degeneracy to that in the 3D case.
These uncertainties on individual cluster measurements are
smallerthanthecluster-to-clusterscatterandarethereforenotshown
in the figures of the main paper.
